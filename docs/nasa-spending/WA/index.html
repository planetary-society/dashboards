Washington state is a cornerstone for developing critical space exploration capabilities, particularly human lunar landing and advanced propulsion. Blue Origin receives substantial investment for Human Landing Systems development and demonstration, vital for returning astronauts to the Moon. Aerojet Rocketdyne advances high-power Solar Electric Propulsion systems for future deep space missions. University of Washington research supports astronaut health, lunar science, and space autonomy, complementing these core capabilities with essential scientific and technological depth.

WA NASA investments bolster diverse industries: major aerospace manufacturers, specialized small businesses, software firms, and technical societies statewide, advancing US space leadership.

Dramatic cuts exceeding 20% in Space Technology, Space Science, Mission Support, ISS, and Aeronautics represent a historic reduction in NASA's mission. This will severely impact Washington's aerospace and research sectors. Aerojet Rocketdyne's advanced propulsion work faces likely termination from the 44% Space Technology cut. University of Washington's planetary science and ISS research are jeopardized by 39% and 28% cuts respectively. Blue Origin's HLS work faces uncertainty. Supporting industries like Tecplot and Crane Electronics are vulnerable to widespread reductions and workforce cuts required by the 23% Mission Support reduction.

Definitions

Contracts: agreements to acquire specific products or services.
Grants: fixed-duration agreements to fund scientific or technological research and development.
Obligations: the funding contractually committed in a given fiscal year.
Total Awarded Value: sum of all potential award values for a given recipient.
Cut impacts: assumes an even application of overall spending cuts for illustrative purposes.
