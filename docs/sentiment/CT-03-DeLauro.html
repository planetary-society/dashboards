Overall Summary

Representative DeLauro, as Ranking Member of the House Committee on Appropriations, is a key figure in federal spending decisions. She has explicitly opposed "senseless attacks on America's scientific supremacy" and cuts to "scientific goals in space," even co-releasing an alternative funding measure that specifically required NASA to continue all Science Mission Directorate missions under formulation. This demonstrates a clear stance against the type of deep cuts proposed for NASA's science activities.

While her extensive background in health policy indicates a strong focus on other critical areas, her district benefits significantly from NASA Science Mission Directorate investments, including astrophysics research at Yale University and optical technology development for space-based telescopes at Zygo Corporation. These local ties, combined with her stated opposition to science funding impoundments and proposed cuts, suggest she may be guardedly receptive to advocacy highlighting how increased NASA space science funding supports both national scientific leadership and local economic and research interests.
