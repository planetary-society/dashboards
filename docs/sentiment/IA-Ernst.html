Overall Summary

Senator Ernst's consistent fiscal conservatism and past criticism of NASA's exploration costs present a significant challenge for advocating increased NASA space science funding. Her skepticism regarding climate science also suggests potential resistance to Earth science initiatives. Furthermore, her primary engagement with space policy has focused on national security, and she does not sit on committees with direct NASA oversight, indicating civil space science may not be a top priority.

However, there are clear opportunities for engagement. Iowa receives substantial NASA science investments, particularly at the University of Iowa for heliophysics and general science, providing a strong local economic and research argument. Her support for STEM education, small business innovation programs, and domestic research and development aligns with the broader benefits of NASA science, offering avenues to connect increased funding to her stated priorities.
