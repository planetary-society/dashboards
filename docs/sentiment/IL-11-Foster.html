About Bill Foster

  • Holds a Ph.D. in physics from Harvard University; B.S. in physics from UW-Madison.
  • Spent 22 years as a particle physicist at Fermilab National Laboratory.
  • Contributed to the discovery of the top quark and neutrino burst from SN 1987A.
  • Awarded multiple prestigious physics prizes, including the Robert R. Wilson Prize (2022).
  • One of few research physicists ever elected to the U.S. Congress.
