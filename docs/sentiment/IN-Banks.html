Overall Summary

Senator Banks's consistent emphasis on fiscal conservatism and national security priorities, coupled with his skepticism regarding climate science, suggests a challenging environment for advocating for increased NASA space science funding, particularly for Earth science. While his role as Ranking Member on the Senate Subcommittee on Science, Manufacturing, and Competitiveness provides a direct avenue for engagement, his overall spending philosophy may lead to scrutiny of all discretionary federal programs.

However, there are significant opportunities for outreach. Indiana universities, notably Purdue and Indiana University, receive substantial NASA science grants supporting planetary, Earth, and biological and physical sciences, and Redwire Space Technologies in Greenville has direct contracts supporting ISS life and microgravity research. Highlighting these local economic and scientific contributions, and connecting foundational science research to broader technological advancements and national competitiveness, could resonate despite his general fiscal stance and specific concerns about climate-related spending.
