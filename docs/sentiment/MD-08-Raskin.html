About Jamie Raskin

  • Graduated magna cum laude from Harvard College and Harvard Law School.

Opportunities

  • Strong Local Economic Interest in NASA Goddard: Representative Raskin has actively opposed proposed cuts to NASA Goddard missions and emphasized the importance of Maryland's space science sector to the state's economy and innovation, participating in bipartisan delegation efforts to support local space industry stakeholders. His district benefits from substantial NASA Science Mission Directorate contracts supporting Goddard Space Flight Center. (Hoyer, 2025-04-12; Raskin, 2023-10-19; USAspending, FY2022-FY2024)

  • Advocacy for Specific NASA Missions: Representative Raskin joined the Maryland delegation in pressing NASA for detailed explanations regarding the cancellation of the OSAM-1 mission, a space technology project developed at Goddard Space Flight Center, after Congress had provided funding for its continuation.   • Promotion of Science-Based Policy: As co-chair of the Congressional Freethought Caucus, Representative Raskin advocates for policy solutions based on reason and science, and recently co-sponsored a resolution celebrating the importance of the scientific method and free inquiry. (American Humanist Association, 2024-05-01; Wikipedia, 2018)

  • Opposition to Federal Funding Freezes: Representative Raskin has publicly opposed sweeping freezes on federal funding, arguing that such actions threaten essential programs and represent an unconstitutional challenge to congressional authority, indicating a general stance against cuts to federal appropriations. (Raskin, 2025-01-28)

Challenges

  • Competing Priorities Focus: Representative Raskin's public statements and official priorities pages highlight a wide array of legislative concerns, including civil rights, healthcare, and gun safety, without explicitly featuring NASA or space science as a primary focus.
  • Lack of Direct Legislative Lever: Representative Raskin does not hold a position on the House Science, Space, and Technology Committee or any appropriations subcommittees directly responsible for NASA funding, which limits his direct legislative influence on space policy.
