Overall Summary

Representative Bergman's strong fiscal conservative stance and consistent calls for reduced federal spending, alongside his prioritization of defense and border security, suggest a challenging environment for advocating increased NASA science funding. His opposition to climate-related legislation also indicates potential resistance to significant portions of NASA's Earth science portfolio. Furthermore, his record of voting against broader spending packages, combined with no direct role on space-related committees, may limit his receptiveness to new or expanded investments in this area.

Despite these challenges, there are compelling local connections that could foster receptiveness. Highlighting these tangible benefits and the district's role in advancing NASA's scientific missions could be key to garnering his support.
