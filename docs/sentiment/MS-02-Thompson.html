Overall Summary

His primary legislative focus as Ranking Member of Homeland Security, coupled with a relatively low level of direct NASA Science Mission Directorate funding in his district, suggests that advocating for increased space science investment may not be a top priority. Furthermore, his expressed concerns about federal budget cuts could make him cautious about supporting new spending initiatives, especially given the proposed 47% cut to NASA science in FY2026.

However, there is a guarded optimism for engagement.
