Overall Summary

Representative Sessions's consistent record of fiscal conservatism and opposition to broad spending bills presents a significant hurdle for advocating for increased NASA science funding, particularly in light of proposed budget cuts. His focus on reducing federal spending suggests he will scrutinize any proposals for additional appropriations.

However, there are clear avenues for engagement. His district benefits directly from NASA science through substantial contracts with Dell Federal Systems for critical data processing infrastructure and significant research grants to Baylor University and Texas A&M for lunar, planetary, and biological sciences. Highlighting these local economic and research benefits, coupled with his historical support for NASA university programs and active participation in the appropriations process, may offer a pathway to garner his support for maintaining or strategically investing in NASA's science mission.
