Overall Summary

Senator Johnson's strong fiscal conservatism and consistent calls for reduced federal spending, alongside his skepticism regarding climate science, present significant challenges for advocating increased NASA science investments, especially given the proposed FY26 cuts and the substantial Earth science component of NASA's portfolio. His lack of direct committee assignments related to space policy also suggests it may not be a primary legislative focus for him.

However, there are clear opportunities to engage him by emphasizing the nearly $50 million in NASA Science Mission Directorate contracts and grants that have directly benefited Wisconsin institutions, primarily the University of Wisconsin System, over the last three fiscal years. Framing these investments as crucial for advanced manufacturing, high-tech job creation, and maintaining Wisconsin's scientific leadership could resonate with his stated priority for science and business background.
