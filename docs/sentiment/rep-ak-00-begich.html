Meeting Strategy

Lead with: NASA Science as a Mission-Critical Energy and Tech Testbed

Lead by applauding his recent leadership on the DOE-NASA Interagency Research Coordination Act. Frame NASA’s Science Mission Directorate not as an abstract expense, but as a vital partner in the energy innovation and Arctic technological research he champions for Alaska.

Bridge issues:

  • Energy Production and Independence: "Your work bridging NASA and the Department of Energy is vital because the same science that enables long-duration space missions also drives the energy innovations we need here in Alaska's unique testbed environments."
  • Aviation Safety and Infrastructure: "NASA’s Earth science and heliophysics research at the University of Alaska Fairbanks provides the real-time data that pilots in our state rely on to navigate safely through challenging Arctic weather."

Watch out for:

  • Avoid assuming a general interest in space equates to a blank check; he is a staunch fiscal conservative who requires a 'mission-critical' case for all federal spending.
  • Be prepared for a lack of familiarity with planetary science or astrophysics, as his public record currently focuses almost exclusively on energy, propulsion, and Earth observations.

Expect this reaction: Expect a business-like and pragmatic response. While his seat on the Science Committee and the local footprint of the University of Alaska Fairbanks make him a natural partner, he will likely be cautious about committing to funding increases without a clear economic or strategic justification.

About Representative Nicholas J. Begich (R-AK At-Large)

  • Serves as the U.S. Representative for Alaska's at-large district, having been sworn into office on January 3, 2025.
  • Founded FarShore Partners, an international software development company, and served as its executive chairman.
  • Holds a Bachelor of Business Administration from Baylor University and a Master of Business Administration from Indiana University Bloomington.
  • Served as a board member of the Alaska Policy Forum, a conservative think tank, and as co-chair of the Alaska Republican Party's Finance Committee.
  • Authored legislation signed into law in 2025 that mandated oil and gas lease sales in the Arctic National Wildlife Refuge and the National Petroleum Reserve-Alaska.
