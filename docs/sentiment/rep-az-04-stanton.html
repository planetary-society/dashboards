Lead with: NASA Science as Arizona’s Economic Engine

Lead with the massive $106 million NASA science investment in the district, specifically highlighting Arizona State University’s leadership in high-profile planetary missions like Psyche and the Lunar Reconnaissance Orbiter. You should frame robust funding for the Science Mission Directorate as the essential fuel for the high-tech, innovation-based economy that the Representative champions through his support of the CHIPS and Science Act.

Bridge issues:

  • Semiconductor Industry and Advanced Manufacturing: "Representative, your leadership on the CHIPS and Science Act has made our district a hub for advanced manufacturing; NASA science missions are the primary customers for these technologies, proving that Arizona-made innovation can succeed in the harshest environments in the solar system."
  • Climate Resilience and Infrastructure: "As Co-Chair of the Extreme Heat Caucus, you understand that Arizona is on the front lines of climate change; NASA’s Earth Science missions provide the essential data used to monitor the droughts and heatwaves that threaten our state’s infrastructure and economy."
  • STEM Workforce Development: "The $106 million in NASA science obligations in our district is a critical investment in our workforce, helping institutions like ASU and local community colleges train the next generation of scientists and engineers who will stay and work here in Arizona."

About Representative Greg Stanton (D-AZ-04)

  • Served as the Mayor of Phoenix from 2012 to 2018 and as a member of the Phoenix City Council from 2000 to 2009.
  • Held the position of Deputy Attorney General of Arizona from 2009 to 2011, where he negotiated a settlement to protect the future of Luke Air Force Base.
  • Recognized by Governing magazine as a Public Official of the Year in 2017 for efforts to expand light rail and reduce greenhouse gas emissions.
  • Earned a Juris Doctor from the University of Michigan Law School and worked as an education attorney and adjunct law professor.
  • Elected to the U.S. House of Representatives in 2018 and has served as the representative for Arizona's 4th and 9th districts.
