Bottom Line

Likely Supportive (high confidence): Representative Stanton is a strong ally for NASA science, primarily because Arizona State University (ASU) in his district leads major missions like Psyche and manages critical instruments for lunar exploration. He recently supported a $9 billion funding target for the Science Mission Directorate, explicitly linking space science to American global competitiveness and national security. Meeting Strategy

Lead with: Arizona’s Engine for the Last Frontier

Lead by thanking Representative Stanton for his recent support of the $9 billion funding target for the NASA Science Mission Directorate. Frame NASA science funding as the essential fuel for the 'STEM pipeline' and local high-tech startups that he views as critical to Arizona’s economic future and national security.

Bridge issues:

  • National Security & Competition with China: "As you work on the CCP Select Committee, your support for NASA science ensures the U.S. maintains its global technological edge and doesn't cede leadership in space to international competitors."
  • STEM Workforce & 'Stapling Green Cards': "By funding NASA missions like Psyche and OSIRIS-REx at ASU, we provide the high-stakes engineering roles that allow us to keep the world-class talent trained in our universities right here in Arizona."

Watch out for:

  • Avoid letting the meeting pivot entirely toward transit or light rail infrastructure; while these are his primary committee focuses, you must keep the focus on the Science Mission Directorate budget.
  • Be cautious about assuming deep expertise in all NASA divisions; his record is heavily focused on planetary science and lunar exploration, so provide clear context when discussing Earth science or astrophysics.

Expect this reaction: The Representative and his staff are likely to be very receptive and engaged, particularly if you connect the conversation to the specific mission achievements and job creation at Arizona State University.

Key Opportunities

  • Direct NASA Record: Leverage his signature on the FY2027 letter requesting $9 billion for the Science Mission Directorate as a clear starting point for support.1 2
  • Local Institution: Emphasize that ASU-led missions like Psyche and the Lunar Reconnaissance Orbiter are direct beneficiaries of robust planetary science funding.4 7
  • Adjacent Interest: Discuss the 'STEM pipeline' by connecting his legislation for graduate fellowships to the need for a skilled workforce to execute NASA's complex science missions.10 1
  • Committee Position: Highlight space science as a strategic asset for the U.S. in its competition with China, a top priority for his work on the CCP Select Committee.6 1
  • District Fiscal Impact: Cite his recent visit to space solar startup Solestial as evidence of his interest in how space technology fuels local business innovation.5

Key Challenges

  • Evidence Gap: His public record is heavily concentrated on planetary science and lunar exploration; more evidence is needed to gauge his interest in Earth Science, Astrophysics, or Heliophysics.13 4
  • Competing Priority: Significant focus on transportation and infrastructure, including light rail and drought resilience, could divert his attention during broader budget negotiations.6

Strategic Considerations

  • Consider exploring the connection between his successful $1 million request for bioscience programs and NASA’s own research in biological and physical sciences.
  • You might find it useful to mention his recent visit to Solestial, using it as a hook to discuss how NASA science mission requirements often drive the innovation that creates such local startups.
  • Think about whether you can invite him to move from a letter-signer to a vocal champion, given his background in municipal leadership and interest in Arizona's role in the 'last frontier.'

About Representative Greg Stanton (D-AZ-04)

  • Served as the Mayor of Phoenix from 2012 to 2018 and as a member of the Phoenix City Council from 2000 to 2009.
  • Held the position of Deputy Attorney General of Arizona from 2009 to 2011, where he negotiated a settlement to protect the future of Luke Air Force Base.
  • Recognized by Governing magazine as a Public Official of the Year in 2017 for efforts to expand light rail and reduce greenhouse gas emissions.
  • Earned a Juris Doctor from the University of Michigan Law School and worked as an education attorney and adjunct law professor.
  • Elected to the U.S. House of Representatives in 2018 and has served as the representative for Arizona's 4th and 9th districts.
