Bottom Line

Likely Supportive (high confidence): Representative Grijalva is a strong ally for NASA science, having signed multiple budget letters requesting $9 billion for the Science Mission Directorate. Her district is a national hub for space exploration, with the University of Arizona leading high-profile efforts like the OSIRIS-REx asteroid mission and critical components for the James Webb Space Telescope. Meeting Strategy

Lead with: The University of Arizona as a Global Leader

Lead by thanking Representative Grijalva for her strong record of supporting the Science Mission Directorate, specifically her signatures on the FY25 and FY27 budget letters. Frame NASA science funding as the essential fuel for the University of Arizona's world-class leadership in missions like the James Webb Space Telescope and the OSIRIS-REx asteroid sample return.

Bridge issues:

  • Public Education and STEM Access: "NASA missions lead the world in scientific discovery, but for AZ-07, they are also the 'gold standard' for inspiring a new generation of Latino and Latina students to pursue STEM careers through programs at the University of Arizona."
  • Environmental Stewardship: "As you champion water resiliency and climate protection on the Natural Resources Committee, please keep in mind that NASA's Earth Science missions provide the critical data Arizona needs to manage our water future and protect our lands."
  • Local Economic Impact: "With nearly $200 million in annual NASA science obligations coming to our district, these missions aren't just about space—they represent high-skilled jobs and local economic stability for Tucson."

Watch out for:

  • Avoid focusing strictly on the 'wonder of exploration'; always tie the science back to its practical impact on STEM education and the local Tucson economy to match her legislative priorities.
  • Do not interpret her lack of a signature on the FY26 budget letter as a shift in stance; her overall record indicates she remains one of the Science Mission Directorate's most consistent supporters.

Expect this reaction: Expect a very positive and receptive meeting. Given her district's massive stake in NASA science and her direct history of signing SMD support letters, she is likely to remain a strong ally against proposed budget cuts.

About Representative Adelita S. Grijalva (D-AZ-07)

  • Elected to the U.S. House of Representatives in a 2025 special election to succeed her father, Representative Raúl Grijalva, and is the first Latina to represent Arizona in Congress.
  • Served on the Pima County Board of Supervisors from 2021 to 2025, where she focused on climate, water resiliency, and recovering from the COVID-19 pandemic.
  • Held a 20-year tenure on the Tucson Unified School District Governing Board from 2002 to 2022, including service as board chair.
  • Spent 25 years as the director of the Pima County Teen Court, a non-profit juvenile diversion program.
  • Holds a Bachelor of Arts degree in political science from the University of Arizona.
