About Representative Kevin Kiley (I-CA-03)

  • Served in the California State Assembly from 2016 to 2022 before being elected to the U.S. House of Representatives in 2022.
  • Earned an undergraduate degree from Harvard University and a law degree from Yale Law School, where he served as an editor of the Yale Law Journal.
  • Worked as an attorney at Irell & Manella, where he assisted in an intellectual property theft case for T-Mobile against the Chinese technology company Huawei.
  • Gained professional experience in the financial and educational sectors as a clerk at the Federal Reserve Bank of New York and as a teacher for Teach for America.
  • Authored federal legislation to stop funding for the California High-Speed Rail project and has expressed public support for charter schools and school choice.
