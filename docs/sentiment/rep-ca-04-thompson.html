Bottom Line

Likely Supportive (moderate confidence): Representative Thompson is a pragmatist who views NASA science through the lens of local research and environmental conservation. His district is a major hub for NASA Earth science and astrophysics funding, particularly through the Bay Area Environmental Research Institute and local universities. Advocates should emphasize how Science Mission Directorate funding sustains high-tech jobs in CA-04 and provides the data necessary for his priority conservation efforts. While generally supportive, his commitment to fiscal responsibility means he will be most receptive to arguments that frame space science as an efficient, high-impact investment in American innovation.

Meeting Strategy

Lead with: Lead with the District's Scientific and Economic Engine

Frame NASA science as a vital economic and environmental driver for CA-04 by highlighting the significant local research footprint, including over $18 million in NASA obligations. You should emphasize that NASA-funded work at the Bay Area Environmental Research Institute and local universities is not just 'space science,' but a direct investment in the district's workforce and conservation efforts.

Bridge issues:

  • Environmental Conservation and Land Preservation: "NASA Earth science missions provide the precise climate monitoring and atmospheric data necessary for the land and wildlife conservation efforts you lead as Co-Chair of the Land Conservation Caucus."
  • STEM Education and Workforce Readiness: "NASA’s local investments, such as the five-million-dollar grant to Sonoma State, are actively training the next generation of California’s high-tech workforce from underserved communities, directly supporting your STEM Education Caucus goals."

Watch out for:

  • Be prepared for pushback on specific budget increases; the Representative is a staunch advocate for 'PAYGO' fiscal discipline and may be hesitant to support new spending without clear offsets.
  • Avoid overstating his record on planetary science or heliophysics, as his documented interest and district funding are heavily concentrated in Earth science and astrophysics.

Expect this reaction: Staff are likely to be very receptive to discussions about local grants and district jobs, but they may remain non-committal on the specific $9 billion Science Mission Directorate funding target due to his focus on deficit reduction.

  • Local Institution: Highlight the significant local impact of the $137.8M NASA grant to the Bay Area Environmental Research Institute for environmental monitoring frameworks.3 4

  • Direct NASA Record: Point to his vote for the CHIPS and Science Act as a foundation for his support of NASA's space technology and exploration missions.1

Key Challenges

  • Ideological Barrier: His commitment to 'PAYGO' rules and reversing deficit spending may make him hesitant to support funding increases without clear economic offsets.7
  • Evidence Gap: There is an evidence gap regarding his specific support for planetary science, heliophysics, and biological and physical sciences, as his record focuses heavily on Earth science and astrophysics.9
  • Political Constraint: He has consistently declined to sign the annual multi-member letters supporting a $9 billion Science Mission Directorate budget topline.5

Strategic Considerations

  • Consider mentioning Remote Sensing Systems’ work on microwave radiances as a bridge to how NASA data helps analyze agricultural and environmental changes that affect local industries like his own vineyards.
  • You might find it useful to reference his 2022 vote for the CHIPS and Science Act as evidence of his established support for strengthening American research institutions through NASA funding.
  • It could be worth mentioning the 'SPIDER' telescope project at UC Davis to illustrate how NASA science drives the 'cutting-edge studies' and domestic manufacturing he advocated for in his support of the CHIPS Act.

About Representative Mike Thompson (D-CA-04)

  • Served in the United States Army's 173rd Airborne Brigade during the Vietnam War.
  • Educated at California State University, Chico, and later taught Public Administration and State Government at San Francisco State University and California State University, Chico.
  • Professional background includes experience as a vineyard owner and maintenance supervisor.
  • Served in the California State Senate from 1990 until his election to the U.S. House of Representatives in 1998.
  • Has engaged in international fact-finding missions, including a 2002 trip to Iraq to investigate claims regarding weapons of mass destruction.
