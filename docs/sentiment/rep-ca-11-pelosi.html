About Representative Nancy Pelosi (D-CA-11)

  • First elected to the House of Representatives in a 1987 special election, she has served 20 terms representing California's San Francisco area.
  • Served as the 52nd Speaker of the House for two non-consecutive tenures (2007–2011 and 2019–2023), becoming the first woman to hold the office.
  • Facilitated the passage of major science and technology legislation, including the CHIPS and Science Act and the Infrastructure Investment and Jobs Act.
  • Held leadership roles on the House Intelligence Committee as ranking member and served on the House Appropriations Committee.
  • Earned a Bachelor of Arts in political science from Trinity College in 1962 and interned for Maryland Senator Daniel Brewster in the 1960s.
