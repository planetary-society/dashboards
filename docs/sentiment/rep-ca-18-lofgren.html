Lead with: Empower the Oversight Champion

Lead by recognizing her powerful role as Ranking Member of the Science Committee and thank her for protecting Congressional authority against administrative budget cuts. You should frame your request for robust Science Mission Directorate funding as essential to the 'balanced portfolio' she championed in the 2026 NASA Reauthorization Act.

Bridge issues:

Watch out for:

  • Be prepared for her to prioritize the Artemis program's timeline and competition with China; ensure you frame planetary science as a necessary partner in maintaining overall U.S. space leadership.

Key Opportunities

  • Committee Position: As Ranking Member of the House Science Committee, she has direct legislative authority over NASA's priorities and has already co-led a reauthorization bill protecting planetary science.1 4
  • District Fiscal Impact: The district receives over $177 million annually in NASA science obligations, with massive grants to local research foundations supporting NASA Ames missions.10 6
  • Direct NASA Record: She has demonstrated a willingness to directly confront NASA leadership regarding 'illegal' impoundment of funds and facility closures that threaten astrophysics missions.2 3
  • Direct NASA Record: She explicitly supports Mars Sample Return and maintaining a balanced planetary science portfolio as a matter of national scientific leadership.1 4

Key Challenges

  • Evidence Gap: Despite her leadership on authorization bills, she has not signed recent 'Dear Colleague' letters specifically requesting $9 billion for the Science Mission Directorate.11
  • Competing Priority: Her strong vocal support for Artemis and the competition with China could create fiscal pressure to prioritize human exploration over science accounts if overall budgets are tight.5
  • Evidence Gap: While her record is exceptionally strong on Planetary Science, Astrophysics, and Earth Science, there is less evidence of her direct engagement with Heliophysics or Biological and Physical Sciences.1 4 8

Strategic Considerations

  • Consider mentioning her past work on open access to research; NASA’s archived exoplanet data from missions like Kepler—managed right here in the district—is a perfect example of taxpayer-funded science benefiting the global community.
  • You might find it useful to lean into her recent efforts to block facility closures at Goddard, using it as a bridge to discuss why local mission-related capabilities at NASA Ames must also be protected.
  • Think about whether to emphasize that 'balanced' science means protecting not just the large missions like MSR, but also the smaller, community-guided astrophysics and planetary projects that sustain the local academic pipeline.

About Representative Zoe Lofgren (D-CA-18)

  • Earned a B.A. in political science from Stanford University and a Juris Doctor from Santa Clara University School of Law.
  • Served as a staffer for Congressman Don Edwards and worked on the House Judiciary Committee during the 1973 impeachment proceedings against President Richard Nixon.
  • Represents a district covering much of Silicon Valley and has been active in tech-related policy areas including net neutrality, digital surveillance, and antitrust regulation.
  • Sponsored the Fair Access to Science and Technology Research Act in 2013 to expedite open access to taxpayer-funded research.
  • Co-sponsored the Nuclear Energy Innovation Capabilities Act in 2018 and the Green New Deal resolution in 2019.
