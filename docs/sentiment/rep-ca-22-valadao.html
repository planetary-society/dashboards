Representative David G. Valadao (R-CA-22)

Representative | R | CA-22

NASA Budget Support

  • FY2026 CJS Appropriations vote: Yea on H.R. 6938

Bottom Line

Likely Supportive (moderate confidence): Representative Valadao is a strategic ally on the House Appropriations Committee who views federal science investment as a driver of regional economic competitiveness and workforce development. While he has not signed recent Science Mission Directorate (SMD) funding letters, his recent efforts to protect NASA-Caltech contracts from administrative cuts demonstrate a willingness to defend space science assets. Advocates should frame NASA science as a vital component of the California research economy and a key motivator for STEM education in the Central Valley. Be prepared to bridge the gap between his general support for NASA's budget and the specific funding needs of planetary and Earth science programs.

Meeting Strategy

Lead with: Defending California’s Research Economy

Lead with Representative Valadao’s recent leadership in challenging administrative cuts to NASA-Caltech contracts and university research. Frame robust funding for the Science Mission Directorate as the best way to prevent these disruptions and protect California’s role as a global leader in space exploration.

Bridge issues:

  • Agriculture and Water Rights: "NASA’s Earth science missions provide the precise data our farmers in the Central Valley need to manage water resources and monitor drought conditions effectively."
  • Education and STEM Workforce: "By supporting the Science Mission Directorate, you are ensuring that the educational resources on your own website and local STEM initiatives continue to inspire students in the 22nd District to pursue high-tech careers."

Watch out for:

  • Avoid framing NASA science funding as a broad federal mandate; instead, focus on its role in regional economic competitiveness to align with his skepticism of large-scale science legislation like the CHIPS Act.
  • Do not assume he is already committed to specific science divisions like Planetary or Heliophysics; while he supports the broad NASA budget, he has not yet signed on to specific Science Mission Directorate funding letters.

Expect this reaction: The office will likely be very receptive to protecting existing California research assets and jobs, but they may be cautious about committing to specific funding increases without a clear connection to fiscal responsibility or local impact.

Key Opportunities

  • Committee Position: As a member of the House Appropriations Committee, Valadao holds significant influence over NASA's annual funding levels.3
  • Direct NASA Record: Valadao has demonstrated a willingness to challenge federal spending cuts that specifically target NASA-Caltech contracts and university research.1
  • Adjacent Interest: His recognition as a 'Champion of Science' by The Science Coalition highlights his support for basic scientific research as an economic driver.2

Key Challenges

  • Evidence Gap: There is an evidence gap regarding his specific positions on NASA's planetary science, heliophysics, astrophysics, and Earth science divisions.4
  • Political Constraint: Valadao voted against the CHIPS and Science Act in 2022, suggesting skepticism toward certain large-scale federal science and technology mandates.10
  • Evidence Gap: He has not signed recent 'Dear Colleague' letters specifically advocating for the Science Mission Directorate's $9 billion funding topline.4

Strategic Considerations

  • Consider exploring how his interest in quantum modeling and simulation might intersect with NASA’s advanced computing needs for complex space missions.
  • You might find it useful to mention his 'Champion of Science' award from The Science Coalition as a reason why his leadership on the Appropriations Committee is so vital for the research community.
  • Think about whether to highlight the University of California’s support for his work, as their nomination for his science award shows he values the input of California’s major research institutions.

About Representative David G. Valadao (R-CA-22)

  • Longtime dairy farmer and partner in Valadao Dairy who served as chairman of the Regional Leadership Council for Land O' Lakes.
  • Served as a member of the California Milk Advisory Board and the Western States Dairy Trade Association.
  • Represented the 30th district in the California State Assembly from 2010 to 2012 prior to his election to Congress.
  • Attended the College of the Sequoias as a part-time student from 1996 to 1998.
  • Introduced the Blue Water Navy Vietnam Veterans Act to grant presumptive Agent Orange exposure status to U.S. service members.
