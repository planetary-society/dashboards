Bottom Line

Champion (high confidence): Representative Chu is one of the most vital advocates for NASA science in Congress, primarily driven by the presence of the Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL) and Caltech in her district. As Democratic Co-Chair of the Planetary Science Caucus, she has been a vocal opponent of budget cuts that threaten flagship missions like Mars Sample Return and has warned that funding instability leads directly to local job losses. Your best path is to thank her for her leadership while providing her with specific talking points on how consistent Science Mission Directorate funding supports the high-skilled workforce and the national economy. Be prepared to discuss the long-term impact of mission architecture shifts on JPL's stability, as this is a primary concern for her office.

Meeting Strategy

Lead with: The JPL Champion

Lead with a heartfelt thank you for her leadership as Co-Chair of the Planetary Science Caucus and her unwavering defense of the Jet Propulsion Laboratory (JPL) workforce. You should frame the Science Mission Directorate budget not just as a scientific necessity, but as an essential safeguard for the thousands of high-skilled jobs in her district.

Bridge issues:

  • STEM Education and Workforce: "As a former educator, you know that NASA's missions are the ultimate classroom, and robust funding for the Science Mission Directorate ensures that students in the San Gabriel Valley have world-class STEM career paths to walk into."
  • Economic Impact of Mars Exploration: "Protecting the Mars Sample Return mission is about more than science—it's about maintaining the $6.4 billion in economic activity and the specialized expertise at JPL that makes our district a global leader in space."
  • Small Business and Innovation: "The space industry is a massive part of our economy, and investments in missions like Mars Sample Return lead to everyday technologies that keep American small businesses competitive."

Watch out for:

  • Do not mistake her vote for broad funding packages as support for every cut within them; she has voiced strong disappointment when those bills target the Mars Sample Return mission.
  • Avoid assuming she is deeply briefed on the Biological and Physical Sciences division, as her record is significantly more focused on planetary science and astrophysics.

Expect this reaction: Expect an exceptionally warm and knowledgeable reception. The Representative is a top-tier champion who will likely agree with your points but may express frustration regarding the political difficulty of reversing administration-proposed cuts.

Key Opportunities

  • Bipartisan Coalition: Leverage her leadership as Co-Chair of the Planetary Science Caucus to build broader bipartisan support for NASA Science Mission Directorate funding.2
  • District Fiscal Impact: Highlight the $6.4 billion in NASA science obligations flowing into her district, particularly through Caltech and JPL, to underscore the economic necessity of robust funding.9
  • Committee Position: Engage her office via her role on the House Committee on the Budget to emphasize space science as a strategic investment during the appropriations process.4
  • Direct NASA Record: Reinforce her specific advocacy for the Mars Sample Return mission, which she considers the nation's highest planetary science priority.1 5
  • Adjacent Interest: Connect NASA science projects like the Europa Clipper and NEO Surveyor to her interests in technological innovation and planetary defense.9 2

Key Challenges

  • Political Constraint: The member faces significant political pressure to support broad funding packages that may include specific science cuts she opposes, as seen in her vote for the FY26 spending bill despite its plan to cancel Mars Sample Return.10
  • Competing Priority: Recent budget instability has already caused layoffs of hundreds of Mars experts at JPL in her district, creating a sense of urgency and potential skepticism regarding NASA's long-term workforce stability.1 6
  • Evidence Gap: While she is a champion for planetary science and astrophysics, her record is thinner regarding specific support for the Biological and Physical Sciences (BPS) division.3 11

Strategic Considerations

  • You might find it useful to mention specific flagship projects like Europa Clipper or NEO Surveyor, which are managed by Caltech and directly demonstrate the local impact of her advocacy.
  • Consider connecting NASA Earth Science data to her priority for climate action, noting how local institutions provide the data needed to track climate change and support clean energy.

About Representative Judy Chu (D-CA-28)

  • Earned a B.A. degree in mathematics from the University of California, Los Angeles, and a Ph.D. in psychology from the California School of Professional Psychology.
  • Served as a psychology professor in the Los Angeles Community College District for 20 years, including 13 years at East Los Angeles College.
  • Elected to the U.S. House of Representatives in 2009, becoming the first Chinese American woman to serve in Congress.
  • Extensive experience in state and local governance, including service on a local school board, the Monterey Park City Council (three terms as mayor), and the California State Assembly.
  • Previously served on the California Board of Equalization, representing most of Los Angeles County.
