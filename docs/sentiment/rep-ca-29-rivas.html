Bottom Line

Likely Supportive (high confidence): Representative Rivas is a natural ally for NASA science with a technical background and a strategic seat on the House Science Committee. She has consistently supported robust funding for the Science Mission Directorate and recently acted to protect the federal science workforce from proposed staff reductions. Advocates should focus on the local economic benefits of missions like the Roman Space Telescope, which includes significant manufacturing work in her district, and frame space science as a primary driver of the STEM education pathways she champions. Expect an informed, sophisticated conversation regarding both the workforce and the technical merits of NASA missions.

Meeting Strategy

Lead with: The Science Workforce and STEM Champion

Lead by framing NASA science as a critical engine for the STEM workforce and technical education pathways the Congresswoman has championed her entire career. You should emphasize her powerful role on the Science Committee and thank her for her recent leadership in protecting the federal science workforce from proposed reductions.

Bridge issues:

  • STEM Education and Workforce Development: "As a leader of the STEM Education Caucus, you know that NASA's ambitious science missions provide the 'north star' that inspires students in our district to pursue engineering and research careers."
  • Local Economic Impact (Roman Space Telescope): "In our district, Spectrolab’s $6 million contract for the Roman Space Telescope’s solar panels is a perfect example of how NASA science funding directly supports high-tech manufacturing jobs right here in CA-29."

Watch out for:

  • Avoid focusing your pitch on the Artemis program or human spaceflight, as the Congresswoman's public record and committee work are almost exclusively centered on science, workforce, and climate issues.
  • Do not assume her support for all specific planetary missions; while she supports the overall science budget, her recent focus has been specifically on missions facing disruption at Goddard, like Roman and Dragonfly.

Expect this reaction: Expect a highly informed and supportive reaction; the Congresswoman's background as a former NASA-funded researcher and her current Science Committee leadership make her one of the most sophisticated advocates for science in the House.

Key Opportunities

  • District Fiscal Impact: Highlight the local manufacturing of Roman Space Telescope solar array panels by Spectrolab, a major $6M contract in the district.8
  • Committee Position: Leverage her influential position on the Science Committee's subcommittees for Research and Technology and Investigations and Oversight.4
  • Adjacent Interest: Connect planetary missions to her personal history as a former NASA-funded researcher who co-authored scientific reports.5
  • Direct NASA Record: Utilize her clear support for the Science Mission Directorate topline, as evidenced by her signing the FY26 and FY27 budget support letters.1
  • Bipartisan Coalition: Reference her recent leadership in co-sponsoring legislation to protect NASA's workforce from reductions in force (RIFs).2 3

Key Challenges

  • Competing Priority: Economic opportunity and safety net protections are high-tier campaign priorities that may compete for attention with space funding requests.11
  • Evidence Gap: There is a lack of direct evidence regarding her stances on deep space exploration, the Artemis program, or commercial human spaceflight.12
  • Evidence Gap: While supportive of science generally, her public record is thin on specific commitments for heliophysics and biological and physical sciences.12

Strategic Considerations

  • You might find it useful to mention her personal history as a researcher on a NASA-funded study, as this gives her a unique, first-hand perspective on the value of the agency’s educational grants.
  • Consider exploring the connection between NASA Earth Science and her focus on climate change mitigation, especially regarding the data needed for the environmental justice policies she pioneered in the California Assembly.
  • Think about highlighting how her role on the Investigations and Oversight Subcommittee is vital for ensuring that NASA's science missions stay on track despite current political and budgetary headwinds.

About Representative Luz M. Rivas (D-CA-29)

  • Earned a Bachelor of Science degree in electrical engineering from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology.
  • Worked in the private sector for Motorola.
  • Founded DIY Girls, a non-profit organization that encourages school-age girls to pursue careers in science, technology, engineering, and mathematics.
  • Served as a member of the California State Assembly from 2018 to 2024 and on the Los Angeles City Public Works Commission.
  • Holds a Master of Education degree from the Harvard Graduate School of Education.
