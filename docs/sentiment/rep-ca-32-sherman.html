Bottom Line

Likely Supportive (high confidence): Representative Sherman is a reliable ally of NASA science, particularly when missions support the Southern California aerospace and research economy. As a member of the Planetary Science Caucus and a recent signer of the $9 billion funding request for the Science Mission Directorate, he is well-aligned with The Planetary Society's core goals. Advocates should emphasize how planetary missions like Mars Sample Return and the Roman Space Telescope drive high-tech jobs at UCLA and local contractors like Aerojet Rocketdyne. However, be mindful that his relationship with NASA is also shaped by his persistent pressure on the agency to complete the environmental cleanup of the Santa Susana Field Lab.

Meeting Strategy

Lead with: The Champion of Planetary Science and Local Research Jobs

Lead with Representative Sherman's active role in the Planetary Science Caucus and his specific defense of missions like Mars Sample Return. You should emphasize that funding the Science Mission Directorate at $9 billion is essential to protecting the high-tech research jobs at UCLA and among local contractors that he has already fought to preserve.

Bridge issues:

  • Economy and Local Labor: "With over $25 million in NASA contracts at UCLA for missions like Dawn, supporting the full $9 billion for NASA science ensures that the 32nd District remains a national leader in planetary exploration and high-tech employment."
  • STEM Workforce Development: "Your commitment to the Congressional App Challenge shows how much you value the next generation of innovators; stable NASA science funding provides the career path those students need right here in the San Fernando Valley."

Watch out for:

  • Be prepared for the Representative to bring up the Santa Susana Field Lab cleanup; if he does, acknowledge the importance of NASA being a good neighbor but quickly pivot back to how robust science funding supports the agency's broader mission goals.
  • Avoid spending too much time on the SLS or Artemis engines produced in the district; while he strongly values these manufacturing jobs, your priority is to secure his continued support for the Science Mission Directorate specifically.

Expect this reaction: Representative Sherman is likely to be highly supportive and engaged, given his membership in the Planetary Science Caucus and his recent sign-on to the $9 billion funding request. As a CPA, he may focus on the specific fiscal details of how the budget is distributed.

Key Opportunities

  • Direct NASA Record: Lean on his signature of the FY27 'Dear Colleague' letter supporting a $9 billion topline for NASA's Science Mission Directorate.1
  • Bipartisan Coalition: Reference his public opposition to budget cuts for Mars Sample Return, where he specifically defended the planetary science community and California aerospace jobs.2
  • Local Institution: Highlight the $25 million in NASA contracts at UCLA for the Dawn mission and other planetary grants as evidence of the district's leading role in solar system exploration.6
  • Committee Position: Use his membership in the Planetary Science Caucus to frame your advocacy as supporting his existing caucus commitments.6

Key Challenges

  • Political Constraint: NASA's delays in cleaning up the Santa Susana Field Lab remain a point of significant friction and could distract from discussions on science mission funding.4
  • Evidence Gap: While strong on planetary science and Artemis-related manufacturing, his record is thinner regarding funding for the Heliophysics and Biological and Physical Sciences divisions.9

Strategic Considerations

  • Consider appealing to his background as a CPA by framing planetary missions as long-term investments that provide a high return on investment for American technical leadership and the local economy.
  • You might find it useful to mention the HaloQuest astrobiology project at The University Corporation as another example of how district-based research is pushing the boundaries of science.
  • It could be worth mentioning that robust Earth Science funding aligns with his environmental priorities, though remain mindful that his primary NASA friction point is the physical cleanup of the Santa Susana site.

About Representative Brad Sherman (D-CA-32)

  • Certified Public Accountant who worked at a Big Four accounting firm and served as an instructor at Harvard Law School's International Tax Program.
  • Served on the California State Board of Equalization from 1991 to 1997, including four years as the board's chair.
  • Introduced the U.S.-Israel Energy Cooperation Act to provide grants for joint research and development of energy-efficient and renewable energy technologies.
  • Authored the Sherman Amendment to the Budget Resolution in 1997, which provided $700 million for the acquisition of environmentally important lands.
  • Advocated for federal relief funding and the delay of tariffs on construction materials to assist rebuilding efforts following the 2025 Palisades Fire.
