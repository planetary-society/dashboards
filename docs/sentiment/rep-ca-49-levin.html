Bottom Line

Likely Supportive (high confidence): Representative Levin is a reliable ally for NASA science who views space exploration as a matter of national leadership and scientific integrity. As a member of the Appropriations Committee, he has explicitly pushed back against budget cuts that threaten the NASA workforce and has signed onto efforts to fund the Science Mission Directorate at robust levels. Meeting Strategy

Lead with: The Appropriations Champion of Scientific Integrity

Lead with Representative Levin's own defense of the scientific workforce against "decades-low" budget cuts and his public commitment on the Appropriations Committee to NASA leadership. Frame Science Mission Directorate funding as the primary tool to protect the engineers and scientists he has pledged to support.

Bridge issues:

  • Climate Change & Natural Resources: "Your leadership on climate action and support for agencies like NOAA align perfectly with NASA’s Earth Science missions, which provide the essential data used to protect our natural resources and transition to a sustainable energy future."
  • Local Scientific Research & Jobs: "In our district, NASA science obligations exceed $350,000, including crucial magnetospheric research grants that directly support the local scientific jobs and innovation you fight for on the Appropriations Committee."

Watch out for:

  • Avoid assuming deep expertise in specific deep-space divisions like heliophysics or astrophysics; his record is much stronger regarding Earth science and general STEM workforce issues.
  • Be prepared for his attention to be divided by his specific subcommittee assignments in Energy and Water or Veterans Affairs, rather than the CJS subcommittee that directly funds NASA.

Expect this reaction: Representative Levin is likely to be highly receptive and supportive, given his history of signing SMD support letters and criticizing budget cuts to NASA scientists. His staff will likely appreciate the connection between national science goals and local economic impact in CA-49.

Key Opportunities

  • Committee Position: Leverage his position on the Appropriations Committee where he has pledged to ensure NASA receives the funding it needs to maintain leadership in space exploration.1
  • Direct NASA Record: Highlight his record of defending NASA scientists and engineers against budget cuts, which he has called 'decades-low' in inflation-adjusted terms.2

  • Adjacent Interest: Reference his personal connection to NASA history, including his memory of the Challenger tragedy, to emphasize the enduring public importance of space exploration.4

Key Challenges

  • Evidence Gap: While broadly supportive of NASA science, his record lacks specific recent commentary or signals regarding the planetary science, astrophysics, or heliophysics divisions.14
  • Competing Priority: His primary committee assignments focus on Energy and Water and Veterans Affairs, which may compete for his legislative attention over the Commerce-Justice-Science funding account.7 10

Strategic Considerations

  • Consider mentioning his personal memory of the Challenger tragedy to underscore why robust funding is necessary for mission safety and the enduring public importance of space.
  • You might find it useful to reference his past recognition of NASA 'Hidden Figures' in the district to illustrate that space exploration is a core part of his community's identity.
  • Think about whether you can connect his work expanding STEM access for veterans to the high-skilled workforce needs of the Science Mission Directorate.

About Representative Mike Levin (D-CA-49)

  • Earned a Juris Doctor from Duke University School of Law with a focus on environmental law and a Bachelor of Arts from Stanford University.
  • Specialized as an attorney in energy and environmental law, focusing on regulatory compliance and government affairs.
  • Held industry leadership roles including director of government affairs at FuelCell Energy, vice president of a cleantech startup, and co-founder of a sustainable energy trade group.
  • Legislative work focuses on environmental policy, including the removal of spent nuclear fuel from the San Onofre Nuclear Generating Station and the transition to zero-emission vehicles.
  • Authored multiple pieces of legislation related to veterans' affairs, including the Johnny Isakson and David P. Roe, M.D. Veterans Health Care and Benefits Improvement Act of 2020.
