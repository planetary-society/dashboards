Bottom Line

Likely Supportive (high confidence): Representative Hurd is an emerging advocate for NASA’s Earth Science missions, having successfully sponsored legislation to integrate commercial satellite data into the Science Mission Directorate. As a member of the House Science Committee, he views NASA's capabilities as vital tools for solving practical problems like wildfire response and water management in Colorado. To effectively engage him, emphasize how continued funding for NASA Science supports regional economic growth and high-tech STEM education in rural communities. Be prepared to focus on Earth Science and commercial partnerships, as his record on deep space exploration and planetary science is currently less defined.

Meeting Strategy

Lead with: The Practical Science Leader

Lead by recognizing Representative Hurd’s legislative leadership on the ASCEND Act, which demonstrates his commitment to NASA’s Science Mission Directorate. Frame NASA science as a pragmatic tool for Western Colorado, emphasizing how satellite data and local research help manage wildfires, water rights, and agricultural productivity.

Bridge issues:

  • Natural Resources and Water: "As you highlighted with the ASCEND Act, NASA's Earth science capabilities provide the essential data that Colorado's researchers and farmers need to respond to wildfires and manage our water resources more effectively."
  • Local STEM and Innovation: "NASA science is a direct investor in our district, providing nearly $2 million for institutions like Colorado Mesa University and the Aspen Global Change Institute to train the next generation of Colorado’s STEM workforce."

Watch out for:

  • Avoid assuming automatic support for deep space exploration or planetary missions; his record currently focuses almost exclusively on Earth science and commercial applications.
  • Do not overstate his commitment to the overall Science Mission Directorate budget, as he has not yet signed the key SMD funding letters that define direct science-specific support.

Expect this reaction: The office will likely be highly receptive to Earth science and commercial partnership discussions, but may be more cautious or non-committal regarding budget increases for planetary or astrophysics programs without a clear cost-efficiency argument.

Key Opportunities

  • Committee Position: Leverage his seat on the House Committee on Science, Space, and Technology to advocate for Science Mission Directorate priorities.4
  • Direct NASA Record: Highlight his sponsorship of the ASCEND Act as evidence of his leadership in enhancing NASA’s Science Mission Directorate capabilities.2 3
  • Local Institution: Discuss the $1.9 million in NASA science funding that supports local research at Colorado Mesa University and the Aspen Global Change Institute.7
  • Adjacent Interest: Emphasize how NASA science data supports wildfire response and agricultural monitoring, which he has identified as critical for his district.1

Key Challenges

  • Evidence Gap: Representative Hurd has a focused track record in Earth Science; there is an evidence gap regarding his views on planetary science, astrophysics, or heliophysics.9
  • Ideological Barrier: His support for NASA often emphasizes efficiency and cost-effectiveness, suggesting a potential preference for commercial data over traditional large-scale missions.1
  • Evidence Gap: The member has not yet signed recent high-level letters specifically advocating for a $9 billion Science Mission Directorate budget topline.10

Strategic Considerations

  • Consider exploring how NASA science programs might provide a pipeline for the students he recognizes through the Congressional App Challenge and his STEM-focused Innovation Fellowships.
  • It could be worth mentioning how the commercial satellite industry he supports via the ASCEND Act depends on the foundational R&D conducted within NASA’s broader science divisions.
  • You might find it useful to ask how his work on the Science Committee's Energy and Environment subcommittees might benefit from NASA’s unique ability to monitor energy infrastructure and environmental changes.

About Representative Jeff Hurd (R-CO-03)

  • Raised in Grand Junction, Colorado, and earned a bachelor's degree in philosophy from the University of Notre Dame.
  • Holds a Juris Doctor from the University of Denver Law School and clerked for the chief judge of the United States Court of Appeals for the Tenth Circuit.
  • Practiced law at an international firm in New York City before returning to Colorado to manage a law office in Grand Junction.
  • Worked for the Grand Junction Area Chamber of Commerce prior to his legal career.
  • Elected to the U.S. House of Representatives for Colorado's 3rd congressional district in 2024.
