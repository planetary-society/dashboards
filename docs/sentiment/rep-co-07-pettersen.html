Champion (high confidence): Representative Pettersen is a committed supporter of NASA science, motivated by both the massive aerospace presence in her district and her legislative focus on environmental resilience. Meeting Strategy

Lead with: Protecting Colorado’s Scientific Engine and Environmental Resilience

Lead by thanking Representative Pettersen for her consistent leadership in signing the $9 billion Science Mission Directorate support letters. You should frame NASA science as the technological foundation for her own legislative priorities in wildfire detection and agricultural protection, while emphasizing the massive $188 million local economic impact from missions like Lucy and OSIRIS-REx.

Bridge issues:

  • Wildfire and Agricultural Resilience: "Because you’ve championed bills like the Strengthening Wildfire Resiliency Through Satellites Act, we want to highlight how NASA’s Earth science missions provide the essential data that makes the early wildfire detection you're fighting for a reality."
  • Local Economic Development: "With major missions like Lucy and OSIRIS-REx managed right here in our district, NASA science isn't just about space—it's about the $188 million that supports our local workforce and high-tech economy."
  • STEM Workforce: "Your support for the AI Education Act shows you value 21st-century skills; NASA science serves as the ultimate inspiration for the next generation of Colorado students to enter these critical STEM fields."

Watch out for:

  • Focus strictly on civilian NASA science missions rather than general aerospace to avoid local political sensitivities surrounding major contractors' defense work.
  • While she is a champion for Earth and Planetary science, avoid assuming deep familiarity with Astrophysics or Heliophysics as her record is currently thin in those specific areas.

Expect this reaction: The office is likely to be highly receptive and well-informed, as the Representative is a proven champion who has already taken direct action to oppose cuts to the Science Mission Directorate.

Key Opportunities

  • Direct NASA Record: Pettersen has repeatedly signed onto letters requesting a $9 billion topline for the Science Mission Directorate across multiple fiscal years.2
  • District Fiscal Impact: The district is a hub for major planetary missions, with Lockheed Martin managing massive contracts for OSIRIS-REx, Lucy, and MAVEN.6
  • Bipartisan Coalition: She recently urged NASA to halt proposed budget cuts to science programs, signaling an active willingness to fight for the SMD budget.1
  • Local Institution: Pettersen authored funding provisions for local company Lunar Outpost to deploy autonomous rovers for lunar surface research.3
  • Adjacent Interest: Her legislative interest in using space-based technology for wildfire and agriculture provides a strong opening to advocate for Earth science missions.5 4

Key Challenges

  • Political Constraint: Pettersen has faced local public protests and political pressure regarding her ties to major aerospace contractors like Lockheed Martin.10 11
  • Competing Priority: Her primary legislative focus is often on high-intensity social issues like the opioid epidemic and behavioral health, which may limit her bandwidth for space policy.7
  • Evidence Gap: While her support for planetary and Earth science is clear, there is an evidence gap regarding her specific positions on astrophysics or heliophysics programs.12

Strategic Considerations

  • Consider mentioning her successful $5 million amendment for Lunar Outpost to show you follow her specific local wins in lunar research.
  • You might find it useful to highlight the research grants at the Colorado School of Mines, such as the EUSO-SPB2 project, to show how NASA science funds local academic excellence.
  • It could be worth mentioning how satellite-based agricultural data protects rural Colorado communities, directly aligning with her bipartisan Satellite-Based Agricultural Data Act.

About Representative Brittany Pettersen (D-CO-07)

  • Elected as the U.S. Representative for Colorado's 7th congressional district in 2022 and reelected in 2024.
  • Previously served in the Colorado Senate and the Colorado House of Representatives.
  • Earned a Bachelor of Arts degree in political science from the Metropolitan State University of Denver.
  • Held a role with New Era Colorado, a nonprofit political advocacy group, prior to serving as a state representative.
  • Advocating for a House measure to allow proxy voting for members following the birth of a child.
