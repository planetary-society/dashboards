Meeting Strategy

Lead with: Defense of the Scientific Workforce

Lead with the Representative's record as a champion for federal workers by framing NASA science funding as a direct protection for the high-skill civil service. Thank her specifically for cosponsoring the 'Saving NASA’s Workforce Act' and explain how stable funding for the Science Mission Directorate prevents the 'reductions in force' she is fighting to stop.

Bridge issues:

  • Federal Workforce Protections: "By supporting the full $9 billion for NASA's Science Mission Directorate, you are ensuring the job security of the expert civil servants who manage complex missions and preventing the involuntary separations your legislation seeks to prohibit."
  • STEM Diversity and Inclusion: "Strong investment in NASA science missions creates the essential research opportunities and inspiration needed to close the gap for underrepresented minorities in the STEM workforce."
  • Institutional Stability in the District: "A fully funded Science Mission Directorate reinforces NASA Headquarters' role as a stable anchor institution in the National Capital Region, which aligns with your efforts to keep federal agency leadership centered in the District."

Watch out for:

  • Be mindful that as a non-voting delegate, her influence is centered on committee leadership and public advocacy rather than floor votes on final appropriations.
  • Avoid referencing specific NASA grant dollar totals for the District, as her record is focused on institutional infrastructure and broader workforce policy rather than a specific local contract footprint.

Expect this reaction: The office is likely to be highly receptive and potentially offer leadership on the issue; Representative Norton is a proven champion who recently lead-signed letters defending the VERITAS and Chandra missions from cuts.

About Representative Eleanor Holmes Norton (D-DC-00)

  • Has represented the District of Columbia as a Democratic congressional delegate since 1991 and is currently serving her eighteenth term.
  • Served as the first female chair of the Equal Employment Opportunity Commission from 1977 to 1981 and has been a professor at Georgetown University Law Center since 1982.
  • Earned a Master of Arts in American studies and a Bachelor of Laws from Yale University after graduating from Antioch College.
  • Has introduced the Nuclear Disarmament and Economic Conversion Act regularly since 1994, which seeks the dismantling of nuclear weapons in coordination with other nuclear-armed nations.
  • Supported the Digital Accountability and Transparency Act of 2014 to require the Department of the Treasury to establish common financial data standards for all government agencies.
