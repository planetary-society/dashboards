Bottom Line

Likely Supportive (moderate confidence): Representative Dunn is a retiring surgeon and veteran who views space primarily as a critical arena for national security and economic infrastructure. While his legislative record focuses on commercial spaceports, he represents a district with significant NASA-funded research at Florida State University, including studies on astronaut health and Earth observations. Advocates should frame NASA science as a tool for American global leadership and a direct contributor to local medical and environmental research. Since he is not seeking re-election, he may be particularly receptive to legacy-defining investments in space infrastructure and STEM workforce development.

Meeting Strategy

Lead with: Strategic Dominance and Human Health

Lead with the argument that NASA science is a cornerstone of American national security and a driver of medical innovation, directly aligning with his roles as a surgeon and a member of the CCP Select Committee. You should frame support for the Science Mission Directorate as essential to maintaining our technological edge over global adversaries while advancing critical health research currently underway at Florida State University.

Bridge issues:

  • National Security and Countering China: "Maintaining our scientific leadership through NASA is a direct way to ensure the United States—not our adversaries—sets the pace for discovery and defines the global standards for space technology."
  • Healthcare and Medical Innovation: "As a physician, you may be interested to know that NASA-funded research at Florida State University is currently studying how space radiation impacts cardiovascular health, which provides vital data for both deep-space travel and heart disease treatments here on Earth."
  • Local Economic and Research Impact: "NASA's science investments in your district, which totaled over $3.7 million recently, are supporting high-level research at Florida State University into everything from public health modeling to our planet's fossil record."

Watch out for:

  • Avoid assuming he is already a vocal champion for the Science Mission Directorate; while he supports NASA's broad budget, he has not signed onto recent letters specifically calling for increased science funding.
  • Be mindful of his retirement at the end of this term; frame your ask as a way to cement his legacy of securing Florida's role as a leader in space-based medical and technological research.

Expect this reaction: The office is likely to be polite and intellectually engaged, particularly regarding medical and security applications, but may be noncommittal on specific funding increases given his history of skipping science-specific budget letters.

Key Challenges

  • Competing Priority: His primary space focus is commercial infrastructure and spaceports, which may draw attention and funding away from pure scientific exploration programs.5 7 8

  • Consider highlighting his own 'Supporting Veterans in STEM Careers Act' to show how NASA's science missions create the precise workforce opportunities he has championed for veterans.
  • You might find it useful to mention his previous service on the board of Space Florida to discuss how NASA science programs provide the 'payloads' and missions that drive the commercial spaceport infrastructure he prioritizes.
  • Think about whether you can connect the FSU research on 'early animals' to the broader narrative of NASA's role in understanding Earth's history as a fundamental part of our national scientific legacy.

About Representative Neal P. Dunn (R-FL-02)

  • He has served as the U.S. Representative for Florida's 2nd congressional district since 2017.
  • A surgeon and medical doctor, he earned a Bachelor of Science in interdisciplinary sciences and a Doctor of Medicine.
  • He served in the United States Army for eleven years of active duty, reaching the rank of major and completing a medical internship at Walter Reed Army Medical Center.
  • His private sector experience includes founding the Panama City Urological Center, the Panama City Surgery Center, and serving as the founding chairman of Summit Bank.
  • On January 13, 2026, he announced he would not seek re-election following a fifth term in the House of Representatives.
