Bottom Line

Likely Supportive (moderate confidence): Representative Lee is a consistent supporter of NASA's broader mission and workforce, exemplified by her vote for the NASA budget and her legislative effort to move the agency's headquarters to Florida. Her district is a major hub for NASA Earth Science and biological research, with the University of South Florida receiving millions to study algae blooms and marine biodiversity. While she has not yet signed specific Science Mission Directorate funding letters, her focus on STEM education and national security provides a natural bridge to advocating for science missions. Advocates should emphasize how NASA science directly benefits Florida's environmental management and maintains American leadership in technology.

Meeting Strategy

Lead with: The Florida-First Science Strategy

Lead with the $6.7 million in NASA science funding currently supporting the University of South Florida in her district. You should frame Science Mission Directorate funding as a direct investment in the tools and data Florida needs to manage its environment and train a high-tech AI workforce.

Bridge issues:

  • Environmental Management and Local Economy: "The research at USF on algae blooms and marine life—which relies on data from NASA science missions like PACE—is exactly the kind of practical environmental management Florida needs to protect its economy."
  • STEM and AI Workforce: "By fully funding NASA's science missions, we are providing the high-level STEM and coding training grounds needed to inspire the 'next generation of trailblazers' you have advocated for in Florida."

Watch out for:

  • Avoid assuming she is already a committed supporter of specific science funding levels; while she supports NASA generally, she has notably not signed the recent programmatic letters for the Science Mission Directorate.
  • Do not focus the meeting on commercial space launches or the Artemis program, as the Representative is already active in those areas—pivot instead to how science missions provide the essential data those programs require.

Expect this reaction: The office is likely to be highly receptive to discussions regarding local jobs and environmental monitoring at USF, but they may remain non-committal on specific topline funding increases without a clear national security or district-centric justification.

Key Opportunities

  • District Fiscal Impact: Highlight the $6.7M in NASA science obligations for FL-15, which primarily supports critical Earth Science research at the University of South Florida.9 1
  • Bipartisan Coalition: Leverage her support for the CAPE Canaveral Act to discuss how a strong Science Mission Directorate budget reinforces Florida's status as a global aerospace hub.2
  • Adjacent Interest: Connect NASA science missions to her stated priority of inspiring 'the next generation of trailblazers' in STEM and computer science fields.7 4
  • Local Institution: Discuss the PACE and GRACE-FO missions, which are specifically utilized by local researchers to monitor ocean health and sea levels.1

Key Challenges

  • Evidence Gap: She has not yet signed the annual programmatic letters specifically requesting the $9 billion funding level for the Science Mission Directorate.3
  • Competing Priority: Her space-related advocacy has recently focused on commercial launch operations and the Artemis program rather than dedicated science accounts.5
  • Evidence Gap: There is a significant evidence gap regarding her positions on planetary science, astrophysics, and heliophysics compared to her strong record on Earth Science.10

Strategic Considerations

  • You might find it useful to mention that a former member of her senior staff recently moved to a role in NASA’s Office of Communications, which could serve as a friendly opening to discuss the agency's impact.
  • Consider exploring the link between NASA's complex scientific data sets and her work on the House AI Task Force, highlighting how space science is a primary driver for developing advanced computing skills.
  • It could be worth mentioning how her support for the CAPE Canaveral Act aligns with a robust science budget, as moving headquarters functions to Florida would require a strong, well-funded agency to manage.

About Representative Laurel M. Lee (R-FL-15)

  • Born at Wright-Patterson Air Force Base to a U.S. Air Force officer who attained the rank of major general.
  • Served as Florida's Secretary of State from 2019 to 2022, where she oversaw election security measures and record voter turnout.
  • Held judicial and legal roles as a judge on Florida's Thirteenth Judicial Circuit, an Assistant United States Attorney, and an Assistant Federal Public Defender.
  • Focuses on technology policy and national security, including serving on the bipartisan House Task Force on Artificial Intelligence.
  • Introduced the DEFIANCE Act to address non-consensual deepfake images and was the lead sponsor of the Reforming Intelligence and Securing America Act.
