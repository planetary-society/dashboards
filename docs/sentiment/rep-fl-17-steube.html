About Representative W. Gregory Steube (R-FL-17)

  • Earned a degree in Animal Science from the University of Florida in 2000 and a Juris Doctor from the University of Florida Fredric G. Levin College of Law in 2003.
  • Served as a Captain in the U.S. Army JAG Corps from 2004 to 2008, including a deployment to Iraq in support of Operation Iraqi Freedom.
  • Held state-level legislative office in the Florida House of Representatives (2010–2016) and the Florida Senate (2016–2018) before his election to Congress.
  • Introduced legislation in 2024 to name the U.S. exclusive economic zone after Donald Trump and proposed renaming the Washington Metropolitan Area Transit Authority (WMATA) in 2025.
  • Successfully led a discharge petition in 2024 to bring a disaster relief bill to the House floor, securing bipartisan support from 29 Republicans and 189 Democrats.
