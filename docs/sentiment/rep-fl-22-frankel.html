About Representative Lois Frankel (D-FL-22)

  • Earned a Juris Doctor from Georgetown University Law Center in 1973 and a bachelor's degree from Boston University in 1970.
  • Served 14 years in the Florida House of Representatives, where she became the first female House Minority Leader in the state's history.
  • Served two terms as the Mayor of West Palm Beach from 2003 to 2011, focusing on business development and pension reform during her tenure.
  • Co-authored a change to Florida's AIDS omnibus law and chaired the state's AIDS Task Force and the Select Committee on Child Abuse & Neglect.
  • Has served in the U.S. House of Representatives since 2013, representing Florida's 21st and 22nd districts.
