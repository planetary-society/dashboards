Expect this reaction: The office will likely be very receptive to discussions about local grants and STEM workforce development, but may remain noncommittal on specific high-level funding increases for science divisions without more evidence of direct economic return for the district.

About Representative Maria Elvira Salazar (R-FL-27)

  • Earned a Bachelor of Arts in communications from the University of Miami and a Master of Public Administration from the Harvard Kennedy School.
  • Spent over three decades as a journalist and news anchor for major Spanish-language networks including Telemundo, Univision, CNN Español, and Mega TV.
  • Served as a White House and Pentagon correspondent for Univision starting in 1988 and later as a bureau chief in Central America.
  • Introduced the 'No AI Fraud Act' in 2024 to establish individual property rights for unique physical attributes, such as voice, against AI-generated deepfakes.
  • Recognized for her professional work with five Emmy Awards and was named to the inaugural Forbes 50 Over 50 list in 2021.
