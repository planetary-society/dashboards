Meeting Strategy

Lead with: Accountability and Local Impact

Lead with Representative Clyde’s influential role on the CJS Appropriations Subcommittee and focus on how NASA science investments drive high-tech manufacturing in Georgia’s 9th District. Frame the Science Mission Directorate as a model for the fiscal accountability and project management he demands of the agency. Given the thin direct SMD record, treat this as the best available opening rather than evidence of likely support.

Bridge issues:

  • Fiscal Accountability and Cost Management: "We share your concern about ballooning mission costs, which is why we support the Science Mission Directorate's use of competitive, peer-reviewed selections to ensure only the most efficient and high-value missions move forward."
  • Small Business and Local Manufacturing: "NASA science funding directly supports Georgia’s economy, including Ninth District companies like Winton Machine and Nsi-Mi Technologies that provide specialized equipment for critical missions."

Watch out for:

  • Avoid assuming Representative Clyde is already a champion of space science; he has not signed recent funding support letters and his public focus remains heavily on deficit reduction.
  • Do not oversell science-specific support when the public record is thin; keep the ask anchored in local value and broad NASA space science.

Expect this reaction: The office is likely to be lukewarm and highly focused on the bottom line. Given Representative Clyde's commitment to deficit reduction, expect him to view any funding request with skepticism unless it is clearly tied to fiscal restraint and regional economic stability. Expect a cautious, potentially non-committal response unless the ask is tied tightly to local priorities.

About Representative Andrew S. Clyde (R-GA-09)

  • Served 28 years in the United States Navy as a commissioned officer in naval aviation units and the Seabees, retiring with the rank of commander in 2013.
  • Completed three combat deployments to Iraq and Kuwait and received the Defense Meritorious Service Medal and four Navy Commendation Medals.
  • Graduated cum laude with a BBA in accounting and business management from Bethel University and earned an MBA in corporate finance and entrepreneurship from the University of Georgia.
  • Established and grew a firearms business, Clyde Armory, Inc., to $12 million in annual sales and 25 employees.
  • Advocated for civil asset forfeiture reform before Congress, leading to the inclusion of the Clyde-Hirsch-Sowers RESPECT Act in the Taxpayer First Act of 2019.
