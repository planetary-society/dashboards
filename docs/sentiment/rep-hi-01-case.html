Meeting Strategy

Lead with: Protecting Hawaii’s High-Tech Economy

Lead by framing NASA science funding as a vital engine for Hawaii’s economic diversification and a safeguard for its world-leading astronomical infrastructure. You should emphasize that Science Mission Directorate (SMD) grants, particularly in planetary defense and Earth science, provide the high-tech jobs and federal investment he has fought to protect from budget cuts that could 'cripple' U.S. research.

Bridge issues:

  • STEM Workforce & Diversity: "NASA's Science Mission Directorate fuels the STEM pipeline that you’ve championed, providing the research grants and Space Grant funding necessary to bring more women and minorities into Hawaii's high-tech workforce."
  • Economic Diversification & Planetary Defense: "By supporting the planetary science budget, you are directly investing in Hawaii’s role as a global leader in planetary defense through the Pan-STARRS and ATLAS systems, which bring millions in federal funding to the islands annually."

Watch out for:

  • Avoid assuming the Representative will prioritize NASA science over his other heavy committee responsibilities in Defense and Homeland Security; always tie space science back to 'national policy' and local economic stability.
  • Do not overstate his past commitment to specific NASA science accounts; while he is a vocal defender of R&D, there is no direct record of him signing onto Science Mission Directorate-specific funding letters.

Expect this reaction: The Representative is likely to be highly receptive to arguments regarding local economic impact and STEM diversity, though his staff may be cautious about committing to specific funding increases given his role in balancing the broader Appropriations budget.

About Representative Ed Case (D-HI-01)

  • Served as a legislative assistant to Congressman and Senator Spark Matsunaga from 1975 to 1978.
  • Earned a bachelor's degree in psychology from Williams College and a Juris Doctor from the University of California Hastings College of Law.
  • Practiced law for nearly two decades at Carlsmith Ball in Honolulu, becoming a partner in 1989 and managing partner from 1992 to 1994.
  • Served as a law clerk to Hawaii Supreme Court Chief Justice William S. Richardson from 1981 to 1982.
  • Worked in the private sector for Outrigger Enterprises Group starting in 2013 before returning to congressional service in 2019.
