Representative Danny K. Davis (D-IL-07)

Representative | D | IL-07

NASA Budget Support

Strong SMD budget signal

  • FY2026 CJS Appropriations vote: Yea on H.R. 6938
  • $9B NASA Science letter signatory: FY26, FY27

Bottom Line

Likely Supportive (high confidence): Representative Davis is a reliable supporter of NASA's Science Mission Directorate, viewing space science as a vital tool for national research and STEM equity. He has consistently signed onto letters requesting a $9 billion budget for NASA science and has a strong record of securing local funding for Chicago-based institutions like the Adler Planetarium and the Field Museum. Advocates should frame NASA science as a driver of educational opportunity and community development, as he frequently links federal science spending to 'broader impacts' for underrepresented youth. Although he is retiring in 2026, his commitment to protecting NASA's workforce and infrastructure remains a strong point of alignment for The Planetary Society.

Meeting Strategy

Lead with: NASA Science as a Catalyst for Educational Equity

Lead with how NASA's Science Mission Directorate (SMD) creates tangible opportunities for Chicago youth, citing the Representative's own recent funding requests for STEM expansion. Frame the current budget threats not just as a loss for space, but as a direct blow to the 'broader impacts' and community access he has championed throughout his career.

Bridge issues:

  • Education and STEM Access: "You should mention that NASA science funding supports local gems like the Adler Planetarium and the Chicago Urban League, ensuring that low-income students in the 7th District have a seat at the table in our nation's space future."
  • Planetary Science and Local Research: "You can highlight that researchers at the Field Museum are using NASA grants to study the very origins of our solar system, proving that world-class planetary science is happening right here in Chicago."

Watch out for:

  • Since the Representative is retiring in 2026, avoid framing this as a long-term political gamble; instead, present it as a critical opportunity to cement his legacy as a protector of American scientific leadership.
  • Be careful not to present general NASA support as a given; while he is a strong ally, he specifically values science that 'gives back' to the community, so always tie the budget ask to local impact or STEM access.

Expect this reaction: Expect a very warm reception, as the Representative is a proven champion who has signed recent letters supporting a $9 billion science budget. The staff will likely be most responsive when you connect the funding to the district's $5.1 million in NASA science obligations.

Key Opportunities

  • Direct NASA Record: Signed the FY26 and FY27 Science Mission Directorate letters calling for a $9 billion budget, indicating direct support for the SMD topline.2
  • Local Institution: The district hosts significant NASA science activity, including $2.5M for the Adler Planetarium and over $1M for the Field Museum for planetary science research.2
  • District Fiscal Impact: Requested $450,000 in federal funding for a Chicago STEM project specifically citing a federal nexus to Title 51 (National and Commercial Space Programs).3

Key Challenges

  • Political Constraint: Representative Davis has announced he will not seek reelection in 2026, which may shift his focus toward immediate legacy priorities rather than long-term space policy.2
  • Evidence Gap: While supportive of the SMD topline and planetary science, there is an evidence gap regarding his specific positions on the Heliophysics and Astrophysics divisions.2

Strategic Considerations

  • Think about whether you can connect NASA’s Earth Science missions to his interest in environmental justice and climate change, especially regarding how satellite data helps monitor urban pollution.
  • You might find it useful to mention the $1.4 million in relocation services contracts in the district as evidence of NASA's broader economic footprint in Chicago beyond research grants.
  • It could be worth exploring how NASA's Open Science initiatives, like the grant to National Louis University, align with his career-long push for 'broader impacts' in federal research.

About Representative Danny K. Davis (D-IL-07)

  • Earned a B.A. in history from Arkansas Agricultural, Mechanical and Normal College, an M.S. in guidance from Chicago State University, and a Ph.D. in public administration from Union Institute & University.
  • Professional background includes service as a high school teacher, government clerk, and executive director of the Greater Lawndale Conservation Commission.
  • Held healthcare leadership roles as the director of training at the Martin L. King Neighborhood Health Center and executive director of the Westside Health Center.
  • Served in local government as a member of the Chicago City Council (1979–1990) and the Cook County Board of Commissioners (1990–1996) prior to his 1997 entry into Congress.
  • Announced on July 31, 2025, that he will not seek reelection to the U.S. House of Representatives in 2026.
