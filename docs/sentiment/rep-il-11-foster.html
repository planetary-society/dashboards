About Representative Bill Foster (D-IL-11)

  • Earned a Ph.D. in physics from Harvard University and is one of the few research physicists ever elected to Congress.
  • Spent 22 years as a high-energy particle physicist at Fermilab, where he worked on the discovery of the top quark and managed the construction of the Anti-Proton Recycler Ring.
  • Received the 1989 Bruno Rossi Prize for cosmic ray physics and the Robert R. Wilson Prize for Achievement in the Physics of Particle Accelerators in 2022.
  • Co-founded Electronic Theatre Controls at age 19, a company that manufactures theatrical lighting fixtures and control systems.
  • Elected as a fellow of the American Physical Society and received an Energy Conservation award from the U.S. Department of Energy for accelerator technology applications.
