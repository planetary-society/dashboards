Meeting Strategy

Lead with: The Appropriations Powerhouse & Local Science Champion

Lead with her influential position on the House Appropriations Committee and the cutting-edge NASA research occurring at Northern Illinois University. You should frame NASA space science as a primary driver of the 'American Innovation' ecosystem she has historically supported to ensure Illinois remains a leader in federal research.

Bridge issues:

  • STEM Workforce Development: "As a leader in expanding STEM opportunities for veterans and students through the Congressional App Challenge, you know that NASA's ambitious science missions are the ultimate tool for inspiring the next generation of Illinois scientists and engineers."
  • Local Research Impact: "Northern Illinois University is currently using NASA grants to unlock the mysteries of early Mars and search for signs of life on other worlds, proving that the 14th District is directly contributing to the most profound questions in human history."

  • Avoid assuming she is a dedicated space science advocate; while she supports the broader NASA budget, she has not signed recent 'Dear Colleague' letters specifically requesting $9 billion for the Science Mission Directorate.
  • Do not frame space funding as a competitor to healthcare spending; instead, acknowledge her healthcare expertise and emphasize that federal R&D investment supports the broader innovation economy she values.

Expect this reaction: The office will likely be supportive of the broad STEM and educational value of NASA but may be non-committal regarding specific funding levels for the Science Mission Directorate given her heavy legislative focus on healthcare and public health.

About Representative Lauren Underwood (D-IL-14)

  • Registered nurse who earned a Bachelor of Science in Nursing from the University of Michigan and a Master of Science in Nursing and Master of Public Health from Johns Hopkins University.
  • Served as a senior advisor at the U.S. Department of Health and Human Services (HHS) during the Obama administration, focusing on the implementation of the Patient Protection and Affordable Care Act.
  • Held private sector experience as the senior director of strategy and regulatory affairs at Next Level Health and served as an adjunct instructor at the Georgetown University School of Nursing & Health Studies.
  • Elected to the U.S. House of Representatives in 2018, becoming the youngest Black woman to serve in Congress.
  • Focuses on healthcare policy and disparities in Congress, specifically regarding maternal health outcomes and protections for individuals with pre-existing conditions.
