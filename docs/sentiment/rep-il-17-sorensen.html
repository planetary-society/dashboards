Meeting Strategy

Lead with: The Scientist-Legislator

Lead with Representative Sorensen’s unique background as a meteorologist and his proven legislative record in streamlining NASA’s research partnerships. You should thank him directly for signing the FY27 Science Mission Directorate support letter, framing a robust NASA science budget as the essential foundation for the interagency innovation he champions.

Bridge issues:

  • Agriculture and Earth Science: "As a member of the Agriculture Committee, you understand better than anyone that NASA’s Earth science missions provide the critical climate data our Illinois farmers rely on for crop planning and economic stability."
  • Interagency Innovation: "Your work on the DOE and NASA Interagency Research Coordination Act shows a commitment to maximizing research efficiency, and a fully funded Science Mission Directorate is what provides the data and discovery that fuel these collaborations."
  • STEM Education and Legacy: "With your personal family connection to the space shuttle program, you have a powerful platform to show students in our district how planetary exploration like the Artemis and Mars programs can inspire the next generation of Illinois engineers."

Watch out for:

  • Avoid assuming his support for Earth science and Mars automatically extends to deep-space astrophysics or heliophysics, as his public record is currently silent on those specific divisions.
  • Do not frame budget requests as a reason to accelerate mission timelines; the Representative has been very clear that he prioritizes astronaut safety and methodical planning over meeting specific dates.

Expect this reaction: Expect a highly receptive and knowledgeable response; as a 'science-first' champion and recent SMD letter signer, he is likely to agree with the technical and economic necessity of NASA science funding.

About Representative Eric Sorensen (D-IL-17)

  • Has served as the U.S. Representative for Illinois's 17th district since 2023.
  • Worked for over 20 years as a meteorologist, including roles as chief meteorologist for WREX in Rockford and senior meteorologist for WQAD in Moline.
  • Studied communications and meteorology at Northern Illinois University.
  • Named a fellow of the Society for Environmental Journalists in 2018.
  • Worked in communications for UnityPoint Health prior to entering congressional service.
