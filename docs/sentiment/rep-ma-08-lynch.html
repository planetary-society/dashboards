Likely Supportive (high confidence): Representative Lynch is a reliable ally for NASA science, particularly as a protector of the federal research workforce and Earth science initiatives. With a professional background in high-altitude ironwork and a focus on labor, he views NASA as a critical employer and a driver of 21st-century STEM jobs. Meeting Strategy

Lead with: Defense of the Scientific Workforce

Lead with Representative Lynch's proven commitment to protecting federal jobs and the scientific enterprise. You should frame NASA science funding as an essential investment in the high-skilled workforce and labor stability that he has consistently defended against proposed budget cuts.

Bridge issues:

  • Labor and Working Families: "I want to thank the Congressman for opposing the proposed 25 percent cut to NASA's budget, which would have eliminated one-third of the agency's workforce and harmed the middle-class families he fights for."
  • Local Earth Science Research: "NASA funding is directly benefiting our district through the $3.6 million in science obligations to local institutions, including critical climate and kelp forest research at UMass Boston."

Watch out for:

  • Avoid framing commercial space partnerships as an unalloyed benefit; the Representative is currently leading oversight investigations into potential conflicts of interest and contracting practices involving major commercial space firms.
  • Do not overstate his familiarity with specific planetary or astrophysics missions; his record indicates a much stronger focus on Earth science and the impact of federal job cuts on national research capabilities.

Expect this reaction: The office is likely to be highly supportive of the NASA science budget ask, as the Representative has consistently signed multi-year letters for Science Mission Directorate funding. Expect a particularly strong positive response if you focus on workforce protection and local Earth science grants.

Key Opportunities

  • Direct NASA Record: Lean on his multi-year record of signing onto 'Dear Colleague' letters that explicitly call for $9 billion in funding for the Science Mission Directorate.1
  • Local Institution: Highlight the local impact of the $3.6 million in NASA science funding flowing into his district, especially the climate-focused Earth science grants at UMass Boston.9 6
  • Committee Position: Utilize his role on the Oversight Committee to discuss how stable NASA funding prevents the 'irreparable harm' to the American scientific enterprise he has previously warned about.10 3

Key Challenges

  • Evidence Gap: There is an evidence gap regarding his specific stances on planetary science, astrophysics, and heliophysics, as his record leans heavily toward Earth science and workforce issues.11 6
  • Political Constraint: His current oversight investigations into conflicts of interest with commercial partners like SpaceX may make him cautious regarding certain public-private partnership models.4 12

Strategic Considerations

  • Consider exploring how NASA's climate and weather data serves as 'essential infrastructure' for modernizing public transit and safety systems, which is a major priority for his district.
  • You might find it useful to bridge his background in vocational training and the Iron Workers to NASA’s reliance on advanced manufacturing and specialized technical skills.
  • It could be worth mentioning the $2 million contract for The Tomorrow Companies in MA-08 as a tangible example of how NASA science supports local innovation and job creation.

About Representative Stephen F. Lynch (D-MA-08)

  • Worked for six years as an apprentice and structural ironworker on high-altitude projects for companies including General Motors and U.S. Steel.
  • Served as the youngest president of the Iron Workers Local 7 union at age 30 while attending the Wentworth Institute of Technology.
  • Holds a bachelor's degree in construction management from Wentworth Institute of Technology, a J.D. from Boston College Law School, and a master's degree from Harvard University's John F. Kennedy School of Government.
  • Practiced law representing labor unions and unemployed workers, and provided pro bono legal services to housing project residents.
  • Focuses on trade policy and manufacturing jobs, serving as a co-founder and co-chair of the Congressional Labor and Working Families Caucus.
