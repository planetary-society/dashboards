Meeting Strategy

Lead with: Lead with the 'Blue Economy' and Ocean Worlds

You should frame NASA science as a direct extension of the Representative's commitment to the 'Blue Economy' and the world-class marine research at Woods Hole. Explain that the same oceanographic expertise used to protect Massachusetts' coasts is now being used by NASA to search for life on moons like Europa, making his district a global leader in both Earth and planetary science.

Bridge issues:

  • Blue Economy & Marine Technology: "The same expertise at Woods Hole that fuels our local 'Blue Economy' is currently helping NASA design strategies to find life on the ocean worlds of our solar system."
  • Environmental Conservation & Climate Change: "NASA’s Earth science missions, such as the S-MODE project involving Woods Hole, provide the precise data you’ve called for to help our coastal communities plan for and adapt to rising sea levels."

Watch out for:

  • Avoid assuming deep interest in space exploration for its own sake; his public record is heavily focused on Earth science and ocean dynamics rather than deep-space missions like Mars Sample Return.
  • Be careful not to let the conversation shift entirely to defense or the Space National Guard; while he is active on the Armed Services Committee, emphasize NASA's role as a civilian science agency.

Expect this reaction: The office is likely to be highly receptive and knowledgeable, given the Representative's consistent history of signing Science Mission Directorate support letters and the $21 million in NASA science funding flowing to his district.

Key Challenges

  • Evidence Gap: Evidence Gap: While he is a strong advocate for Earth science, there is very little public record of his positions on planetary science, astrophysics, or heliophysics.6
  • Competing Priority: His legislative energy is heavily concentrated on coastal resilience and maritime defense, which could sideline deep-space science if it is not framed as relevant to those interests.4 10

Strategic Considerations

  • Consider mentioning his support for the CHIPS and Science Act to highlight how NASA's Science Mission Directorate drives the domestic innovation and high-tech manufacturing he prioritizes.
  • You might find it useful to emphasize that the search for life on ocean worlds is a direct application of astrobiology, bridging the gap between his interest in marine biology and planetary exploration.
  • Think about whether you can leverage his focus on national security by discussing how NASA’s scientific R&D maintains the technological edge required for the modernization of the forces he oversees.

About Representative William R. Keating (D-MA-09)

  • Earned a Master of Business Administration from Boston College and a Juris Doctor from Suffolk University Law School.
  • Served three terms as the District Attorney of Norfolk County, where he founded the Norfolk Anti-Crime Council and established a drug court pilot program.
  • Legislative focus in the U.S. House has included maritime policy, fisheries management, and nuclear safety regarding the Pilgrim Nuclear Generating Station.
  • Participated in a congressional delegation to Afghanistan, Pakistan, and Iraq following the 2011 execution of Osama bin Laden.
  • Facilitated the cleanup and sale of a defunct naval air base in South Weymouth to support private development projects.
