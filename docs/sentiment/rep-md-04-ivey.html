Meeting Strategy

Lead with: The Defender of NASA Goddard and Science Jobs

Lead by thanking the Representative for his vocal leadership on the CJS Appropriations subcommittee and his public defense of the Science Mission Directorate. You should frame NASA science funding as the vital economic engine that sustains over $1.3 billion in annual local investment and protects the jobs of thousands of civil servants and contractors in his district.

Bridge issues:

  • Federal Workers and Civil Servants: "With the district receiving an average of $1.3 billion annually in NASA science obligations, protecting the Science Mission Directorate budget is the most direct way to prevent layoffs and safeguard the careers of the thousands of experts at Goddard Space Flight Center."

Expect this reaction: Expect a highly receptive and enthusiastic reaction; as a co-chair of the Advancing Humanity in Space Caucus who has personally rallied with The Planetary Society, the Representative is likely to see you as a key ally in his fight against proposed budget cuts.

About Representative Glenn Ivey (D-MD-04)

  • Served as chairman of the Maryland Public Service Commission, where he oversaw the deregulation of the electric power industry and monitored the telecommunications industry.
  • Held multiple senior legal roles on Capitol Hill, including chief counsel to Senate Majority Leader Tom Daschle and majority counsel to the Senate Banking Committee.
  • Served as an assistant U.S. attorney for the Department of Justice under Eric Holder and was twice elected as the state's attorney for Prince George's County, Maryland.
  • Taught trial advocacy at Harvard Law School and served as an adjunct professor at the University of Maryland School of Law for nearly two decades.
  • Earned an A.B. degree in politics with honors from Princeton University and a J.D. from Harvard Law School.
