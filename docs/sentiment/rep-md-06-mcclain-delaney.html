Meeting Strategy

Lead with: The Defender of Maryland's Space Workforce

Lead by thanking the Congresswoman for her fierce defense of NASA Goddard and missions like Dragonfly and Nancy Grace Roman. You should frame robust Science Mission Directorate (SMD) funding as essential to protecting what she calls a 'national jewel'—Maryland's high-tech workforce and its leadership in scientific discovery.

Bridge issues:

  • American Innovation and Jobs: "As you’ve noted, NASA missions are what American innovation looks like—they support good-paying jobs in our district and inspire the next generation to reach higher."
  • Agricultural Stability: "NASA’s Earth science missions provide the critical data needed for the agricultural conservation and supply chain stability you've prioritized on the Agriculture Committee."

Watch out for:

  • While she is a champion for major Maryland missions, her record is thinner regarding specific support for Heliophysics or Biological and Physical Sciences, so keep the focus on the broader SMD topline.
  • Don't let the conversation get lost in AI or quantum policy; instead, frame NASA’s complex science missions as the ultimate real-world application for the deep-tech standards she is drafting.

Expect this reaction: Expect a very warm and knowledgeable reception; the Congresswoman is already a signed supporter of the $9 billion science budget and will likely view your visit as a reinforcement of her own committee work.

Key Opportunities

  • Committee Position: As a member of the House Committee on Science, Space, and Technology, she has a direct platform to influence NASA's budget and oversight.6
  • Direct NASA Record: She has signed direct letters of support for a $9 billion Science Mission Directorate budget in both the FY26 and FY27 cycles.4
  • Local Institution: She has explicitly defended the Dragonfly (Planetary Science) and Nancy Grace Roman (Astrophysics) missions against facility closures and workforce cuts at NASA Goddard.3 1 2
  • Direct NASA Record: She co-sponsored the 'Saving NASA's Workforce Act' to protect career employees from involuntary separations during budget uncertainties.10
  • District Fiscal Impact: Her district receives significant NASA science funding, averaging $106K annually, including research into growing fresh food for astronauts in space.5

Key Challenges

  • Evidence Gap: While a champion for large Maryland-based missions, her record is thinner regarding specific support for the Heliophysics and Biological and Physical Sciences divisions.11
  • Competing Priority: She has significant legislative focuses on AI safeguards and rural broadband that may compete for her primary attention in committee hearings.9 8

Strategic Considerations

  • It could be worth mentioning her support for the Frostburg State University planetarium as a way to thank her for investing in regional STEM literacy.
  • You might find it useful to discuss how budget stability for the Science Mission Directorate directly supports her goal of preventing involuntary separations of career employees at NASA.
  • Consider exploring how NASA's research into growing fresh food for astronauts—a project already receiving funding in her district—bridges her interests in space exploration and agricultural innovation.

About Representative April McClain Delaney (D-MD-06)

  • Earned a Bachelor of Science degree in communications from Northwestern University and a Juris Doctor from the Georgetown University Law Center.
  • Served as the deputy administrator of the National Telecommunications and Information Administration (NTIA) within the U.S. Department of Commerce from 2022 to 2023.
  • Spent much of her legal career as a media lawyer and founded the Washington, D.C. division of Common Sense Media, a nonprofit focused on the effects of media on children.
  • Advocates for increasing funding for climate science research and providing tax incentives for investments in decarbonization technologies.
  • Supports the use of new technologies to help secure the Mexico–United States border.
