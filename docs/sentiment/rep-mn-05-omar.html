Meeting Strategy

Lead with: The Champion of the $9B Science Topline

Lead by thanking the Representative for her consistent leadership in signing the FY25, FY26, and FY27 letters requesting $9 billion for NASA’s Science Mission Directorate. You should frame the current budget threat not just as a space issue, but as a direct threat to the $17 million in active research funding that supports high-tech jobs and student opportunities at the University of Minnesota.

Bridge issues:

  • STEM Education: "Just as you inspire local students through the Congressional App Challenge, NASA’s science missions provide the real-world 'moonshot' goals that motivate Minnesota’s youth to pursue careers in coding and computer science."
  • Environmental Justice: "NASA’s Earth science missions provide the essential data required to address the climate crisis and protect vulnerable communities, directly supporting your goal of a transition to a 100% clean energy economy."
  • Workforce Protections: "By funding NASA science, we are investing in the University of Minnesota’s research workforce, ensuring that the technology boom in areas like solar physics and astrobiology benefits local workers and researchers."

Watch out for:

  • Avoid framing NASA funding as a competition with social programs; instead, emphasize that federal scientific research is a public good that creates the high-skilled jobs she advocates for.
  • Do not assume the Representative has a stance on specific flagship missions like Mars Sample Return, as her public record focuses on the broad Science Mission Directorate budget rather than individual mission profiles.

Expect this reaction: Expect a very receptive and supportive reaction, as she is already a proven champion for the Science Mission Directorate topline and represents a district with significant NASA research investment.

About Representative Ilhan Omar (D-MN-05)

  • Elected in 2018 as the first Somali American in the U.S. Congress and the first woman of color to represent Minnesota.
  • Served in the Minnesota House of Representatives from 2017 to 2019, where she was an assistant minority leader.
  • Earned a bachelor's degree in political science and international studies from North Dakota State University and was a policy fellow at the University of Minnesota.
  • Professional background includes roles as a community nutrition educator at the University of Minnesota and a child nutrition outreach coordinator at the Minnesota Department of Education.
  • Daughter of a colonel in the Somali Army and granddaughter of the director of Somalia's National Marine Transport.
