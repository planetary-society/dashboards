Likely Supportive (moderate confidence): Representative Guest is a fiscally conservative Appropriator who views NASA primarily through the lens of Mississippi’s economic development and local workforce needs. While he has supported broad NASA budget bills, his specific engagement with the Science Mission Directorate is currently focused on Earth science and medical research at local universities. Meeting Strategy

Lead with: Lead with Mississippi's Science ROI

Frame NASA science as a high-yield investment in Mississippi's high-tech economy and workforce. You should emphasize that the district receives an average of $10.7 million annually in NASA science obligations, supporting cutting-edge research at local institutions.

Bridge issues:

  • STEM Education and Rural Tech Development: "Your work expanding STEM access in rural districts is vital, and NASA science programs provide the real-world applications that inspire Mississippi students to pursue these high-paying careers."
  • National Defense and Local Tech Grants: "The space radiation research at the University of Mississippi Medical Center and Earth science work at Mississippi State are direct examples of how NASA science funding supports our local universities and national security interests."

Watch out for:

  • Avoid assuming his vocal defense of the Stennis Space Center equates to automatic support for science funding; he has not yet signed on to recent Science Mission Directorate support letters.
  • Prepare for skepticism regarding topline spending increases, as the Representative has a history of voting against large-scale science and technology packages due to fiscal concerns.

Expect this reaction: The Representative's staff will likely be polite and supportive of NASA's broad mission but may remain non-committal on specific Science Mission Directorate funding levels unless the local economic benefit is explicitly clear.

Key Opportunities

  • Committee Position: Leverage his influential seat on the House Committee on Appropriations to advocate for the stability of the Science Mission Directorate's budget.1 3
  • District Fiscal Impact: Highlight the $32.2M in NASA science obligations in the district, including critical space radiation research at the University of Mississippi Medical Center.5
  • Local Institution: Connect planetary science goals to the earth science and remote sensing expertise at Mississippi State University, which has received multiple NASA grants.5
  • Direct NASA Record: Draw on his strong history of defending the Stennis Space Center to build a case for supporting the broader NASA science ecosystem that relies on Mississippi's testing infrastructure.2 11 12

Key Challenges

  • Ideological Barrier: His fiscal conservatism and past opposition to large-scale science spending bills like the CHIPS and Science Act suggest he may resist Science Mission Directorate funding increases if they are not seen as fiscally restrained.1 6
  • Evidence Gap: There is a notable evidence gap regarding his views on planetary science, astrophysics, and heliophysics; he has not signed recent funding support letters for these divisions.4 13

Strategic Considerations

  • Consider mentioning his membership in the Unmanned Systems Caucus to bridge toward NASA’s use of autonomous technology in Earth science and planetary exploration.
  • You might find it useful to ask how his role on the Appropriations Committee can help ensure stable funding for the science missions that utilize the engine testing infrastructure at Stennis.
  • It could be worth mentioning the 'Tech Hub' designation for biotech in Mississippi as a reason to support NASA’s biological and physical sciences research conducted at UMMC.

About Representative Michael Guest (R-MS-03)

  • Earned a bachelor's degree in accounting from Mississippi State University and a Juris Doctor from the University of Mississippi School of Law.
  • Served as Assistant District Attorney for Madison and Rankin counties from 1994 to 2008 and as District Attorney from 2008 until his election to Congress.
  • Has represented Mississippi's 3rd congressional district in the United States House of Representatives since 2019.
  • Reported in 2021 for failing to properly disclose stock trades in energy companies BP and ExxonMobil held by a family trust.
  • Voted against a 2024 military aid package for Ukraine, despite reports that the funding would have supported defense jobs within his constituency.
