Bottom Line

Likely Supportive (high confidence): Representative Ross is a reliable ally for NASA science who views space exploration as a key driver of innovation for North Carolina's Research Triangle. As a member of the House Science Committee, she has specifically advocated for protecting the NASA workforce and has supported the Science Mission Directorate's budget through formal letters. Advocates should emphasize the strong local impact of NASA Earth science grants at NC State University and thank her for protecting the technical professionals who make these missions possible. Meeting Strategy

Lead with: The Innovation Champion of Research Triangle

Lead with the Member's commitment to federal research as a driver for North Carolina’s high-tech economy and her active leadership on the Science Committee. You should thank her for signing the Science Mission Directorate budget support letters and frame robust NASA science funding as the engine that powers the Research Triangle's innovation ecosystem and maintains a competitive edge over global rivals.

Bridge issues:

  • Clean Energy and the Environment: "NASA's Earth science missions, like the climate and flood modeling conducted right here at NC State, provide the essential data you've long advocated for to protect North Carolina’s coastline and infrastructure."
  • Aerospace Workforce and STEM: "Securing robust funding for the Science Mission Directorate is vital to fulfilling the goals of your 'Saving NASA’s Workforce Act' by ensuring we don't lose the highly-skilled technical professionals who make historic discoveries possible."

Watch out for:

  • Avoid assuming deep familiarity with planetary science or astrophysics divisions; her record is heavily focused on Earth science and workforce issues, so provide clear context when discussing non-Earth missions.
  • Be prepared for her to balance NASA science needs against Department of Energy priorities, as she holds a leadership role as Ranking Member on the Energy Subcommittee.

Expect this reaction: Expect a highly receptive and knowledgeable response. Representative Ross has established herself as a reliable ally who has explicitly pledged in committee hearings to fight for robust NASA funding.

About Representative Deborah K. Ross (D-NC-02)

  • Served as a member of the North Carolina House of Representatives from 2003 to 2013 and has represented North Carolina's 2nd congressional district since 2021.
  • Daughter of an Air Force physician and a teacher, earning a Juris Doctor from the University of North Carolina School of Law in 1990.
  • Maintained a legal practice at Smith Moore Leatherwood LLP focused on the economic development, energy, utilities, and infrastructure needs of businesses and government.
  • Advocated for state coastal protection policies based on scientific sea level rise forecasts and supported the CHIPS and Science Act of 2022 to increase U.S. semiconductor manufacturing.
  • Served as legal counsel for GoTriangle, a regional transit agency, and introduced the Pro Codes Act regarding the copyright of model codes used in government regulations.
