Meeting Strategy

Lead with: Champion the Subcommittee Ranking Member's Leadership

Lead by thanking the Representative for her vocal, bipartisan leadership in opposing the proposed 47% cut to NASA's Science Mission Directorate. You should frame your request as an appeal to her role as Ranking Member of the Space and Aeronautics Subcommittee to continue protecting the 'national asset' of the NASA workforce from budget setbacks that could cede U.S. leadership to China.

Bridge issues:

  • National Security and Global Leadership: "I want to echo your concern that cutting NASA science would be an historic setback; we must ensure robust funding for the Science Mission Directorate so the U.S. doesn't cede our leadership in space and Earth science to global competitors like China."
  • Regional Innovation and Local Grants: "NASA's science missions are driving innovation right here in the Fourth District, supporting over $17 million in research at institutions like Duke and UNC that are working on everything from the Roman Space Telescope to global water monitoring."

Watch out for:

  • Avoid treating this as an introductory meeting; as Ranking Member, she is an expert on the portfolio and already a signed supporter of $9B for NASA Science. Focus on providing her with local data points she can use to defend the budget to colleagues.
  • Be prepared for her to mention the difficulty of balancing human exploration and science; when this happens, emphasize that science payloads on missions like Artemis and CLPS are what provide the high-value 'return' on our investment in exploration.

Expect this reaction: The Representative and her staff are likely to be highly receptive and supportive, as she is a primary champion for NASA science who has already led bipartisan efforts to prevent drastic funding cuts.

About Representative Valerie P. Foushee (D-NC-04)

  • Elected to the U.S. House of Representatives in 2022, becoming the first African American and first woman to represent North Carolina's 4th congressional district.
  • Authored and passed the bipartisan Innovating Mitigation Partnerships for Asphalt and Concrete Technologies (IMPACT) Act to decarbonize and improve the efficiency of cement and concrete production.
  • Served in the North Carolina State Senate and House of Representatives, with legislative experience on committees for Appropriations on Education/Higher Education and Finance.
  • Earned a Bachelor of Arts in Political Science and African-American Studies from the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill in 2008.
  • Worked for 21 years as a secretary for the Chapel Hill Police Department and served on the Chapel Hill-Carrboro City Schools Board of Education.
