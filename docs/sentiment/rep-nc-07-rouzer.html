Meeting Strategy

Lead with: NASA Science as a Tool for North Carolina Resilience

Lead with Representative Rouzer's influential role on the Science Committee and focus on how NASA Earth science directly protects his district from coastal and weather threats. You should frame space science funding as an investment in the data and technology that local universities like UNC Wilmington use to track hurricanes and storm intensity.

Bridge issues:

  • Coastal and Weather Resilience: "NASA's Earth Science missions act as an 'oceanic rain gauge,' providing the critical data that UNC Wilmington uses to understand storm intensification and protect North Carolina's coast from future hurricanes."
  • STEM and Technical Workforce Development: "By supporting the Science Mission Directorate, you are fueling partnerships like the one between Fayetteville State University and JPL, which uses deep learning to prepare the next generation of North Carolina's technology leaders."

Watch out for:

  • Avoid overstating his historical support for specific science funding levels; while he voted for the broad NASA Reauthorization, he has not yet signed the annual letters calling for full Science Mission Directorate funding.
  • Be prepared for the office to pivot toward NOAA or weather-specific applications; you must actively bridge these back to the NASA Science Mission Directorate's role in building the satellite and research infrastructure that makes that data possible.

Expect this reaction: The staff will likely be very receptive to the district-specific research impacts, but may be non-committal regarding the $9 billion funding target for the Science Mission Directorate specifically.

Key Opportunities

  • Committee Position: Leverage his role on the Science, Space, and Technology Committee where he has praised NASA reauthorization for keeping the nation at the forefront of discovery.1 2
  • District Fiscal Impact: Highlight the $861K in NASA science obligations in his district, specifically research on global water cycles and ocean-atmosphere heat exchange at UNC Wilmington.9 4
  • Local Institution: Point to the Fayetteville State University and JPL partnership as a successful model for advancing science through deep learning and aerospace collaboration.4
  • Adjacent Interest: Discuss his recent support for H.R. 390 as an opening to talk about how NASA Science Mission Directorate research improves wildfire and emergency response capabilities.10

Key Challenges

  • Evidence Gap: There is an evidence gap regarding his specific support for planetary science, astrophysics, and heliophysics; his focus has been almost exclusively on Earth science and weather applications.11 6
  • Competing Priority: Although he supports the broader NASA budget, he has not signed onto the FY25, FY26, or FY27 letters advocating for the full $9 billion Science Mission Directorate funding level.3

Strategic Considerations

  • Consider exploring his background in agricultural economics to discuss how NASA's spatial data on sea surface salinity and precipitation impacts long-term soil health and crop risk management.
  • It could be worth mentioning his support for H.R. 390 to show how NASA Science research directly enables the advanced aerial wildfire response capabilities he recently voted to improve.
  • You might find it useful to emphasize that NASA science obligations in his district totaled over $861,000 recently, illustrating that this is a local economic driver, not just a federal expenditure.

About Representative David Rouzer (R-NC-07)

  • Earned a Bachelor of Arts in agricultural business management, agricultural economics, and chemistry from North Carolina State University.
  • Served as an associate-rural administrator for the U.S. Department of Agriculture from 2005 to 2006.
  • Held the position of assistant to the dean at the North Carolina State University College of Agriculture and Life Sciences.
  • Operated as a small business owner of The Rouzer Company and Warehouse Distribution.
  • Served as a legislative aide and Senior Policy Adviser for U.S. Senators Jesse Helms and Elizabeth Dole from 1996 to 2001.
