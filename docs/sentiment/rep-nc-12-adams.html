About Representative Alma S. Adams (D-NC-12)

  • Earned a Bachelor of Science and a Master of Science in art education from North Carolina A&T State University, followed by a Ph.D. in art education and multicultural education from Ohio State University.
  • Served as a college administrator and art professor at Bennett College for over 30 years and previously chaired the art department at the Palmer Institute.
  • Co-founded the African American Atelier in 1990 to advance awareness and appreciation for African American visual arts and cultures.
  • Chaired the North Carolina Legislative Black Caucus Foundation, which provides scholarships to students attending the state's Historically Black Colleges and Universities (HBCUs).
  • Held state-level leadership roles as chair of the North Carolina House Appropriations Committee and vice chair of the Commerce, Small Business, and Entrepreneurship Committee.
