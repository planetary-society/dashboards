Meeting Strategy

Lead with: Lead with Bipartisan Leadership and Strategic Competition

Lead by thanking Rep. Bacon for his direct leadership as Co-Chair of the Planetary Science Caucus and his recent efforts to secure $9 billion for NASA's Science Mission Directorate. Frame the current budget cuts not just as a scientific loss, but as a threat to the U.S. 'pioneering spirit' and our national security leadership in space.

Bridge issues:

  • STEM Workforce Development: "With your personal research into STEM education and your work on the 'leaky' talent pipeline in Nebraska, you know that NASA science is the primary engine for inspiring the diverse workforce we need for high-paying, high-skill jobs."

Expect this reaction: The office is likely to be highly receptive and welcoming, as Rep. Bacon is an active 'Champion of Science' and a caucus leader on these issues. Expect them to share your alarm regarding budget cuts to the Science Mission Directorate and the Mars Sample Return mission.

About Representative Don Bacon (R-NE-02)

  • Served 29 years in the United States Air Force, retiring as a brigadier general in 2014 after specializing in electronic warfare, intelligence, and reconnaissance.
  • Commanded wings at Ramstein Air Base in Germany and Offutt Air Force Base in Nebraska, and served as the Air Force's director of ISR strategy, plans, doctrine, and force development.
  • Holds master's degrees from the National War College and the University of Phoenix, and previously served as an assistant professor at Bellevue University.
  • Advocated for elevating the electromagnetic spectrum to an official domain of warfare and has been a member of the House Armed Services Committee since 2017.
  • Served as a member of the House Agriculture Committee since 2017, representing a district that includes Omaha and areas surrounding Offutt Air Force Base.
