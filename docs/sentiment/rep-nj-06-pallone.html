Meeting Strategy

Lead with: Institutional Champion and Workforce Defender

Lead by thanking the Representative for his recent signature on the FY27 $9 billion Science Mission Directorate letter and his active inquiry into protecting NASA's technical workforce. Frame NASA science funding not just as a research line item, but as a critical component of American innovation and employment that he has long defended as a 'Champion of Science.'

Bridge issues:

  • Environment and Climate Change: "NASA's Earth Science missions provide the essential satellite data needed to monitor climate impacts and manage environmental cleanup in New Jersey, directly supporting your work to protect our coastal communities."
  • STEM Education and Workforce Development: "Just as the Congressional App Challenge builds coding skills locally, robust funding for NASA's science missions ensures there are high-level career opportunities for the next generation of American innovators you are championing."

Watch out for:

  • Avoid letting the meeting drift too far into commercial satellite licensing or FCC jurisdictional issues; while these are his expertise, you must steer the conversation back to the Science Mission Directorate's research budget.
  • Do not assume the Representative is familiar with specific deep-space or planetary mission names, as his public record is almost entirely focused on Earth-centric and commercial space applications.

Expect this reaction: The office is likely to be very supportive of the broad budget ask, given his FY27 letter signature, but they may pivot the conversation toward the protection of federal employees and NASA's civil service workforce.

About Representative Frank Pallone (D-NJ-06)

  • Holds a Bachelor of Arts from Middlebury College, a Master of Arts from The Fletcher School of Law and Diplomacy at Tufts University, and a Juris Doctor from Rutgers School of Law–Camden.
  • Served as a member of the Long Branch City Council from 1982 to 1988 and the New Jersey Senate from 1984 to 1988 before his election to the U.S. House of Representatives.
  • Sponsored the American Data Privacy and Protection Act (ADPPA), the first online privacy bill to pass committee markup.
  • Opposed the Gabriella Miller Kids First Research Act, arguing it was an insufficient attempt to fund pediatric cancer research at the National Institutes of Health.
  • Introduced legislation to modify the Magnuson-Stevens Fishery Conservation and Management Act, the primary regulatory framework for U.S. fisheries.
