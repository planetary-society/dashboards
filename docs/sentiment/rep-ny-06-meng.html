Meeting Strategy

Lead with: The Champion of the Scientific Pipeline

Lead with Representative Meng's powerful role as Ranking Member of the CJS Appropriations Subcommittee and her vocal defense of the Science Mission Directorate (SMD). You should frame NASA science funding as the essential fuel for the STEM education pipeline and university partnerships she champions in Queens.

Bridge issues:

  • Education and Student Access: "By robustly funding NASA's science missions, we provide the real-world inspiration that fuels programs like the one you hosted at the New York Hall of Science to keep Queens students engaged in the STEM pipeline."
  • Economy and Local Research: "NASA's investment in our district—including nearly $400,000 in recent grants to CUNY for heliophysics and general science—shows how the Science Mission Directorate directly supports high-level academic jobs right here in New York."

Watch out for:

  • Avoid centering your pitch on commercial space partnerships or private-sector efficiency; the Representative has expressed deep concern regarding conflicts of interest between NASA and major commercial contractors.
  • While she is a staunch defender of the broad science budget, do not overstate her support for specific missions like Mars Sample Return or Hubble, as her public record focuses on topline protections rather than individual mission endorsements.

Expect this reaction: Expect a highly receptive response; the Representative is a recognized 'champion' who has publicly called proposed NASA science cuts 'shocking.' However, her staff may pivot the conversation toward demanding better agency transparency and accountability regarding spending plans.

Key Challenges

  • Evidence Gap: Her record is currently thin on support for specific missions in planetary science, astrophysics, or biological sciences, as she often focuses on broad SMD and STEM protection.9
  • Political Constraint: A strong skepticism toward major commercial partners and perceived conflicts of interest could complicate advocacy centered on public-private space science partnerships.5
  • Political Constraint: High demand for agency accountability and transparency may shift the focus of her office toward oversight rather than purely increasing budget toplines.2

  • You might find it useful to specifically mention the $300,000 heliophysics grant at the Research Foundation of CUNY, as it directly connects her subcommittee oversight to local district impact.
  • Consider exploring the intersection of Earth Science missions and her commitment to environmental protection and emission reductions.
  • Think about whether to frame consistent funding as a tool for agency accountability, arguing that budget stability prevents the wasteful mission cancellations she has recently criticized.

About Representative Grace Meng (D-NY-06)

  • Elected to the U.S. House of Representatives in 2012, becoming the first Asian American to represent New York in Congress.
  • Served in the New York State Assembly from 2009 to 2012, where she authored the Reverse Mortgage Act of 2009.
  • Holds a Bachelor of Arts from the University of Michigan and a Juris Doctor from the Benjamin N. Cardozo School of Law at Yeshiva University.
  • Attended Stuyvesant High School in New York City with an initial intent to pursue a career in teaching.
  • Stated concerns regarding national security threats posed by the Chinese Communist Party following the 2024 arrest of a former staff member.
