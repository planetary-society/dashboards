About Representative Jerrold Nadler (D-NY-12)

  • Graduated from Stuyvesant High School in 1965 and Columbia University in 1969, later earning a J.D. from Fordham University School of Law in 1978.
  • Served eight terms as a New York State Assemblyman from 1977 to 1992, where he founded and chaired the Assembly Subcommittee on Mass Transit and Rail Freight.
  • Has served as a U.S. Congressman since 1992, representing various districts in Manhattan and Brooklyn, and is currently the dean of New York's U.S. House delegation.
  • Professional experience prior to and during his early political career includes roles as a legal assistant, legislative assistant, and law clerk for various firms and the New York State Assembly.
  • Voted in 2015 to support an agreement to lift economic sanctions against Iran in exchange for the dismantling and scaling back of its nuclear program.
