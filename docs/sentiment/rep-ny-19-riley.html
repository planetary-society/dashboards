About Representative Josh Riley (D-NY-19)

  • Earned a bachelor's degree in government and economics from the College of William & Mary and a Juris Doctor from Harvard Law School.
  • Served as a policy analyst at the U.S. Department of Labor, focusing on unemployment and trade adjustment programs.
  • Worked as general counsel to Senator Al Franken on the U.S. Senate Judiciary Committee and as a Heyman Fellow on the Senate Committee on Health, Education, Labor and Pensions.
  • Practiced law as a partner at the firms Boies Schiller Flexner and Jenner & Block.
  • Introduced bipartisan legislation to update the Department of Energy’s Weatherization Assistance Program and to expand financial aid for poultry producers during avian flu outbreaks.
