Meeting Strategy

Lead with: Workforce Champion on the Science Committee

Lead with her position as Ranking Member of the Investigations and Oversight Subcommittee and her consistent support for the $9 billion Science Mission Directorate budget. Frame space science as a foundational pillar for Ohio’s high-tech workforce and its historic identity as a leader in aerospace.

Bridge issues:

  • Economy and Jobs: "With $15.3 million in NASA science funding flowing into our district over the last three years, protecting the Science Mission Directorate budget is essential for maintaining the high-tech jobs and infrastructure that support our regional tech hub."
  • STEM Diversity: "Missions like the exploration of Europa provide the perfect 'Beyond the Stars' inspiration for your 'Girls in S.T.E.A.M.' initiative, turning today's NASA science into tomorrow's technical careers for young women in Akron."

Watch out for:

  • Be prepared for the office to be focused on her current investigation into NASA facility access and potential conflicts of interest; acknowledge her oversight leadership but pivot back to the necessity of stable funding.
  • Because she is a strong advocate for airship research and aeronautics, explicitly emphasize that Science Mission Directorate funding supports different but equally vital regional research, such as planetary science at the University of Akron.

Expect this reaction: Representative Sykes is likely to be a high-level champion for your ask, as she has already signed multiple letters supporting the full $9 billion science budget and serves in a key leadership role on the Science Committee.

  • Competing Priority: Her strong focus on aeronautics and 'sustainable airships' could lead her to prioritize NASA's aeronautics accounts over Science Mission Directorate funding if budget trade-offs occur.14 6

About Representative Emilia Strong Sykes (D-OH-13)

  • Served as the minority leader of the Ohio House of Representatives from 2019 to 2021, where she oversaw a significant increase in bipartisan legislation.
  • Holds a Juris Doctor from the University of Florida Levin College of Law and a Master of Public Health from the College of Public Health and Health Professions.
  • Helped secure Akron's designation as a regional tech hub focused on sustainable plastics and rubber manufacturing, projected to create 6,300 jobs.
  • Introduced the bipartisan RAIL Act to strengthen railroad safety regulations and co-sponsored legislation to provide tax credits for domestic retreaded tire manufacturing.
  • Professional experience includes serving as a law clerk for the U.S. Bankruptcy Court in the Northern District of Georgia and as an administrative advisor in the Summit County fiscal office.
