Meeting Strategy

Lead with: NASA Science as the Engine for Oklahoma's Space Economy

Lead with her influential seat on the Appropriations Committee and her vocal support for the Artemis program. Frame NASA's Science Mission Directorate as the essential precursor for the human exploration of Mars she champions, while highlighting the $244 million software contract and local science grants that keep Oklahoma at the forefront of space technology. Given the thin direct SMD record, treat this as the best available opening rather than evidence of likely support.

Bridge issues:

  • Weather Forecasting and Disaster Mitigation: "Because you have prioritized weather forecasting to keep Oklahomans safe from tornadoes, I want to highlight how NASA Earth science missions provide the critical data that makes those life-saving predictions possible."
  • STEM Workforce and Rural Education: "NASA science grants in our district, like those at Southern Nazarene University, are exactly the kind of high-level research opportunities that keep our rural STEM workforce competitive and inspired."

Watch out for:

  • Avoid assuming that her support for Artemis or general NASA budget votes translates to automatic support for the Science Mission Directorate, as she has not yet signed on to specific SMD funding requests.
  • Do not oversell science-specific support when the public record is thin; keep the ask anchored in local value and broad NASA space science.

Expect this reaction: The office will likely be very receptive to general NASA talk and local economic impacts but may be cautious or non-committal regarding specific funding increases for science divisions without a clear return-on-investment argument.

About Representative Stephanie I. Bice (R-OK-05)

  • First American of Iranian and Pakistani descent elected to Congress, representing Oklahoma's 5th district since 2021.
  • Worked for eight years in financial oversight, business strategy, and marketing for her family's technology company in Oklahoma City.
  • Served six years in the Oklahoma Senate, including a term as Assistant Majority Floor Leader, prior to her election to the U.S. House of Representatives.
  • Holds a bachelor's degree in marketing with a minor in international business from Oklahoma State University.
  • Publicly supports government-backed research funding and secured commitments to ensure more research dollars go to the Midwest.
