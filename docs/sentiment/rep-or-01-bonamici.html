Meeting Strategy

Lead with: The Committee Champion for Science Defense

You should lead by thanking the Representative for her active leadership on the House Science Committee, specifically her recent public defense of the Science Mission Directorate (SMD) against proposed cuts. Frame your request as supporting her ongoing efforts to protect critical missions like VERITAS and the Atmospheric Observing System from being "gutted" by budget instability.

Bridge issues:

  • Wildfire Mitigation and Earth Science: "We appreciate your work on the FireSense project and the ASCEND Act; robust funding for NASA’s Earth Science Division is essential to ensure these technologies continue protecting Oregon from increasingly intense wildfire seasons."
  • Local Research and the Search for Life: "NASA science funding is directly at work in our district, such as the exobiology research at Portland State University that helps us understand the origins of life—work that is only possible with a strong Science Mission Directorate budget."

Watch out for:

  • Avoid assuming she will automatically sign the $9B 'Dear Colleague' letter; while she is a champion, she has historically opted for specific mission defenses and committee-level advocacy over that specific budget signal.
  • Be prepared for her to pivot toward Earth Science or NOAA-related issues; acknowledge those successes but gently steer the conversation back to the broader Science Mission Directorate topline to protect the full portfolio.

Expect this reaction: Expect a very warm and knowledgeable reception. As a member of the Science Committee who has already publicly rebuked NASA cuts, she and her staff will likely agree with your goals and may look for specific ways to use her committee leverage.

About Representative Suzanne Bonamici (D-OR-01)

  • Served as a consumer protection attorney for the Federal Trade Commission in Washington, D.C., and represented small businesses in private practice.
  • Introduced the Tsunami Warning, Education, and Research Act of 2014 to authorize funding for National Oceanic and Atmospheric Administration (NOAA) programs.
  • Advocated for strengthening coastline preparedness and resiliency through improved forecasting, notification systems, and supportive technologies.
  • Earned an associate degree from Lane Community College, followed by a bachelor's degree and a J.D. from the University of Oregon.
  • Served in both the Oregon House of Representatives and the Oregon State Senate prior to her election to the U.S. House of Representatives in 2012.
