Meeting Strategy

Lead with: The Workforce and Utility Advocate

Lead with her influential role on the House Science Committee and thank her for signing the FY27 budget letter for the Science Mission Directorate. You should frame NASA science as the essential engine for Oregon's technical workforce and a critical tool for district-specific needs like wildfire mitigation.

Bridge issues:

  • Wildfire Resiliency and Earth Science: "We deeply appreciate your leadership on the FireSense amendment; by robustly funding the Science Mission Directorate, you are ensuring NASA’s Earth science data continues to provide the critical intelligence needed to protect Oregon from wildfires."
  • STEM Workforce Development: "NASA’s science missions serve as the ultimate classroom for the skilled technical workforce you advocate for, creating high-tech career pathways for Oregon students through public-private partnerships."

Watch out for:

  • While she is a Science Committee member, her most vocal enthusiasm is often for the Artemis human exploration program; be ready to pivot the conversation back to how science missions provide the foundational data Artemis needs.
  • Avoid overstating her past support for deep-space planetary missions specifically, as her legislative record to date is much more concentrated on Earth science and workforce applications.

Expect this reaction: Representative Salinas is likely to be highly receptive and well-informed. Given her active committee work and her signature on the FY27 science budget letter, expect an engaged discussion that focuses on practical results and workforce impacts.

Key Opportunities

  • Committee Position: As a member of the House Science, Space, and Technology Committee and its Space and Aeronautics Subcommittee, she has direct oversight of NASA's science programs.1 8
  • Direct NASA Record: She has already demonstrated a willingness to advocate for science funding by signing the FY2027 letter calling for a $9 billion Science Mission Directorate budget.3
  • Direct NASA Record: Salinas successfully secured an amendment to the 2026 NASA Reauthorization Act specifically to enhance NASA's FireSense wildfire mitigation project.1 2
  • District Fiscal Impact: She explicitly links NASA's mission to the local Oregon economy, noting a 'robust ecosystem' of electronics and software companies in the district that contribute to the space supply chain.2 7

Key Challenges

  • Competing Priority: Her primary public focus within NASA has been the Artemis program and human exploration, including introducing a resolution for 'National Moon Landing Day.'4 8
  • Evidence Gap: While she supports Earth science and general NASA science funding, her record is thin on specific advocacy for planetary science, astrophysics, or heliophysics.1 2 3

Strategic Considerations

  • Consider mentioning local contractors like Northwest Uld, Inc. to highlight that NASA science funding translates into direct economic activity within her district's tech ecosystem.
  • You might find it useful to reference her concern for the NASA workforce, such as her interest in the Goddard investigation, to frame science funding as a matter of preserving American human capital.
  • It could be worth exploring her role as a leader in the Agriculture Committee to discuss how NASA Earth science supports forestry and rural economic development in Oregon.

About Representative Andrea Salinas (D-OR-06)

  • Served as a legislative aide to U.S. Senator Harry Reid and U.S. Representatives Pete Stark and Darlene Hooley.
  • Worked as the legislative director of the Oregon Environmental Council and later operated her own legislative consulting practice.
  • Held the position of Oregon Vice President for Strategies 360, a political consulting firm, prior to her state legislative service.
  • Registered as a federal lobbyist for the National Treasury Employees Union and later lobbied in Portland from 2015 to 2017.
  • Graduated from the University of California, Berkeley, and served as the majority whip in the Oregon House of Representatives.
