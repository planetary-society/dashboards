Meeting Strategy

Lead with: The Champion of Space as Critical Infrastructure

Lead by thanking the Representative for his leadership as Co-Chair of the NASA Space Exploration Caucus and his consistent support for the $9 billion Science Mission Directorate budget. Frame NASA science not just as discovery, but as a vital component of the national security and critical infrastructure protections he champions in the Space Infrastructure Act.

Bridge issues:

  • National Security and Infrastructure: "Because you are leading the effort to designate space systems as critical infrastructure, you understand that NASA's heliophysics and planetary science data are essential for protecting our nation's space-based assets from external threats."
  • STEM Workforce Development: "Your work on the MAKERS Act to build a STEM-capable workforce is directly supported by NASA science missions, which serve as the ultimate 'makerspaces' for inspiring and training the next generation of American engineers."
  • Bipartisanship: "As a leader of the Problem Solvers Caucus, you can help ensure NASA science remains a rare area of bipartisan unity that keeps America globally competitive during these challenging budget cycles."

Watch out for:

  • Avoid diving too deep into specific scientific divisions like astrophysics or Earth science, as his record lacks specific mentions of these; stick to the broad Science Mission Directorate (SMD) funding levels he has already signed onto.
  • Be aware that while he is a strong supporter, he does not sit on the Appropriations committee, so your ask should be for him to use his leadership as Caucus Co-Chair to influence his colleagues.

Expect this reaction: The office is likely to be highly receptive and should be treated as a 'champion' for NASA science; expect them to focus on how space science aligns with national security and workforce goals.

  • Committee Position: Leverage his leadership role as Co-Chair of the NASA Space Exploration Caucus to champion the Science Mission Directorate's funding priorities among his Republican colleagues.1 2
  • Direct NASA Record: Point to his consistent support for the Science Mission Directorate budget, having signed $9 billion funding request letters for FY25, FY26, and FY27.3
  • Adjacent Interest: Highlight how planetary exploration and heliophysics data are vital to his goal of designating space as critical national infrastructure.4 5

  • Evidence Gap: The Representative's public record lacks specific mentions of several Science Mission Directorate divisions, specifically planetary science, astrophysics, and Earth science.10
  • Political Constraint: His primary committee assignments focus on tax and intelligence, meaning he does not have a direct vote on the Science appropriations subcommittee responsible for NASA's budget.1
  • Competing Priority: A strong background in law enforcement and national security may cause him to prioritize defense-related space programs over pure scientific discovery during budget negotiations.4 5 8

About Representative Brian K. Fitzpatrick (R-PA-01)

  • Has served as a U.S. Representative for Pennsylvania since 2017, representing the state's 8th and later 1st districts.
  • Served as an FBI supervisory special agent and special assistant U.S. attorney, leading the agency's Campaign Finance and Election Crimes Enforcement program.
  • Was embedded with U.S. Special Forces in Mosul, Iraq, as part of Operation Iraqi Freedom during his service with the FBI.
  • Holds a Bachelor of Science in business administration, a Master of Business Administration, and a Juris Doctor.
  • Sponsored the INTERDICT Act, signed into law in 2018, which provided funding for U.S. Customs and Border Protection to expand technological screening for narcotics.
