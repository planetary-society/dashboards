Meeting Strategy

Lead with: The Bipartisan Security and Workforce Champion

Lead by thanking the Representative for his leadership as Co-Chair of the NASA Space Exploration Caucus and his consistent signatures on Science Mission Directorate budget letters. Frame robust NASA science funding as a vital component of national security and a primary engine for the STEM workforce pipelines he champions in Congress.

Bridge issues:

  • National Security and Infrastructure: "Since you have worked to designate space as critical infrastructure, supporting NASA's science missions is essential because they provide the technological base and strategic data we need to protect our national security assets."
  • STEM Workforce Development: "NASA's science missions are the ultimate training ground for the highly skilled workforce you are building through the MAKERS Act, inspiring students at community colleges and technical schools to pursue high-tech careers."
  • Bipartisan Problem Solving: "As a leader of the Problem Solvers Caucus, you understand that space science is a rare area of bipartisan consensus that keeps America competitive and demonstrates how government can work effectively for the public good."

Watch out for:

  • Avoid over-emphasizing the direct economic impact of NASA science grants in the district, as the current funding level is relatively small compared to other areas.
  • Be prepared for the office to focus on the overall Science Mission Directorate budget rather than specific divisions like Earth or Planetary science, as his record is strongest on the total funding level.

Expect this reaction: The office is likely to be highly receptive and welcoming, as Representative Fitzpatrick is already a proven champion who signs SMD support letters and leads the relevant caucus. About Representative Brian K. Fitzpatrick (R-PA-01)

  • Served as an FBI supervisory special agent and national supervisor for the Public Corruption Unit, leading the Campaign Finance and Election Crimes Enforcement program.
  • Embedded with U.S. Special Forces during Operation Iraqi Freedom and served in locations including Mosul, Iraq, and Kyiv, Ukraine.
  • Earned a Bachelor of Science in business administration, a Master of Business Administration, and a Juris Doctor.
  • Previously served as a special assistant United States attorney in California before being elected to the U.S. House of Representatives in 2016.
