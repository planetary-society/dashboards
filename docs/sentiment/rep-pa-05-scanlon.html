Lead by thanking Representative Scanlon for her consistent leadership in signing onto the $9 billion Science Mission Directorate funding letters and for her active membership in the Planetary Science Caucus. Bridge issues:

  • STEM Workforce Retention: "As you’ve championed through the America COMPETES Act, we need a larger and more diverse STEM workforce; NASA’s missions provide the unique inspiration required to keep students engaged and reduce attrition in these critical fields."

About Representative Mary Gay Scanlon (D-PA-05)

  • Earned a Bachelor of Arts from Colgate University and a Juris Doctor from the University of Pennsylvania Law School.
  • Served as pro bono counsel at Ballard Spahr, coordinating free legal services for low-income recipients and first responders.
  • Held the position of attorney with the Education Law Center of Philadelphia, focusing on the implementation of special education laws.
  • Appointed vice chair of the Tax Commission in 2006 and served as president of the Wallingford-Swarthmore School District board.
  • Has represented Pennsylvania in the U.S. House of Representatives since 2019, following a career in public interest law.
