Meeting Strategy

Lead with: The Technical Champion

Lead by acknowledging her background as an engineer and science teacher who understands that NASA science is the engine for the STEM workforce. You should emphasize that maintaining the $9 billion Science Mission Directorate topline is essential to keep the inspiration pipeline flowing for her students and future technical leaders.

Bridge issues:

  • STEM Workforce and Education Development: "As a former teacher and STEM Caucus leader, you know that NASA's missions provide the 'see it to be it' moments that drive students toward the technical careers you champion in the Mathematical and Statistical Modeling Education Act."
  • National Security and Workforce Readiness: "Your work on the Armed Services Committee highlights the need for a strong civilian technical workforce; NASA science acts as a vital training ground for the specialized talent our national security infrastructure relies on."
  • Local Economic Impact: "With over $500,000 in recent NASA science funding and local partners like Ansys providing critical mission software, PA-06 is directly invested in the success of the Science Mission Directorate."

Watch out for:

  • Avoid getting sidetracked by defense-specific personnel cuts she has recently flagged; keep the focus on how NASA science provides the innovation that prevents the workforce from becoming overburdened.
  • Do not assume she is intimately familiar with the specific needs of smaller NASA divisions like Heliophysics or Biological Sciences, as her public record focuses more on planetary science and general STEM initiatives.

Expect this reaction: Expect a very warm and engaged reception, as she is a self-proclaimed 'space geek' who has already demonstrated commitment by signing the $9 billion funding request letter for FY2025.

  • Direct NASA Record: Rep. Houlahan signed a bipartisan letter requesting $9 billion for the NASA Science Mission Directorate for FY2025.1 2

Key Challenges

  • Competing Priority: She has expressed concern about workforce reductions in the Space Force and Air Force, which may lead her to prioritize defense-specific labor budgets over science funding.9
  • Evidence Gap: While she supports the broad science budget, her public record is thin on specific priorities for the Heliophysics, Astrophysics, and Biological and Physical Sciences divisions.10

Strategic Considerations

  • Consider mentioning her support for the Mercury 13 Act as a bridge to discussing how NASA's current science missions continue to break barriers and inspire diverse participation in STEM.
  • You might explore how NASA’s Earth Science data supports her work with the Climate Solutions Caucus by providing the foundational research needed for resilient infrastructure and energy security.
  • Think about whether to invite her to use her platform on the Intelligence and Armed Services committees to highlight how NASA's technical superiority supports U.S. leadership in critical technologies.

About Representative Chrissy Houlahan (D-PA-06)

  • Earned a bachelor's degree in engineering from Stanford University and a master's degree in Technology and Policy from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology.
  • Served on active duty in the U.S. Air Force as a project manager for air and space defense technologies and later separated from the Air Force Reserve as a captain.
  • Held private sector leadership roles as chief operating officer for both the sportswear start-up AND1 and the non-profit start-up B-Lab.
  • Worked in education as an 11th-grade science teacher and served as president and CFO/COO of the literacy nonprofit Springboard Collaborative.
  • Stated that leadership in critical and emerging technologies, such as biotechnology, is a core component of U.S. national security.
