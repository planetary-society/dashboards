Meeting Strategy

Lead with: Lead with Local Economic Impact and a 'Space Geek' Connection

Lead by acknowledging the Representative's self-described status as a 'space geek' and her engineering background. You should then immediately pivot to the nearly $20 million in local NASA-related contracts in PA-06, specifically the $18.2 million supporting Goddard Space Flight Center, to show that NASA science is a core driver of the local economy. Given the thin direct SMD record, treat this as the best available opening rather than evidence of likely support.

Bridge issues:

  • STEM Education and Workforce Diversity: "As the Co-Chair of the Women in STEM Caucus, you've seen how NASA's missions inspire students to follow in the footsteps of Sally Ride; robust funding for the Science Mission Directorate ensures we keep that inspiration alive for the next generation of female scientists and engineers."
  • Economic Impact in PA-06: "With local companies like Bluestone Environmental Group receiving over $18 million to support the Goddard Space Flight Center, it's clear that a strong NASA Science budget directly sustains high-tech jobs and environmental leadership right here in our district."

Watch out for:

  • Avoid assuming her membership in the Planetary Science Caucus guarantees an automatic 'yes' on specific funding increases, as she lacks a clear public record of voting for or signing on to recent Science Mission Directorate budget requests.
  • Do not oversell science-specific support when the public record is thin; keep the ask anchored in local value and broad NASA space science.

Expect this reaction: The office will likely be very receptive and welcoming due to the Representative's personal passion for space, but they may be non-committal on specific funding levels given her heavy focus on competing defense and intelligence priorities.

About Representative Chrissy Houlahan (D-PA-06)

  • Earned a bachelor's degree in engineering from Stanford University and a master's degree in Technology and Policy from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology.
  • Served three years on active duty in the U.S. Air Force as a project manager working on air and space defense technologies and later served in the Air Force Reserve until 2004.
  • Held private sector leadership roles as the chief operating officer for both AND1 and the non-profit B-Lab.
  • Worked as an 11th-grade science teacher through Teach for America and served as president and CFO/COO of the early childhood literacy nonprofit Springboard Collaborative.
  • Maintains a focus on national security that prioritizes leadership in critical and emerging technologies such as biotechnology and research and development.
