Meeting Strategy

Lead with: Lead with Local Economic Value and Workforce Training

Position NASA science as a high-value investment in Northeast Pennsylvania’s economy by highlighting local manufacturing and university research. You should frame NASA Science Mission Directorate funding as a tool to support the high-tech trades and STEM initiatives that the Representative has championed early in his term.

Bridge issues:

  • Workforce Development and STEM Education: "Just as you support the Congressional App Challenge to prepare a future-ready workforce, NASA science missions provide the real-world technical challenges that inspire and train our district’s next generation of innovators."
  • Transportation and Aviation Safety: "You’ve already utilized NASA research to protect commercial pilots; supporting the Science Mission Directorate ensures the agency remains the gold standard for the technical expertise your work on the Aviation Subcommittee relies on."

Watch out for:

  • Avoid pitching NASA science as a discretionary expenditure; because the Representative is focused on balancing the budget to curb inflation, you must frame funding as a high-return investment in technical leadership.
  • Do not overstate his past support for specific science missions; while he voted for the broad NASA budget, his record on the Science Mission Directorate specifically is currently thin.

Expect this reaction: The Representative is likely to be pragmatic but cautious; expect a focus on how NASA spending impacts local jobs rather than the abstract value of exploration, with a potentially lukewarm response to funding increases that aren't tied to economic growth.

About Representative Robert P. Bresnahan (R-PA-08)

  • Began serving as the United States representative for Pennsylvania's 8th congressional district in 2025.
  • Holds a degree in business from the University of Scranton, graduating in 2012.
  • Served as CEO of Kuharchik Construction, a highway electrical business, and is the owner of RPB Ventures, a real estate development company.
  • Held leadership roles on technical and industry-related boards, including serving as treasurer of the Automated Vehicle Coalition and the TecBRIDGE board.
  • Served on the boards of the National Electrical Contractors Association and the Luzerne County Industrial Development Authority.
