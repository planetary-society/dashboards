Lead with: The Workforce and Research Engine of PA-15

Lead with the $37 million in NASA science obligations coming into the district, specifically highlighting Penn State's leading roles in astrophysics and planetary research. You should frame Science Mission Directorate (SMD) funding as a critical investment in the STEM workforce and high-tech training he champions through the Career and Technical Education Caucus. Given the thin direct SMD record, treat this as the best available opening rather than evidence of likely support.

Bridge issues:

  • Career and Technical Education (CTE): "NASA science missions in our district are providing the 'real world' training and skills-based education that the CTE Caucus advocates for, ensuring Pennsylvania remains a leader in high-tech industry preparation."
  • Agriculture and Food Policy: "As you lead the Farm Bill efforts, it is important to note that NASA’s Earth science data provides the precise crop and resource monitoring our farmers need to ensure rural economic stability and food security."

Watch out for:

  • Avoid framing NASA science as a general spending increase without specific local or workforce ties; his vote against the CHIPS and Science Act suggests skepticism toward large-scale science spending that lacks a clear industrial focus.
  • Do not oversell science-specific support when the public record is thin; keep the ask anchored in local value and broad NASA space science.

Expect this reaction: The office will likely be receptive to arguments about Penn State’s local economic impact but may be cautious or lukewarm regarding specific funding increases for science divisions given his focus on fiscal restraint. About Representative Glenn Thompson (R-PA-15)

  • Holds a bachelor's degree in therapeutic recreation from Pennsylvania State University and dual master's degrees in therapeutic recreation and health science from Temple University.
  • Worked for 28 years as a therapist, rehab services manager, and licensed nursing home administrator.
  • Served for 25 years as a member or president of the Howard Volunteer Fire Company 14, volunteering as a firefighter, emergency medical technician, and rescue technician.
