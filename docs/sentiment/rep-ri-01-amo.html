About Representative Gabe Amo (D-RI-01)

  • Serves as the U.S. representative for Rhode Island's 1st congressional district since 2023, becoming the first person of color to represent the state in Congress.
  • Held senior executive branch roles as the deputy director of the White House Office of Intergovernmental Affairs under the Biden administration and worked in the Obama administration's Office of Public Engagement and Intergovernmental Affairs.
  • Served as a principal advisor to Rhode Island Governor Gina Raimondo, focusing on outreach to local government, business, and faith communities.
  • Academic background includes a Bachelor of Arts in political science from Wheaton College and graduate study in comparative social policy at Oxford University as a Marshall Scholar.
  • Campaign platform and legislative activity have addressed issues including climate change and voting for military aid packages for international partners.
