About Representative James E. Clyburn (D-SC-06)

  • First elected in 1992, he is serving his 17th term in the U.S. House of Representatives and has held multiple senior leadership positions, including Majority Whip and Assistant Democratic Leader.
  • A graduate of South Carolina State College with a baccalaureate in history, he served as the first nonwhite advisor to a South Carolina governor and was the Commissioner of the South Carolina Human Affairs Commission from 1974 to 1992.
  • Promotes nuclear energy as a cheaper alternative to fossil fuels and pushed for a 2010 contract to convert plutonium from decommissioned weapons into nuclear fuel.
  • Proposed and supported legislation for the expansion and deepening of South Carolina's ports to accommodate larger commercial ships from China passing through the Panama Canal.
  • Advocated for the Access for All Americans Act to fund and quadruple the number of community health centers providing medical care to uninsured and low-income citizens.
