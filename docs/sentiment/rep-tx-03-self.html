Meeting Strategy

Lead with: Space Science as the Strategic Engine of American Dominance

Lead by framing NASA's Science Mission Directorate as the essential 'discovery engine' that fuels the technological innovation and American leadership you seek on the Space and Aeronautics Subcommittee. Position space science not as a discretionary expense, but as the foundational research required to out-compete the Chinese Communist Party and secure the next industrial frontier in space.

Bridge issues:

  • Geopolitical Competition with China: "Scientific discoveries in space aren't just for textbooks—they are the critical tools we need to set the rules of the road and ensure America, not the CCP, leads the next century of space development."
  • STEM Workforce Development: "NASA science funding is currently delivering nearly $5 million to our district for robotics education, directly supporting the same STEM pipeline you champion through the Congressional App Challenge."

Watch out for:

  • Avoid leading with a request for massive budget increases without first acknowledging the representative’s concerns regarding the national debt; instead, frame science missions as high-yield strategic investments.
  • Do not assume his seat on the Space Subcommittee implies automatic support for the Science Mission Directorate, as he has previously voted against broader NASA budget legislation and has no record of signing SMD-specific support letters.

Expect this reaction: Expect a skeptical or lukewarm response to funding requests unless they are explicitly tied to national security, fiscal efficiency, or local district impacts. While he is a space enthusiast, his 'nay' vote on broader NASA funding indicates he may require a very strong case for the return on investment.

About Representative Keith Self (R-TX-03)

  • Earned a Bachelor of Science degree in engineering from the United States Military Academy in 1975.
  • Served in the United States Army from 1975 to 1999, including roles in the Army Special Forces and Army Rangers.
  • Retired as a lieutenant colonel after being recalled to active duty from 2002 to 2003.
  • Served three terms as the county judge for Collin County, Texas, from 2007 to 2018.
  • Elected to the United States House of Representatives for Texas's 3rd congressional district in 2022.
