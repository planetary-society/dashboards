About Representative John R. Carter (R-TX-31)

  • Has represented Texas's 31st congressional district since 2003, a district that includes Fort Hood, home of the U.S. Army's 3rd Cavalry Regiment and 1st Cavalry Division.
  • Earned a Juris Doctor from the University of Texas School of Law in 1969 and served as the first general counsel to the Texas House of Representatives' Agriculture Committee.
  • Served as a judge for the 277th District Court of Williamson County for approximately 20 years before retiring from the bench in 2001 to run for Congress.
  • Introduced legislation to grant combatant casualty status to victims of the 2009 Fort Hood shooting and sponsored the Military Spouses Residency Relief Act.
  • Disclosed capital gains from the sale of ExxonMobil stock in 2006 and 2007 following an amendment to his financial disclosure forms in 2009.
