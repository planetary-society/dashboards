Meeting Strategy

Lead with: The Technical Engine of the Space City

Lead by connecting NASA space science to the Representative's mission of making Houston the primary hub for American space activity. You should frame science missions as the essential research and development that creates the technological spillovers he values for both the energy sector and commercial space partners like SpaceX. Given the thin direct SMD record, treat this as the best available opening rather than evidence of likely support.

Bridge issues:

  • Energy Dominance and Technology: "As the 'Energy Congressman,' you know how vital technical innovation is; NASA's planetary science missions drive the same kind of extreme-environment engineering breakthroughs that keep Houston's energy sector at the top of the global market."
  • District Economic Impact: "By supporting the Science Mission Directorate, you are investing in high-tech contracts and specialized fabrication jobs that companies like Coastal Mechanics Company in Houston rely on to support NASA missions."

  • Avoid assuming the Representative supports funding increases for Earth Science simply because of his work on local flood mitigation; his primary focus remains on energy dominance and fiscal cuts.
  • Do not oversell science-specific support when the public record is thin; keep the ask anchored in local value and broad NASA space science.

Expect this reaction: The office will likely be skeptical of requests for budget increases given their intense focus on fiscal responsibility and cutting 'wasteful' spending; expect a lukewarm reaction to any proposal that doesn't emphasize efficiency or direct Houston benefits. Expect a cautious, potentially non-committal response unless the ask is tied tightly to local priorities.

About Representative Wesley Hunt (R-TX-38)

  • Served as a U.S. Army officer and Apache helicopter pilot, deploying once to Iraq and twice to Saudi Arabia as a diplomatic liaison officer.
  • Earned a Bachelor of Science in leadership and management with mechanical engineering from the United States Military Academy at West Point.
  • Holds three advanced degrees from Cornell University: a Master of Business Administration, a Master of Public Administration, and a Master of Industrial and Labor Relations.
