About Representative Robert J. Wittman (R-VA-01)

  • Earned a Bachelor of Science in biology from Virginia Tech, a Master of Public Health from UNC-Chapel Hill, and a Ph.D. from Virginia Commonwealth University.
  • Served for 20 years with the Virginia Department of Health as an environmental health specialist and field director for the Division of Shellfish Sanitation.
  • Participated in the Corps of Cadets and Army ROTC during his undergraduate studies at Virginia Tech.
  • Authored environmental and energy legislation, including the Chesapeake Bay Accountability and Recovery Act and the Advancing Offshore Wind Production Act.
  • Represented Virginia's 1st congressional district since 2007, following service in the Virginia House of Delegates and as mayor of Montross.
