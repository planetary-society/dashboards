Meeting Strategy

Lead with: The Essential Local Economic Engine

Lead with the $286.9 million in NASA science obligations flowing directly into Virginia's Second District, emphasizing that NASA science isn't just about discovery—it's a critical driver of the local high-tech workforce. You should frame the Science Mission Directorate (SMD) budget as the essential operational fuel for Wallops Flight Facility and Langley Research Center, which she has actively worked to protect and visit.

Bridge issues:

  • Economic Resilience and Local Infrastructure: "NASA Science funding supports nearly $100 million in annual local contracts, like the aircraft maintenance and sounding rocket operations at Wallops, that keep Virginia’s aerospace economy and high-tech jobs competitive."
  • Coastal Conservation and Infrastructure: "As a champion of the BEACH Act to protect our wetlands, you know that NASA's Earth science missions provide the precise satellite data necessary to manage coastal resilience and keep our local habitats healthy."
  • STEM and Workforce Development: "By supporting NASA science, we’re also investing in programs like RockOn at Wallops, which give students hands-on STEM experience and build the next generation of Virginia’s aerospace workforce."

Watch out for:

  • Avoid assuming her support for NASA facilities automatically translates to support for planetary or astrophysics budget increases, as she has not yet signed on to any Science Mission Directorate funding letters.
  • Be prepared for her to prioritize national security and veterans' healthcare over science-specific funding during fiscal discussions, given her prominent roles on those committees.

Expect this reaction: The office is likely to be very warm toward the preservation of NASA's local footprint and infrastructure, but may be non-committal or cautious regarding specific requests for large budget increases for non-defense science.

About Representative Jennifer A. Kiggans (R-VA-02)

  • Served in the United States Navy for ten years as a helicopter pilot flying H-46 and H-3 helicopters.
  • Practiced as an adult geriatric nurse practitioner after attending nursing school at Old Dominion University and Vanderbilt University.
  • Advocated for maintaining federal funding for NASA's Wallops Flight Facility and Langley Research Center.
  • Maintains ties to the aerospace industry by supporting private sector activities at facilities involving companies such as Rocket Lab, Firefly, and Northrop Grumman.
  • Elected to the U.S. House of Representatives in 2022 and 2024 after serving in the Virginia Senate from 2020 to 2022.
