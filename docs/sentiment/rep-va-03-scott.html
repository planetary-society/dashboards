Meeting Strategy

Lead with: The Champion of Hampton Roads Science

Lead by thanking Representative Scott for his steadfast support in signing the FY25, FY26, and FY27 Science Mission Directorate budget letters. You should frame the $9 billion funding goal not just as a scientific necessity, but as a vital protection for the $350 million in NASA science funding that fuels the local economy and research institutions like Hampton University.

Bridge issues:

  • STEM Education and Workforce Development: "As Ranking Member of the Education and Workforce Committee, you've seen how programs like STEMConnect at the Virginia Air and Space Center inspire students; fully funding NASA science ensures we keep this pipeline open for the next generation of Virginia’s technical workforce."
  • Local Economic and Infrastructure Investment: "NASA science isn't just about distant planets; it's about the 600-plus jobs at NASA Langley and the $130 million contract for the AIM mission at Hampton University that sustain the Hampton Roads community."

Watch out for:

  • Avoid focusing exclusively on deep-space exploration; while he supports it, his most passionate public defenses often center on Earth science, climate change, and sea-level rise monitoring.
  • Do not oversell his specific record on Heliophysics or Astrophysics; while he signs the broad budget letters, his public statements are thinner on these specific divisions compared to Earth science.

Expect this reaction: Expect a highly receptive and knowledgeable response; the Representative is a 'champion' who has already taken concrete actions to support the SMD budget and has personally praised the revitalization of NASA Langley.

About Representative Robert C. "Bobby" Scott (D-VA-03)

  • Served in the Massachusetts Army National Guard from 1970 to 1973 and the Army Reserve from 1974 to 1976.
  • Exposed to STEM fields through his mother, a University of Michigan chemistry graduate and science educator, and his father, a pioneering surgeon.
  • Earned a B.A. in government from Harvard College and a Juris Doctor from Boston College Law School before practicing law for nearly two decades.
  • Served in the Virginia House of Delegates and the Senate of Virginia prior to his 1992 election to the U.S. House of Representatives.
  • Holds the distinction of being the first African American Representative from Virginia since Reconstruction and the first Filipino American voting member of Congress.
