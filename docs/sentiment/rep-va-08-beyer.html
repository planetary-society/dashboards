Representative Donald S. Beyer (D-VA-08)

Representative | D | VA-08

NASA Budget Support

Strong SMD budget signal

  • FY2026 CJS Appropriations vote: Yea on H.R. 6938
  • $9B NASA Science letter signatory: FY26, FY27

Bottom Line

Likely Supportive (high confidence): Rep. Beyer is a reliable ally for NASA space science, evidenced by his membership in the Planetary Science Caucus and his repeated signatures on letters calling for robust Science Mission Directorate funding. His district is a major hub for NASA science, hosting tens of millions in research grants and massive support contracts for supercomputing and engineering. Advocates should emphasize how NASA science fuels the Northern Virginia technology sector and his personal interest in machine learning, particularly as it applies to Earth observation and data analysis. While he is already supportive, reinforcing the local economic impact of these science missions will solidify his role as a champion.

Meeting Strategy

Lead with: The Tech-Forward Champion

Lead by thanking the Representative for his consistent leadership as a member of the Planetary Science Caucus and for signing the FY26 and FY27 letters supporting a $9 billion Science Mission Directorate budget. You should frame NASA science not just as exploration, but as a critical driver of the Northern Virginia technology sector and the advanced computing fields he is personally pursuing.

Bridge issues:

  • Technology and Machine Learning: "NASA’s Science Mission Directorate is a primary driver of the advanced computing and machine learning techniques you are studying, and the supercomputing support services right here in our district are essential for processing the massive data sets from missions like Earth observation and planetary exploration."
  • Environmental Conservation: "The NASA Earth science grants awarded to local institutions like the Institute for Global Environmental Strategies show how space-based data is being used directly in our community to solve environmental challenges and advance science education."

Watch out for:

  • Avoid treating this as an introductory meeting; the Representative is already a sophisticated supporter, so focus on reinforcing his leadership rather than explaining basic concepts.
  • Be careful not to overlook the importance of Earth science, as his district receives significant funding for environmental monitoring and conservation research.

Expect this reaction: Expect a very warm and knowledgeable reception; staff will likely be familiar with the $9 billion target and may want to discuss how NASA science integrates with broader technology and climate goals.

Key Opportunities

  • Direct NASA Record: Consistent support for the Science Mission Directorate, having signed the FY26 and FY27 funding letters calling for a $9 billion budget.1
  • Committee Position: Active member of the Planetary Science Caucus, signaling a specific interest in space exploration and planetary research.1
  • District Fiscal Impact: The district receives over $18 million annually in NASA science funding, supporting institutions like the Smithsonian and the Institute for Global Environmental Strategies.1

Key Challenges

  • Evidence Gap: While supportive of the overall science budget, his record is thinner on specific advocacy for the Heliophysics and Astrophysics divisions compared to Earth science.1

Strategic Considerations

  • You might find it useful to mention the Smithsonian’s research on Saturn’s moon Dione, which is funded by a NASA grant right here in the district, to highlight the local connection to deep-space exploration.
  • It could be worth asking how the Planetary Science Caucus can specifically help protect the Science Mission Directorate from the proposed budget cuts.
  • Think about whether you can connect his background in the automotive industry to NASA's work in robotics or autonomous systems for planetary rovers.

About Representative Donald S. Beyer (D-VA-08)

  • Served as the U.S. Ambassador to Switzerland and Liechtenstein from 2009 to 2013 and as the Lieutenant Governor of Virginia from 1990 to 1998.
  • Enrolled at George Mason University in 2022 to pursue a master's degree in computer science with a concentration in machine learning.
  • Co-founded the Northern Virginia Technology Council and has received the Earl Williams Leadership in Technology Award.
  • Spent over 30 years in the automotive industry as an owner of the Beyer Automotive Group and served as chairman of the American International Automobile Dealers Association.
  • Raised in a military family as the son of a U.S. Army officer and holds a degree in economics from Williams College.
