Meeting Strategy

Lead with: NASA Science as a Workforce Engine

Lead with the Representative's own record as a vocal defender of NASA's workforce and a member of the Science Committee. You should frame the Science Mission Directorate budget not just as a research goal, but as the essential fuel for the 34,000 federal and contract jobs in his district that he has pledged to protect.

Bridge issues:

  • Federal Workforce Protection: "Thank you for introducing the Saving NASA’s Workforce Act; fully funding the Science Mission Directorate at $9 billion is the best way to prevent the layoffs and program cancellations that threaten the highly skilled neighbors you have championed."
  • Local Aerospace Industry: "With major science missions like TESS and the Joint Polar Satellite System being built and managed right here in the 10th District, a strong NASA science budget is a direct investment in our local economy and technology sector."

Watch out for:

  • Avoid treating this as an introductory meeting; the Representative is already a documented champion who has signed recent SMD support letters and physically protested budget cuts at NASA headquarters.
  • Do not overlook the civil service protection angle; he often views NASA through the lens of labor rights and oversight, so ensure you connect scientific milestones to the stability of the people who achieve them.

Expect this reaction: Expect an enthusiastic and supportive reaction. As a member of the Science Committee who has already publicly advocated for 'fully funding' NASA science, he will likely look for specific ways to leverage his committee role to fight current budget pressures.

About Representative Suhas Subramanyam (D-VA-10)

  • Served as a White House technology policy advisor during the Obama administration, leading a task force on IT modernization and emerging technology regulation.
  • Operated as a business owner in the technology sector and practiced law prior to his service in the U.S. House of Representatives.
  • Introduced the LEASH DOGE Act to increase transparency and oversight of the Department of Government Efficiency, specifically regarding its impact on public services.
  • Served his community as a volunteer EMT and firefighter and was a member of the Loudoun Health Council.
  • Earned a Juris Doctor from Northwestern University and a bachelor's degree in philosophy from Tulane University.
