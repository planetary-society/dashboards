Meeting Strategy

Lead with: NASA Science as the Engine of Northern Virginia’s Innovation

Lead by framing NASA science as a cornerstone of the district's high-tech economy and a key driver of the 'space and quantum' technologies the Representative already champions. You should emphasize that the nearly $500 million in annual NASA science funding flowing into VA-11 is a direct investment in the local innovation and high-quality jobs he prioritizes.

Bridge issues:

  • Technology Modernization and Economic Development: "With major NASA science contracts for space communications and engineering services at local firms like Peraton and SAIC, space science is a $1.5 billion pillar of our district’s workforce and technical leadership."
  • Local Academic Excellence (George Mason University): "You have called George Mason University an 'engine of opportunity'; today, that engine is fueled by over $20 million in NASA grants for Earth science and astrophysics research happening right here in Fairfax County."

Watch out for:

  • Avoid letting the meeting turn into a general discussion on federal IT procurement or efficiency; steer the conversation back to the science results that these modern systems enable.
  • While he is a strong supporter, do not assume he is familiar with specific mission names like Artemis or Dragonfly, as his public record is currently focused on high-level technology and budget targets.

Expect this reaction: The Representative is likely to be highly receptive and supportive, given he has already signed the FY27 science budget letter and represents a district with one of the largest NASA science footprints in the country.

About Representative James R. Walkinshaw (D-VA-11)

  • Served as chief of staff to U.S. Representative Gerry Connolly from 2009 to 2019.
  • Elected to the Fairfax County Board of Supervisors in 2019 and 2023, representing the Braddock District.
  • Chaired the Fairfax County Board’s Environmental Committee and the National Capital Region Transportation Planning Board.
  • Earned a Bachelor of Arts in politics from New York University in 2005.
  • Won a special election in September 2025 to represent Virginia's 11th congressional district following the death of Gerry Connolly.
