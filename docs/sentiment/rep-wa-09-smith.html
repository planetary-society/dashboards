About Representative Adam Smith (D-WA-09)

  • Elected to the U.S. House of Representatives in 1996 and has been reelected 14 times, currently serving as the dean of Washington's House delegation.
  • Served in the Washington State Senate from 1991 to 1997, where he was the youngest state senator in the country at age 25.
  • Graduated from Fordham University with a Bachelor of Arts in political science and earned a Juris Doctor from the University of Washington School of Law.
  • Worked as a prosecutor for the city of Seattle and as a pro tem judge before entering the U.S. House of Representatives.
  • Co-sponsored the Smith–Mundt Modernization Act of 2012 regarding the domestic dissemination of U.S. public diplomacy information.
