About Representative Marilyn Strickland (D-WA-10)

  • Earned a Bachelor of Arts in business from the University of Washington and a Master of Business Administration from Clark Atlanta University.
  • Served as the 38th mayor of Tacoma from 2010 to 2018, where she engaged in international economic development and drew foreign investment from China and Vietnam.
  • Held private sector roles as a manager for Starbucks' online business and as the president of the Seattle Metropolitan Chamber of Commerce.
  • Contributed to the launch of Tacoma's public broadband cable service, Click!, while working with an advertisement agency.
  • Daughter of an African-American serviceman stationed at Fort Lewis and is the first member of Congress of both Korean and African-American heritage.
