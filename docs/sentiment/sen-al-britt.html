Likely Supportive (moderate confidence): Senator Britt is a 'fierce advocate' for Alabama's space industry, particularly the Marshall Space Flight Center and the Artemis program. While her public record focuses heavily on propulsion and human exploration, her state is a major hub for NASA science, receiving an average of $585 million annually in science obligations. Meeting Strategy

Lead with: Secure Alabama's Role as a Science Powerhouse

Lead by emphasizing that NASA’s $585 million annual investment in Alabama science is the engine behind the state's high-tech workforce. You should urge her to use her seat on the CJS Appropriations Subcommittee to protect the Science Mission Directorate, which sustains the research infrastructure at Alabama’s universities and centers.

Bridge issues:

  • Education & Workforce Development: "NASA science funding is a direct investment in Alabama's students; for example, the major research grants at UAH and your bipartisan work for HBCUs are exactly what sustain our state's technical leadership and research capacity."
  • Artemis and Space Infrastructure: "By supporting the Science Mission Directorate, we provide the critical planetary and lunar data needed to ensure the Artemis missions—and the Marshall-built SLS—can safely land the next generation of Alabamians on the Moon."

Watch out for:

  • Avoid assuming her support for the Space Launch System (SLS) and Marshall Space Flight Center translates to automatic support for science; she has not yet signed key letters specifically calling for a $9 billion Science Mission Directorate budget.
  • Be prepared for her to prioritize human exploration and propulsion over science if budget trade-offs are mentioned, as her recent focus has been almost exclusively on protecting Marshall's 'propulsion excellence.'

Expect this reaction: Given her role on the Appropriations Committee, the office will likely be receptive but focused on the fiscal bottom line. Expect a reaction that is supportive of NASA’s local economic impact but potentially non-committal on specific science toplines unless they are clearly tied to Marshall's mission.

Key Opportunities

  • Committee Position: As a member of the Senate Commerce, Justice, Science, and Related Agencies Appropriations Subcommittee, she has direct authority over the funding levels for all NASA Science divisions.1
  • District Fiscal Impact: Alabama receives significant NASA science funding, with obligations totaling $1.8B from FY23-FY25, including a $104M cooperative agreement at UAH for Earth science, heliophysics, and planetary science research.3 4
  • Adjacent Interest: She has demonstrated a willingness to leverage her position to secure funding for adjacent science needs, such as weather research and AI infrastructure at Alabama institutions.8 2

Key Challenges

  • Evidence Gap: While broadly supportive of NASA, her specific record on Science Mission Directorate funding is thin, as she did not sign recent Senate letters advocating for a $9 billion science budget.9
  • Evidence Gap: There is a lack of direct public evidence regarding her specific positions on the astrophysics, planetary science, or biological and physical sciences divisions.10
  • Competing Priority: Her intense focus on protecting 'propulsion excellence' and heavy-lift rockets like the Space Launch System may lead her to prioritize human exploration accounts over science during tight budget cycles.11 5 12

Strategic Considerations

  • Consider mentioning her support for the Vortex Southeast Hazardous Weather Research Program to bridge into the importance of NASA’s Earth Science missions for Alabama’s safety and economy.
  • You might find it useful to highlight the 'METTS' and 'SEAS' contracts at Marshall, which demonstrate that local engineering trades and mission systems engineering are deeply integrated with NASA science mission success.
  • Think about framing heliophysics and space weather research as a matter of 'National Security,' aligning with her established priority of protecting Alabama’s defense assets like Redstone Arsenal.

About Senator Katie Boyd Britt (R-AL)

  • Served as president and CEO of the Business Council of Alabama from 2019 to 2021, focusing on workforce and economic development.
  • Held the role of chief of staff to U.S. Senator Richard Shelby from 2016 to 2018 and previously served as his press secretary.
  • Practiced law at Butler Snow LLP, where she established the firm's government affairs branch.
  • Earned a Bachelor of Science in political science and a Juris Doctor from the University of Alabama.
  • Advocates for the expansion of broadband access and legislative reform regarding technology and social media platforms.
