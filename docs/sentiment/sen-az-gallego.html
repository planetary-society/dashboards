Bottom Line

Likely Supportive (high confidence): Senator Gallego is a strong ally for NASA science, driven by the massive economic and academic footprint of the University of Arizona and Arizona State University in his home state. He views space not just as a scientific frontier, but as a workforce engine that requires skilled machinists and manufacturing engineers, not just 'rocket scientists.' Advocates should focus on how Science Mission Directorate funding sustains high-tech jobs and world-class research facilities in Arizona, such as those managing the OSIRIS-REx and Psyche missions. While he is generally supportive, your best path is to frame NASA science as a vital component of Arizona's economic competitiveness and national security.

Meeting Strategy

Lead with: The Workforce Engine of Arizona Space Science

Lead with the $428 million in NASA science investment currently flowing into Arizona's universities and frame these missions as a vital workforce engine. You should emphasize that space science is not just for 'rocket scientists' but relies on the skilled machinists and manufacturing engineers that the Senator prioritizes for the working-class economy.

Bridge issues:

  • Working-Class Economy and Labor: "Senator, the high-tech hardware built for missions like OSIRIS-REx and Psyche at our state universities creates exactly the kind of mid-level technical jobs for machinists and manufacturers that you have championed."
  • National Security and Planetary Defense: "Arizona's leadership in the Catalina Sky Survey and the NEO Surveillance mission makes our state the front line of planetary defense, protecting our national security by tracking potential asteroid threats."

Watch out for:

  • Avoid focusing exclusively on the 'academic' curiosity of space science; the Senator has explicitly stated that elite institutions don't always turn out the volume of workers needed for the space economy.
  • Be prepared for a pivot to energy or veterans' affairs; while he signed the FY27 science budget letter, his primary committee assignments are in more traditional economic and defense sectors.

Expect this reaction: The Senator is likely to be highly supportive and receptive, as he has already directly signaled support by signing the FY27 Science Mission Directorate budget letter and represents a state with a massive NASA science footprint.

About Senator Ruben Gallego (D-AZ)

  • Served in the U.S. Marine Corps from 2002 to 2006, deploying to Iraq as a lance corporal with Lima Company, 3rd Battalion, 25th Marines.
  • Graduated from Harvard College in 2004 with a bachelor's degree in government.
  • Served in the Arizona House of Representatives from 2011 to 2014 and the U.S. House of Representatives from 2015 to 2025 before being elected to the U.S. Senate.
  • Authored a memoir in 2021 titled 'They Called Us "Lucky": The Life and Afterlife of the Iraq War's Hardest Hit Unit' regarding his military service.
  • Held professional roles as Director of Latino and New Media operations for Strategies 360 and worked for the public relations firm RIESTER.
