Meeting Strategy

Lead with: Protect the Scientific Workforce and Flagship Missions

Lead by thanking the Senator for his vocal opposition to Mars Sample Return budget cuts and his efforts to prevent layoffs at the Jet Propulsion Laboratory. You should emphasize that a robust topline for the Science Mission Directorate is the only way to prevent the "lost science" and workforce erosion he has publicly warned against.

Bridge issues:

  • Education and STEM Workforce Development: "Because you have championed the STEM pipeline through the RESEARCHER Act, you know that steady NASA science funding is vital to keeping the next generation of engineers from leaving the field due to financial instability."
  • Global Competitiveness: "Maintaining our commitment to flagship planetary missions is essential to preserving U.S. space leadership and our competitive edge over international rivals like China."

Watch out for:

  • While the Senator is a champion for planetary science and JPL, avoid assuming he has the same level of active support for smaller science divisions like Heliophysics or Astrophysics where his record is thin.
  • Do not overlook that his support is often framed through the lens of local economic impact; be prepared to connect broad science goals back to the stability of California's specialized aerospace workforce.

Expect this reaction: The Senator's staff will likely be highly receptive and knowledgeable, though they may be cautious about specific funding tradeoffs given his role on the Budget Committee.

Key Opportunities

  • Direct NASA Record: Thank the Senator for leading multiple congressional letters to the White House and NASA to restore full funding for the Mars Sample Return mission.1 2 4
  • District Fiscal Impact: Highlight the $8.6 billion in NASA science obligations in California, noting that flagship projects like JWST and Europa Clipper rely on the state's workforce.7 10
  • Committee Position: Leverage his role on the Senate Budget Committee to advocate for a stable, high topline for the Science Mission Directorate to avoid "short-sighted" funding shifts.1
  • Bipartisan Coalition: Connect planetary mission success to his "NASA Talent Exchange Program Act," framing science missions as catalysts for commercial space collaboration.5
  • Adjacent Interest: Engage his technical background as a mechanical engineer when discussing the complex hardware and software required for deep-space exploration.6

Key Challenges

  • Competing Priority: The Senator's intense focus on Mars Sample Return and the JPL workforce may lead to a focus on one program over a balanced portfolio across all science divisions.2 3
  • Evidence Gap: There is an evidence gap regarding the Senator's specific stances on Astrophysics, Heliophysics, and Biological and Physical Sciences funding.11
  • Political Constraint: He frequently frames space investment as a tool for competition with China, which may deprioritize scientific missions that do not have a clear geopolitical angle.2 3

Strategic Considerations

  • Consider mentioning NASA’s Earth Science division, as the Senator prioritizes wildfire and drought response, areas where NASA’s orbital data provides direct benefits to California.
  • You might find it useful to reference his "NASA Talent Exchange Program Act" to discuss how science missions serve as a critical training ground for the commercial space industry.
  • Think about whether you can leverage his engineering background to discuss the technical complexity of missions like Europa Clipper or the James Webb Space Telescope.

About Senator Alex Padilla (D-CA)

  • Earned a degree in mechanical engineering from the Massachusetts Institute of Technology (MIT) in 1994.
  • Worked as an engineer for Hughes Aircraft, where he wrote software for satellite systems.
  • Chaired the California State Senate Select Committee on Science, Innovation and Public Policy.
  • Served as California's 29th Secretary of State from 2015 to 2021 before being appointed to the U.S. Senate.
  • Appointed as a Commissioner to the National Security Commission on Emerging Biotechnology.
