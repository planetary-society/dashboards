Meeting Strategy

Lead with: Space Leadership through Florida Innovation

Lead with the strategic necessity of out-pacing international rivals like China in the modern-day space race. You should frame robust NASA Science Mission Directorate funding as the primary engine driving the technological innovation and workforce development that Senator Moody champions for Florida's Space Coast.

Bridge issues:

  • Space Exploration and National Security: "By supporting robust funding for NASA's space science missions, you are ensuring the United States maintains the technological edge required to remain the premier nation in space operations and stay ahead of the People's Republic of China."
  • Aerospace Innovation and Florida's Economy: "NASA science obligations bring over $400 million annually into Florida, fueling high-tech engineering at the University of Central Florida and projects like the GOLD mission that utilize our state's talented workforce."

Watch out for:

  • Avoid assuming her support for NASA infrastructure, like moving headquarters to Florida, equates to an automatic endorsement of higher science budgets; she often prioritizes administrative efficiency and taxpayer savings.
  • Do not oversell her record on science-specific funding levels; as an appointed Senator, she has not yet established a public voting record or signed onto recent Science Mission Directorate funding letters.

Expect this reaction: The office will likely react positively to the local economic and security arguments but may be non-committal or cautious regarding specific funding increases until they see how they align with the Senator's focus on fiscal efficiency.

About Senator Ashley Moody (R-FL)

  • Holds a bachelor's and master's degree in accounting from the University of Florida, a Juris Doctor from the University of Florida School of Law, and a Master of Laws in international law from Stetson University College of Law.
  • Served as an assistant U.S. attorney for the Middle District of Florida and as a circuit court judge in Hillsborough County from 2006 to 2017.
  • Served as the 38th Attorney General of Florida from 2019 to 2025, where she engaged in litigation regarding federal health care policy and state ballot initiatives.
  • Appointed to the U.S. Senate in January 2025 by Governor Ron DeSantis to fill the vacancy created by the resignation of Marco Rubio.
  • Advocated for the maintenance of the moratorium on oil and gas leasing off Florida's coasts, citing potential risks to military operations and the state's tourism industry.
