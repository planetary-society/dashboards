About Senator Rick Scott (R-FL)

  • Served 29 months in the U.S. Navy as a radarman on the USS Glover (FF-1098) and attended college on the G.I. Bill.
  • Co-founded and served as CEO of Columbia/HCA, which became the nation's largest for-profit healthcare company before his resignation amid a federal fraud investigation.
  • Served two terms as the 45th Governor of Florida (2011–2019) before being elected to the U.S. Senate in 2018.
  • Proposed a plan to encourage students to pursue majors in engineering and science by reducing tuition for those specific fields.
  • Maintained extensive business interests as a venture capitalist, with investments in healthcare, manufacturing, and technology companies.
