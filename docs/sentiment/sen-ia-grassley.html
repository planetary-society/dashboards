About Senator Chuck Grassley (R-IA)

  • Longest-serving Republican in congressional history, serving in the U.S. Senate since 1981 and previously in the U.S. House of Representatives (1975–1981) and Iowa House of Representatives (1959–1975).
  • Holds a Bachelor of Arts and a Master of Arts in political science from Iowa State Teachers College and pursued Ph.D. coursework at the University of Iowa.
  • Early career experience includes working as an assembly line worker and sheet metal shearer at Iowa factories, as well as operating an IBM machine and teaching at Charles City College.
  • Authored the federal wind energy tax credit in 1992 and has consistently supported tax incentives for alternative energy sources including ethanol, biomass, and biodiesel.
  • Extensive record of legislative oversight and investigations into federal spending, pharmaceutical industry payments to physicians, and whistleblower protections, including authoring the Whistleblower Protection Act of 1989.
