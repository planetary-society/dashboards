Senator Richard J. Durbin (D-IL)

Senator | D | IL

NASA Budget Support

  • FY2026 CJS Appropriations vote: Yea on H.R. 6938

Bottom Line

Likely Supportive (high confidence): Senator Durbin is a reliable proponent of federal research funding and views the NASA Science Mission Directorate as a critical component of American innovation. As a senior member of the Appropriations Committee and co-chair of the STEM Education Caucus, he is well-positioned to protect science budgets from proposed cuts. Advocates should emphasize the significant local impact of NASA science in Illinois, particularly the multi-million dollar astrophysics grants at the University of Chicago and the University of Illinois. While he is broadly supportive, connecting NASA science to his 'American Innovation' legislative framework is the most effective path to securing his continued advocacy.

Meeting Strategy

Lead with: The American Innovation Champion

You should frame NASA space science as a core pillar of the Senator's own 'American Innovation Act' vision. Remind him that his legislative framework specifically identifies the NASA Science Directorate as essential for maintaining U.S. global leadership and technological competitiveness.

Bridge issues:

  • Basic Scientific Research and Innovation: "Senator, your American Innovation Act correctly identifies the NASA Science Directorate as a key engine for U.S. leadership; we need your leadership on the Appropriations Committee to ensure these programs are protected from proposed cuts."
  • STEM Talent and Workforce: "With over $70 million in NASA science funding flowing to Illinois institutions like the University of Chicago and the University of Illinois, these missions are directly supporting the high-skilled STEM workforce you have fought to retain in the United States."

Watch out for:

  • Do not assume the Senator is already committed to the $9 billion Science Mission Directorate topline; while he is a champion of the agency, he did not sign the most recent 'Dear Colleague' letters for that specific amount.
  • Avoid over-emphasizing historical shuttle issues or general aerospace; focus your time on the Science Mission Directorate to align with his specific legislative record on research funding.

Expect this reaction: Expect a highly receptive meeting, as your request aligns with his long-standing legislative priorities. However, as a senior Appropriator, his staff will likely be realistic about the difficult fiscal environment and may not offer immediate commitments to specific funding levels.

Key Opportunities

  • Committee Position: Durbin serves on the Senate Committee on Appropriations, providing him with direct oversight and influence over NASA's annual funding levels.6 7
  • Direct NASA Record: He has a proven track record of introducing legislation to provide inflation-indexed, 5% annual increases specifically for the NASA Science Directorate.1 2
  • District Fiscal Impact: Illinois institutions receive substantial NASA science funding, including over $18M for astrophysical observations at the University of Chicago and $5M for galaxy evolution studies at the University of Illinois.5

Key Challenges

  • Evidence Gap: While Durbin supports the Science Directorate topline, his public record is thin on specific support for the planetary science, heliophysics, and Earth science divisions.1 2 5
  • Evidence Gap: Despite his legislative support for science funding, he did not sign the recent FY26 or FY27 'Dear Colleague' letters requesting a $9 billion budget for the Science Mission Directorate.5

Strategic Considerations

  • Consider mentioning the Bradley University drop tower as a concrete example of how he has successfully bridged local Illinois infrastructure with NASA's microgravity research needs.
  • You might find it useful to explore how NASA's quantum sensing and satellite research—which he supported in the National Quantum Initiative—depends on a robustly funded Science Mission Directorate.
  • Think about whether to highlight the $18.9 million astrophysics grant at the University of Chicago as a prime example of the 'basic scientific research' he defended against arbitrary budget moves.

About Senator Richard J. Durbin (D-IL)

  • Earned a B.S. from the School of Foreign Service at Georgetown University and a J.D. from the Georgetown University Law Center.
  • Served as an adjunct professor of medical law at the Southern Illinois University School of Medicine for five years.
  • Co-chairs the Senate Science, Technology, Engineering, and Math (STEM) Education Caucus.
  • Maintained a private law practice and gained business experience as the co-owner of a pub in Springfield, Illinois.
  • Served as legal counsel to the Illinois State Senate Judiciary Committee and to the Lieutenant Governor of Illinois.
