Meeting Strategy

Lead with: The Guardian of a Balanced NASA

Lead by thanking the Senator for his public commitment to rejecting massive cuts to NASA science, specifically his stance that prioritizing exploration at the total expense of science is a mistake. As the Chair of the CJS Appropriations Subcommittee, you should urge him to use his unique leverage to codify this 'balanced' vision in the upcoming budget to protect American leadership.

Bridge issues:

  • STEM Workforce and National Competitiveness: "NASA science grants at the University of Kansas are doing exactly what your RESEARCHER Act aims to do—providing the financial stability and high-tech training our graduate and postdoctoral researchers need to keep the U.S. ahead of global competitors."
  • Agricultural Stability and Rural Development: "Missions like SWOT, which researchers at Kansas State University are using to study water storage and river discharge, provide the critical data our farmers and ranchers need to protect the backbone of the Kansas economy."

Watch out for:

  • Avoid framing science funding as a competitor to the Artemis program; the Senator views Moon-to-Mars missions as vital for maintaining a strategic advantage over China. Always frame science as an essential partner to exploration within a 'balanced' agency.
  • Do not overstate his commitment to specific Science Mission Directorate divisions beyond Earth Science and Heliophysics, as his record is thinner there and he has not signed recent science-specific budget letters.

Expect this reaction: The Senator is likely to be very receptive but pragmatically cautious, as his staff will emphasize the difficulty of balancing these priorities in a fiscally constrained environment. Expect them to affirm his public support for science while warning that flat funding may be the only realistic victory against deeper cuts.

  • Competing Priority: The Senator must balance science funding against the massive costs of the Artemis program, which he views as vital for maintaining a strategic advantage over China.2 3
  • Evidence Gap: There is an evidence gap regarding the Senator's specific stance on the Astrophysics and Biological and Physical Sciences divisions, as his record primarily emphasizes Heliophysics and Earth science.8 10
  • Political Constraint: As an appropriator in a fiscally constrained environment, he may be forced to settle for flat funding rather than the growth needed for ambitious new planetary missions.1 2

About Senator Jerry Moran (R-KS)

  • Earned a Bachelor of Science in economics and a Juris Doctor from the University of Kansas, later serving as an adjunct professor of political science at Fort Hays State University.
  • Professional background includes experience as a banker and 15 years in private law practice, including service as a state special assistant attorney general and deputy county attorney.
  • Served seven terms in the U.S. House of Representatives and was elected to the U.S. Senate in 2010, where he has been the dean of the Kansas congressional delegation since 2021.
  • Maintains ties to the military and aerospace sectors through service on the United States Air Force Academy Board of Visitors since 2014.
  • Recognized by industry leaders for legislative work on technology and entrepreneurship, including sponsorship of the JOBS Act and the Global Leadership in Advanced Manufacturing Act.
