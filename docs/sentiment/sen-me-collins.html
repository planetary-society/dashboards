Likely Supportive (high confidence): Senator Collins is a pivotal figure for NASA science as the Chair of the Senate Appropriations Committee. She has demonstrated a strong commitment to the Science Mission Directorate by rejecting massive proposed budget cuts and protecting dozens of missions that were slated for termination. Meeting Strategy

Lead with: Champion of Scientific Competitiveness and Economic Impact

Lead by thanking the Senator for her pivotal leadership as Appropriations Chair in rejecting proposed 50% cuts to NASA science and protecting 55 missions from termination. You should emphasize that her committee actions directly safeguard Maine’s high-tech manufacturing and research jobs that are essential to missions like Dragonfly and Mars Sample Return.

Bridge issues:

  • Energy & Environment: "NASA’s Earth science missions provide the vital data that Maine institutions like the Gulf of Maine Research Institute and the University of Maine use to manage our forests and protect our coastal economy."
  • STEM & Education: "Your work to expand STEM pathways ensures that Maine students can gain the skills needed to support the future aerospace workforce and follow the inspiring example of Caribou’s own Dr. Jessica Meir."

Watch out for:

  • Avoid framing your request as a call for unrestrained spending; the Senator consistently emphasizes 'fiscally responsible' packages that restrain overall federal spending.
  • Be careful not to overstate her direct support for specific Science Mission Directorate toplines, as she has not signed recent joint letters calling for budget increases outside of the official appropriations process.

Expect this reaction: The office is likely to be supportive of NASA's broad mission but will be cautious about committing to specific funding increases given the Senator's role in balancing the entire federal budget. Expect a professional response that emphasizes the committee process over external advocacy targets.

  • Committee Position: As Chair of the Appropriations Committee, she has already acted to protect the Science Mission Directorate from severe funding cuts and the termination of 55 science missions.1 2
  • District Fiscal Impact: Maine companies are critical to planetary exploration, with Fiber Materials Inc. receiving over $9.6M for thermal protection systems used in the Dragonfly and Mars Sample Return missions.4
  • Local Institution: Local research institutions like the Gulf of Maine Research Institute and the University of Maine are deeply integrated into NASA Earth science and carbon monitoring projects.5
  • Direct NASA Record: She has a personal interest in humanizing space exploration, having authored a resolution to ensure women are fully integrated into the astronaut corps and the future moonwalking missions.9
  • Bipartisan Coalition: Her consistent voting record against rescissions and in favor of broad NASA budget bills shows a stable baseline of support for the agency.10 11

Key Challenges

  • Evidence Gap: While she funds the accounts, she has not signed recent joint letters calling for specific topline increases for the Science Mission Directorate, preferring to work through official committee channels.11
  • Political Constraint: Despite her support for science, she often emphasizes 'fiscally responsible' packages that 'restrain spending,' which may cap potential growth for the science budget.3
  • Evidence Gap: Her record lacks specific recent statements or funding actions focused specifically on the Astrophysics and Heliophysics divisions, compared to her strong engagement with Earth and Planetary science.4 5

Strategic Considerations

  • Consider mentioning the specific $9.6M in contracts for Fiber Materials Inc. in Biddeford, as their thermal protection systems are critical 'Made in Maine' components for planetary exploration.
  • You might find it useful to link NASA science to national security and U.S. competitiveness, which are high-priority areas for her as a member of the Intelligence Committee.
  • It could be worth mentioning her past leadership in ensuring women are fully integrated into the astronaut corps, using it as a hook to discuss how science missions create opportunities for diverse explorers.

About Senator Susan M. Collins (R-ME)

  • Served as a legislative assistant to U.S. Senator William Cohen and as staff director for the Oversight of Government Management Subcommittee.
  • Appointed by President George H. W. Bush as the New England regional director for the Small Business Administration in 1992.
  • Authored the Safe Treatments and Opportunities to Prevent Pain Act to encourage National Institutes of Health research into opioid therapy alternatives.
  • Criticized the Trump administration in 2025 for cutting science funding and firing federal scientists.
  • Received the Veterans of Foreign Wars 2017 Congressional Award for legislative contributions on behalf of military veterans.
