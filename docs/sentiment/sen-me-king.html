About Senator Angus S., Jr. King (I-ME)

  • Founded Northeast Energy Management, Inc. in 1989, a company focused on electrical energy conservation projects, and later co-founded Independence Wind in 2007.
  • Served as the 72nd Governor of Maine from 1995 to 2003, where he launched the nation's first initiative to provide laptops to all public middle school students.
  • Held early career roles as chief counsel to the U.S. Senate Subcommittee on Alcoholism and Narcotics and as a legislative assistant to U.S. Senator William Hathaway.
  • Developed professional expertise in the alternative energy sector, serving as vice president of a company focused on hydroelectric and biomass projects in New England.
  • Earned a Bachelor of Arts from Dartmouth College and a Juris Doctor from the University of Virginia School of Law before entering private law practice.
