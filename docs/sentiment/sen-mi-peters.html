Bottom Line

Champion (high confidence): Senator Peters is a powerful and strategic ally for NASA science, utilizing his influential seats on both the Appropriations and Commerce Committees to protect scientific research from budget cuts. His support is reinforced by Michigan's significant aerospace manufacturing sector and world-class research at the University of Michigan, which currently develops critical instruments for missions to Titan and Mars. Advocates should emphasize how NASA science drives domestic manufacturing and national security—themes Peters has consistently championed through legislation like the PROSWIFT Act.

Meeting Strategy

Lead with: The Appropriations Defender

Lead by thanking the Senator for his aggressive defense of NASA's science budget on the Appropriations Committee. You should frame your request as supporting his existing efforts to prevent 'harmful funding cuts' that would undermine both national security and Michigan's leadership in space research.

Bridge issues:

  • National Security and Infrastructure: "Your leadership on the PROSWIFT Act proved that NASA heliophysics is a national security priority; we need your help on the Appropriations Committee to ensure these space weather missions are fully funded to protect our grid and satellites."
  • Advanced Manufacturing and Research: "NASA science is a direct driver of Michigan’s economy, with the University of Michigan leading work on the Dragonfly mission to Titan and local manufacturers supporting the hardware that makes these discoveries possible."

Watch out for:

  • Be careful when discussing commercial partnerships; the Senator has expressed specific concerns about conflicts of interest and the potential for commercial ties to undermine NASA's core missions.
  • While he is a champion, do not be complacent; he is deeply concerned that ongoing staff reductions and budget instability are already undermining NASA's long-term vision for Mars.

Expect this reaction: The office is likely to be highly receptive and may already view the Senator as a primary 'champion' for this cause, given his recent signature on the FY27 science support letter and his vocal committee questioning.

Key Opportunities

  • Committee Position: As a member of the CJS Appropriations Subcommittee, Peters has direct authority over the annual funding levels for all NASA science divisions.1
  • Direct NASA Record: He signed the FY27 letter calling for a robust $9 billion budget for the Science Mission Directorate, showing a direct commitment to the agency's science goals.3
  • Direct NASA Record: Peters has a proven track record of legislative leadership in heliophysics, having authored and passed the bipartisan PROSWIFT Act to improve space weather forecasting.4
  • Local Institution: Michigan institutions receive substantial NASA science investment, including multi-million dollar contracts for the Dragonfly mass spectrometer and solar wind sensors.6 8
  • Direct NASA Record: He has publicly challenged NASA leadership nominees to ensure that budget cuts do not undermine long-term visions for future Mars exploration.2

Key Challenges

  • Evidence Gap: Evidence Gap: While highly active in heliophysics and general Earth science, his record is less specific regarding astrophysical research or biological and physical sciences.9
  • Political Constraint: He has expressed sharp concerns regarding potential conflicts of interest in commercial space partnerships, which could impact his view of certain collaborative science missions.10

Strategic Considerations

  • Consider mentioning the 'One Small Step' Act if you discuss lunar science, as the Senator took a personal interest in protecting the historical heritage of the Apollo landing sites.
  • You might find it useful to connect Earth science funding to his priority of protecting the Great Lakes, noting how NASA's orbital observations provide the data necessary to monitor local water health.
  • It could be worth highlighting his recent visit to aerospace manufacturers in Detroit to show how NASA's high-tech requirements sustain a skilled workforce in Michigan.

About Senator Gary C. Peters (D-MI)

  • Served as a lieutenant commander in the United States Navy Reserve for over 10 years, including duty in the Persian Gulf supporting Operation Southern Watch and assignments with Naval Mobile Construction Battalion 26.
  • Spent 22 years as a financial advisor and investment professional, including roles as a vice president at Paine Webber and assistant vice president at Merrill Lynch.
  • Held academic positions as the Griffin Endowed Chair in American Government at Central Michigan University and taught finance and strategic management at Wayne State and Oakland University.
  • Served as a senior policy and financial analyst for the Michigan Department of Treasury and on arbitration panels for the New York Stock Exchange and the Financial Industry Regulatory Authority.
  • Holds multiple advanced degrees, including a Master of Business Administration, a Juris Doctor, and Master of Arts degrees in Political Science and Philosophy.
