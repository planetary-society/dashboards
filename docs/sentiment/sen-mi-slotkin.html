About Senator Elissa Slotkin (D-MI)

  • Served as a Central Intelligence Agency (CIA) analyst and intelligence briefer, completing three tours in Iraq alongside the military.
  • Held multiple senior roles at the Department of Defense, including acting Assistant Secretary of Defense for International Security Affairs and Principal Deputy Under Secretary of Defense for Policy.
  • Served on the National Security Council staff as director for Iraq policy and as a senior advisor on Iraq policy at the U.S. State Department.
  • Earned a Bachelor of Arts in sociology from Cornell University's agricultural school and a Master of International Affairs from Columbia University.
  • Maintains professional experience in international security, having overseen policy related to Russia, Europe, the Middle East, and Africa.
