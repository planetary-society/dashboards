About Senator Roger F. Wicker (R-MS)

  • Served as an officer in the United States Air Force from 1976 to 1980 and as a member of the Air Force Reserves from 1980 to 2003, retiring at the rank of lieutenant colonel.
  • Earned a bachelor's degree in journalism and political science and a J.D. degree from the University of Mississippi.
  • Held legislative roles at both the state and federal levels, serving as a Mississippi State Senator, a U.S. Representative for Mississippi's 1st district, and a U.S. Senator since 2007.
  • Gained early congressional exposure as a U.S. House of Representatives page and later served as a political counselor and House Rules Committee counsel to then-Congressman Trent Lott.
  • Maintains a legislative focus on defense and infrastructure, having advocated for increased defense spending and public-private partnerships for rural economic development.
