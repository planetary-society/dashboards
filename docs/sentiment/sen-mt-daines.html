Lead with: Montana’s High-Tech Research Economy

Since the Senator’s direct record on space science funding is limited, you should lead with the significant local impact of NASA science in Montana, which received over $31 million in obligations recently. Frame NASA's Science Mission Directorate not as a spending line, but as a strategic investment in Montana State University's leadership in heliophysics and planetary research that supports the state's high-tech workforce. Given the thin direct SMD record, treat this as the best available opening rather than evidence of likely support.

Bridge issues:

Watch out for:

  • Avoid assuming the Senator is a committed supporter of science budget increases; while he has supported broad NASA bills, he has not signed recent letters specifically requesting funding for the Science Mission Directorate.
  • Do not oversell science-specific support when the public record is thin; keep the ask anchored in local value and broad NASA space science.

Expect this reaction: The office will likely be receptive to talk of Montana-based jobs and quantum technology but may be non-committal or lukewarm regarding specific funding increases due to the Senator's staunch commitment to reducing federal spending.

About Senator Steve Daines (R-MT)

  • Earned a Bachelor of Science degree in chemical engineering from Montana State University.
  • Spent 13 years at Procter & Gamble, including six years in Hong Kong and China managing operations and opening factories.
  • Served as an executive at RightNow Technologies, a cloud-based software company, where he managed customer care and international sales.
  • Introduced the North Fork Watershed Protection Act to withdraw federal lands from geothermal and mineral resource development.
  • Co-sponsored the Israel Anti-Boycott Act and legislation related to cybersecurity and infrastructure protection during his congressional tenure.
