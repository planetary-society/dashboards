Meeting Strategy

Lead with: American Dominance and Strategic Competition

Lead with the strategic necessity of NASA science to maintain U.S. technological dominance over adversaries, particularly China. You should frame Science Mission Directorate funding as a critical tool for space diplomacy and national security, ensuring the U.S. remains the leader in the global 'space silk road.'

Bridge issues:

  • Projecting American Strength: "NASA’s scientific missions are essential for space diplomacy; by leading in exploration and research, we prevent adversaries from establishing their own international standards and coalitions in orbit."
  • Local Economic Prosperity: "NASA science investments are directly supporting North Carolina jobs, such as the $6.1 million Roman Space Telescope project at Duke University and air quality monitoring research at RTI in the Research Triangle."

Watch out for:

  • Avoid assuming his support for the broader NASA budget implies automatic approval for science increases; he remains a staunch fiscal conservative focused on controlling government spending.
  • Do not oversell his historical support for the Science Mission Directorate specifically, as he has not yet signed on to recent funding letters targeted at the science topline.

Expect this reaction: The office will likely respond positively to arguments about countering China and supporting NC research, but expect a non-committal or lukewarm response regarding specific funding increases due to the Senator's commitment to fiscal discipline.

Key Opportunities

  • Committee Position: Senator Budd serves as Chairman of the Science, Manufacturing, and Competitiveness Subcommittee, giving him direct influence over NASA's scientific and technological research priorities.3
  • Local Institution: North Carolina receives significant NASA science funding, including $6.1M for Roman Space Telescope infrastructure at Duke University and $1.6M for air quality monitoring at RTI.4
  • Bipartisan Coalition: He co-sponsored the National Quantum Initiative Reauthorization Act, which explicitly authorizes funding for NASA research in quantum sensing and satellite communications.2
  • Direct NASA Record: The Senator voted to advance the nomination of the current NASA Administrator and has expressed interest in leveraging space diplomacy to counter international rivals.6 1
  • Adjacent Interest: His leadership on AI innovation and workforce development aligns with NASA's needs for advanced data analysis and high-tech STEM training.3 7

Key Challenges

  • Competing Priority: Senator Budd has a strong focus on fiscal responsibility and controlling government spending, which may lead to scrutiny of non-defense discretionary spending increases.5
  • Evidence Gap: While he supports the broader NASA budget, he has not yet signed on to specific funding letters for the Science Mission Directorate in recent sessions.8
  • Evidence Gap: There is a lack of recent public evidence regarding his specific positions on planetary science, heliophysics, or biological and physical sciences programs.9

Strategic Considerations

  • Consider mentioning how NASA’s massive science datasets provide the 'fuel' for the AI innovation and infrastructure development the Senator champions as Subcommittee Chairman.
  • It could be worth mentioning how NASA science grants align with his goals for the CHIPS Training in America Act by developing a highly skilled workforce in microelectronics and sensing.

About Senator Ted Budd (R-NC)

  • Serves as the junior U.S. Senator for North Carolina since 2023, following three terms as the U.S. Representative for North Carolina's 13th district (2017–2023).
  • Holds a Bachelor of Science in business administration from Appalachian State University and a Master of Business Administration from the Wake Forest University School of Business.
  • Introduced the Seeing Objects at Altitude Regularly (SOAR) Act, which requires the FAA to mandate tracking systems for high-altitude balloons operating at or above 10,000 feet.
  • Participated in the Task Force on Artificial Intelligence during his tenure in the U.S. House of Representatives.
  • Maintains private sector experience as a businessman and owner of a gun store in Rural Hall, North Carolina.
