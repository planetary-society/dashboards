About Senator Ben Ray Luján (D-NM)

  • Served as a member and chairman of the New Mexico Public Regulation Commission, where he implemented renewable energy standards and diversified utility sources to include solar, wind, and biomass.
  • Cosponsored the STEM Education Coordination Act to increase the production of scientists and innovators in the United States.
  • Initiated renewable energy legislation such as the SOLAR Act and co-authored the Community College Energy Training Act of 2009.
  • Introduced legislation to address PFAS/PFOA groundwater contamination around Air Force bases and developed policies for net-zero carbon emissions.
  • Holds a Bachelor of Business Administration from New Mexico Highlands University and previously attended the University of New Mexico.
