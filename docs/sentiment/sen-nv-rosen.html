Meeting Strategy

Lead with: Champion of the Scientific Workforce

Lead by thanking the Senator for her recent, forceful leadership in opposing the proposed 47 percent cut to NASA Science. Frame your ask as a way to support her commitment to protecting the STEM workforce and preventing the 'blatant damage' she warned these cuts would cause to American innovation.

Bridge issues:

  • STEM Education and Nevada’s Research Economy: "In Nevada, NASA science funding supports critical research into Mars biosignatures and astrophysics at our universities, directly fueling the STEM workforce you have championed through the Women in STEM Caucus."
  • Economic Competitiveness and Parity: "Protecting the NASA Science Mission Directorate ensures we maintain the technological parity and microgravity research capabilities necessary to compete with international rivals."

Watch out for:

  • Avoid bringing up the NASA Administrator's recent nomination or current leadership directly; the Senator's recent 'No' vote on the nominee suggests significant friction with the agency's executive direction.
  • The Senator may pivot to commercial space stations or satellite security; while these are her priorities, ensure you steer the conversation back to how Science Mission Directorate funding provides the foundational research those platforms depend on.

Expect this reaction: Expect a highly supportive and knowledgeable reaction, as the Senator is a public 'champion' for NASA science who has recently used her committee seat to challenge budget cuts. The staff will likely be receptive but may focus heavily on how cuts impact local Nevada research grants.

Key Challenges

  • Political Constraint: Her recent vote against the NASA Administrator nominee suggests a period of heightened friction with agency leadership that could complicate bipartisan funding asks.3

About Senator Jacky Rosen (D-NV)

  • Earned an associate degree in computing and information technology in 1985 and worked as a computer programmer.
  • Held professional roles at Summa Corporation and Southwest Gas, and later operated a private consulting business serving the utility and medical sectors.
  • Served in the U.S. House of Representatives from 2017 to 2019 before being elected to the U.S. Senate in 2018.
  • Holds a Bachelor of Arts in psychology from the University of Minnesota.
  • Daughter of a U.S. Army veteran who served during the Korean War.
