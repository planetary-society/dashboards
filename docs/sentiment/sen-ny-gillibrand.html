About Senator Kirsten E. Gillibrand (D-NY)

  • Graduated magna cum laude from Dartmouth College with a degree in Asian Studies and earned a Juris Doctor from the UCLA School of Law.
  • Served as Special Counsel to the Secretary of Housing and Urban Development (HUD) during the Clinton administration, working on labor and market initiatives.
  • Represented New York's 20th congressional district in the U.S. House of Representatives from 2007 to 2009 before being appointed to the U.S. Senate.
  • Authored provisions of the STOCK Act to limit insider trading by members of Congress and later introduced bipartisan legislation to ban stock ownership for members and senior executive branch officials.
  • Led legislative efforts to reform the military justice system, specifically shifting the prosecution of sexual assault and other serious crimes to independent military prosecutors.
