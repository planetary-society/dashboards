Meeting Strategy

Lead with: NASA Science as Ohio’s Innovation Engine

Lead by echoing the Senator’s own view that NASA Glenn is a hub of innovation, not just statecraft. You should frame the Science Mission Directorate as the essential fuel for the high-tech research and innovation sectors he wants to anchor permanently in Ohio.

Bridge issues:

  • Workforce and Innovation: "Senator, you have said that NASA Glenn is where the real innovation happens; supporting NASA’s Science Mission Directorate ensures that the breakthrough research powering our future in space stays rooted in Ohio’s economy."
  • University Research Leadership: "Projects like the Roman Space Telescope and the Antarctic meteorite search at Ohio State and Case Western prove that Ohio isn't just building hardware—we are leading the world's most important scientific discoveries."

Watch out for:

  • Avoid assuming he is already a dedicated advocate for the Science Mission Directorate topline; his record shows broad NASA support but his office has yet to sign science-specific funding letters.
  • Be prepared for the staff to pivot the conversation toward hypersonic weapons or lunar power; acknowledge their importance but pivot back to how science missions provide the data and discovery those technologies require.

Expect this reaction: The office will likely respond enthusiastically to arguments about Ohio's economic and innovative leadership but may remain non-committal on specific Science Mission Directorate funding levels.

Key Opportunities

  • Local Institution: Highlight Ohio's role in the Antarctic Search for Meteorites (ANSMET) and the Roman Galactic Exoplanet Survey, which are managed through Case Western and Ohio State.4
  • Direct NASA Record: Leverage Husted's public statements that NASA Glenn is a critical center for the innovation required to 'inhabit other places' and explore 'other planets.'2
  • District Fiscal Impact: Note that NASA science obligations in Ohio totaled over $178 million between 2023 and 2025, providing a significant economic baseline for the state.4
  • Adjacent Interest: Connect NASA science funding to his interest in robotics and STEM education pathways for K-12 students.1

Key Challenges

  • Evidence Gap: There is an evidence gap regarding his stance on specific Science Mission Directorate divisions, including Earth Science, Heliophysics, and Biological and Physical Sciences.7 8
  • Evidence Gap: Husted has not yet signed the annual Science Mission Directorate topline letters (FY26/FY27), suggesting he may prioritize broad NASA support over science-specific funding levels.7
  • Competing Priority: High focus on national defense and hypersonic weapons development may divert his attention or budget priorities toward military-adjacent NASA programs.6

Strategic Considerations

  • Consider exploring the connection between NASA science missions and his goal of upward mobility by emphasizing the high-paying, stable careers created by NASA contracts in the state.
  • You might find it useful to mention his focus on AI literacy in schools, framing NASA's complex science data sets as a premier training ground for Ohio's future workforce.

About Senator Jon Husted (R-OH)

  • Appointed to the U.S. Senate in 2025 after serving as the 66th Lieutenant Governor of Ohio and the 50th Secretary of State of Ohio.
  • Held leadership roles in the Ohio state legislature, including serving as Speaker of the Ohio House of Representatives and as a state senator.
  • Served as Vice President of Business and Economic Development at the Dayton-Area Chamber of Commerce prior to entering state elective office.
  • Earned both a bachelor's and a master's degree from the University of Dayton, where he was a member of the 1989 NCAA Division III National Championship football team.
  • Utilized technology to modernize state government services, including launching the Ohio Business Central online filing program and partnering with Google's Get Your Business Online initiative.
