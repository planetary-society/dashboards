Bottom Line

Likely Supportive (moderate confidence): Senator Moreno is a key ally for NASA space science due to his seat on the Senate Commerce Committee and his fierce protection of the NASA Glenn Research Center in Ohio. His primary interest lies in the Artemis program and maintaining U.S. technological superiority over China, particularly through nuclear space power and propulsion. To engage him, advocates should frame Science Mission Directorate programs as essential contributors to Ohio's high-tech economy and as critical precursors to human lunar and Mars missions. Be prepared to address his strong commitment to reducing federal spending by emphasizing the high return on investment and strategic necessity of these missions.

Meeting Strategy

Lead with: Technological Superiority and the Ohio Hub

Lead with the Senator's commitment to making NASA Glenn a 'center of excellence' for the technologies needed to beat China in space. You should frame NASA science missions as the essential technological pipeline that sustains Ohio’s specialized workforce and provides the data necessary for American lunar and deep-space leadership.

Bridge issues:

  • Geopolitical Competitiveness with China: "NASA’s space science missions are the front line of our technological competition, ensuring that the U.S. maintains the strategic edge needed to beat China to the Moon and beyond."
  • Protecting Ohio's High-Tech Economy: "By supporting the Science Mission Directorate, you are directly reinforcing NASA Glenn’s role as a national leader, which currently brings over $178 million in NASA science obligations and high-skill contracts to Ohio companies like Sierra Lobo."

Watch out for:

  • Avoid presenting scientific research as a settled end-point; the Senator explicitly views science as an 'evolving process of learning' and may push back against claims presented as 'indisputable facts.'
  • Do not oversell his support for general science funding as a given; while he is a NASA Glenn champion, he is also a fiscal hawk committed to 'massively scaling down' the federal government.

Expect this reaction: The Senator will likely be very receptive to science that enables lunar exploration and nuclear propulsion, but expect a cooler, more transactional response when discussing broad science budget increases that don't have a clear strategic or local link.

Key Opportunities

  • Local Institution: The Senator has explicitly stated his intention to 'beef up' resources and add functionality to NASA Glenn Research Center in Ohio.2
  • Committee Position: He serves on the Senate Committee on Commerce, Science, and Transportation, placing him in a direct position to influence NASA authorization and science priorities.6
  • District Fiscal Impact: Ohio received $178.7M in NASA science obligations between FY2023 and FY2025, including major contracts for local firms like Sierra Lobo and Zin Technologies.6
  • Bipartisan Coalition: He recently joined a bipartisan delegation to support Glenn Research Center's role in developing lunar nuclear reactor systems for the Artemis program.1
  • Adjacent Interest: Moreno supports the U.S. government honoring its long-term technology commitments, such as those made in the CHIPS and Science Act.7

Key Challenges

  • Ideological Barrier: The Senator is a self-described fiscal hawk who aims to 'massively scale down' the federal government to reduce the national debt.5
  • Evidence Gap: There is a significant evidence gap regarding his support for Earth Science, Astrophysics, and Heliophysics, as his focus remains heavily on exploration-related science.9 10
  • Evidence Gap: He has not yet signed on to collective letters supporting the full Science Mission Directorate budget topline for the upcoming fiscal years.9
  • Known Skepticism: Moreno has expressed a philosophy that science is an 'evolving process' rather than a set of 'indisputable' facts, which may affect his receptiveness to certain Earth Science findings.11

Strategic Considerations

  • Consider highlighting the synergy between NASA science and the Senator’s interest in nuclear energy, specifically how Glenn’s work on space fission power aligns with his goal of domestic energy dominance.
  • You might find it useful to frame space science funding as a 'commitment' the government must honor, similar to the Senator's stance on the CHIPS Act and domestic semiconductor projects.
  • Think about emphasizing the efficiency and ROI of planetary missions to appeal to his background as a business owner who values measurable results over bureaucratic growth.

About Senator Bernie Moreno (R-OH)

  • Earned a bachelor's degree in business administration from the University of Michigan.
  • Began his career at General Motors and later owned more than a dozen car dealerships, primarily in Ohio.
  • Founded Ownum, a blockchain-based technology company, and established the Sales Center for Excellence at Cleveland State University.
  • Served on the board of trustees for the MetroHealth System from 2019 to 2021.
  • Advocates for a diversified energy policy including coal, oil, natural gas, and the construction of more nuclear power plants.
