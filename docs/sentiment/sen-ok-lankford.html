Meeting Strategy

Lead with: The Efficiency Model for Oklahoma Growth

Lead with NASA as a benchmark for government efficiency, a concept the Senator has championed by using NASA programs as a model for federal property reform. You should frame Science Mission Directorate funding as a high-yield investment in Oklahoma's workforce that avoids the 'waste' he targets in other agencies. Given the thin direct SMD record, treat this as the best available opening rather than evidence of likely support.

Bridge issues:

  • National Security and GPS Resiliency: "NASA’s research into space weather and heliophysics provides the essential data needed to protect the GPS and satellite infrastructure you are working to secure through the GPS Resiliency Report Act."
  • Government Efficiency and Local Economic Growth: "By supporting NASA science, we are fueling the $173 million GEOCARB mission at the University of Oklahoma and building the exact kind of private-sector aerospace synergy you praised at the Agile Space Industries groundbreaking."

Watch out for:

  • Avoid leading with 'climate change' terminology when discussing Earth Science missions; instead, focus on technical engineering successes, data management, and weather predictability at Oklahoma universities.
  • Do not oversell science-specific support when the public record is thin; keep the ask anchored in local value and broad NASA space science.

Expect this reaction: The office is likely to be lukewarm and highly skeptical of requests for increased spending. Expect staff to ask sharp questions about how these programs avoid 'duplication' or 'waste' in line with the Senator's 'Federal Fumbles' reports. Expect a cautious, potentially non-committal response unless the ask is tied tightly to local priorities.

About Senator James Lankford (R-OK)

  • Earned a Bachelor of Science in secondary education specializing in speech and history from the University of Texas at Austin.
  • Served as an ordained Southern Baptist minister and spent over a decade as president of the Falls Creek Baptist Conference Center.
  • Has expressed skepticism regarding the scientific consensus on climate change, characterizing it as a myth and criticizing federal funding for climate reporting.
  • Introduced the Secure Elections Act in 2017 to authorize grants for updating state voting technology.
  • Advocates for expanded domestic oil and gas exploration and opposes EPA regulation of emission standards.
