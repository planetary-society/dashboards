Likely Supportive (high confidence): Senator Wyden is a consistent supporter of NASA's Science Mission Directorate, backed by significant federal investment in Oregon's research universities. His support is particularly strong for Earth science and oceanography, which provide critical data for Oregon's coastal safety and natural resource management. Meeting Strategy

Lead with: The Champion of Data-Driven Discovery

Lead by thanking the Senator for his consistent leadership in signing the $9 billion funding letters for NASA’s Science Mission Directorate. Frame NASA science as the essential engine behind Oregon’s premier research institutions, emphasizing that these federal investments are critical to maintaining the 'scientific scrutiny' he champions in all policy areas.

Bridge issues:

  • University-Led Innovation: "Senator, your support for a $9 billion NASA science budget directly empowers Oregon State and the University of Oregon to lead the nation in oceanography and disaster modeling through millions in active NASA grants."
  • Natural Resources and Coastal Safety: "NASA’s Earth science missions provide the precise data Oregon needs for forest carbon monitoring and tsunami preparedness, turning space-based observations into local safety and economic stability."

Watch out for:

  • Avoid spending significant time on human spaceflight or Artemis; the Senator’s record is almost exclusively focused on the Science Mission Directorate and its practical applications on Earth.
  • Do not assume his support for the NASA topline translates to a pass for the agency; he has a history of demanding high transparency and data integrity from federal agencies.

Expect this reaction: The office is likely to be highly receptive and well-informed, as the Senator is a proven ally who has already signed onto the $9 billion SMD request. Expect the staff to be supportive but focused on how these funds specifically benefit Oregon’s research ecosystem.

About Senator Ron Wyden (D-OR)

  • Served in the U.S. House of Representatives from 1981 to 1996 before being elected to the U.S. Senate in 1996.
  • Holds a Juris Doctor from the University of Oregon School of Law and a Bachelor of Arts in political science from Stanford University.
  • Advocates for internet freedom and privacy, having co-authored Section 230 of the Communications Decency Act and introduced the Algorithmic Accountability Act of 2019.
  • Engages in legislative oversight of federal agencies including the FAA, where he proposed amendments to its mission and requested public safety data transparency.
  • Supports federal funding for embryonic stem-cell research and has consistently voted against limiting the EPA's ability to regulate greenhouse gas emissions.
