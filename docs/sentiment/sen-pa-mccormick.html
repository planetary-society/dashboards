Bottom Line

Persuadable (moderate confidence): Senator McCormick is a newcomer who views space and science primarily through the lens of economic competition with China and domestic industrial growth. While he is a fiscal conservative who prefers the government provide only the 'building blocks' for the private sector, his state has a massive stake in NASA science, ranging from lunar lander development in Pittsburgh to world-class astrophysics research at Penn State. Advocates should frame Science Mission Directorate funding as a critical tool for maintaining U.S. technological leadership and fueling Pennsylvania's high-tech workforce. Meeting Strategy

Lead with: Science as a Strategic Building Block for Global Leadership

Lead with NASA science as the essential foundation for American technological competition with China and a catalyst for Pennsylvania’s high-tech industrial base. Frame the Science Mission Directorate as the provider of the 'building blocks'—the basic research and first-customer contracts—that allow PA-based startups and universities to lead in the global space economy.

Bridge issues:

  • Great Power Competition with China: "Ensuring robust funding for NASA's robotic science missions is critical to maintaining our technological lead over China and securing the high-skilled workforce necessary for national security."
  • Pennsylvania’s Commercial Space Economy: "NASA's science programs are the primary customers for local innovators like Astrobotic; cutting these budgets directly threatens the growth of the commercial space industry in Pittsburgh."

Watch out for:

  • Avoid framing NASA science as a purely government-led program; the Senator is skeptical of the government 'putting its finger on the scale' and prefers to see it as a facilitator for private sector success.
  • Do not overstate his past commitment to specific science funding levels; while he supports NASA broadly, he has yet to sign a Science Mission Directorate-specific funding letter.

Expect this reaction: The Senator's staff will likely be very receptive to arguments regarding local economic impact and national security, but expect a more guarded, conditional reaction regarding specific budget increases given his fiscal conservative philosophy.

About Senator David McCormick (R-PA)

  • Served as a Captain in the 82nd Airborne Division during the Gulf War, receiving a Bronze Star for his actions as executive officer of a combat engineering company.
  • Holds a Bachelor of Science in mechanical engineering from the United States Military Academy at West Point and a Ph.D. in international relations from Princeton University.
  • Extensive private sector experience as the CEO of Bridgewater Associates and the software company FreeMarkets, as well as a consultant for McKinsey & Co.
  • Held senior government roles in the George W. Bush administration, including Under Secretary of the Treasury for International Affairs and Under Secretary of Commerce for Industry and Security.
  • Served as a member of the Defense Policy Board Advisory Committee and authored a book on the downsizing of the U.S. Army at the end of the Cold War.
