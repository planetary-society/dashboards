About Senator Jack Reed (D-RI)

  • Graduated from the United States Military Academy at West Point with a Bachelor of Science in 1971 and later earned a Master of Public Policy from Harvard University.
  • Served as an active duty Army officer from 1971 to 1979, including roles as a paratrooper, platoon leader, and company commander in the 82nd Airborne Division.
  • Returned to West Point in 1978 to serve as an associate professor in the Department of Social Sciences before retiring from the Army Reserve as a major in 1991.
  • Proposed a legislative framework in 2024 to mitigate existential risks from advanced artificial general intelligence models.
  • Served three terms as a Rhode Island state senator and three terms in the U.S. House of Representatives before his election to the U.S. Senate in 1996.
