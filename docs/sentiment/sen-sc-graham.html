Meeting Strategy

Lead with: South Carolina’s High-Tech Frontier

Lead with the $15.3 million in NASA science funding currently flowing into South Carolina, particularly the cutting-edge heliophysics and astrobiology research at Clemson and Furman. You should emphasize that as a senior member of the CJS Appropriations Subcommittee, he is uniquely positioned to protect these local research pipelines from proposed funding cuts.

Bridge issues:

  • Aerospace and Advanced Manufacturing: "Senator, you have been a champion for aerospace leaders like Boeing in Charleston; the NASA science grants at Clemson serve as the R&D engine training the highly skilled technical workforce those companies need to thrive in our state."
  • National Security: "The heliophysics research being conducted at Clemson to understand space weather is directly relevant to protecting our national security satellites and the resilient infrastructure you have long advocated for."

Watch out for:

  • Avoid overstating his specific commitment to the Science Mission Directorate topline, as he has not signed recent multi-member funding letters despite his subcommittee role.
  • Be prepared for a pivot to fiscal restraint given his role as Budget Chairman; avoid framing space science as a competitor to his defense priorities.

Expect this reaction: The office will likely be receptive to local economic and workforce arguments, though the Senator’s reaction will be cautious and pragmatic due to his broader responsibility to oversee fiscal restraint as Budget Chairman.

  • Evidence Gap: There is a notable evidence gap regarding his specific positions on planetary science, astrophysics, and Earth science, as he did not sign recent SMD-specific funding letters.2 9

About Senator Lindsey Graham (R-SC)

  • Served over 33 years in the U.S. Air Force and Air Force Reserve, retiring at the rank of colonel after serving as a judge advocate and chief prosecutor in Europe.
  • Earned a Bachelor of Arts in psychology and a Juris Doctor from the University of South Carolina before entering private legal practice.
  • Held legislative office at the state and federal levels, serving in the South Carolina House of Representatives and the U.S. House of Representatives before his 2002 election to the U.S. Senate.
  • Advocates for an interventionist foreign policy and increased defense spending, frequently supporting U.S. military presence in Iraq and Afghanistan.
  • Maintains a legislative focus on national security, judicial nominations, and immigration reform, including past bipartisan efforts on climate change and border policy.
