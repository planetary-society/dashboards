Meeting Strategy

Lead with: Champion Utah’s Scientific Innovation and Economic Edge

Lead with Senator Curtis’s recent appointment to the Senate Commerce Committee and the significant $35 million in NASA science obligations currently flowing into Utah. You should frame space science funding not just as a research goal, but as a critical driver of the Utah high-tech economy, specifically highlighting the Space Dynamics Laboratory (SDL) which he recently visited. Given the thin direct SMD record, treat this as the best available opening rather than evidence of likely support.

Bridge issues:

  • Small Business and Economic Innovation: "NASA’s small business programs are exactly the kind of innovation engines your RAMP for Innovators Act aims to protect, supporting Utah companies like Care Weather Technologies in developing cutting-edge radar constellations."
  • National Security and U.S.-China Relations: "Robust funding for NASA space science is essential for maintaining our technological lead over China and ensuring that the United States remains the global leader in international space policy."

Watch out for:

  • Avoid assuming that the Senator's focus on climate stewardship through the Conservative Climate Caucus translates into automatic support for NASA Earth Science funding; he often prioritizes market-based innovation and cost efficiencies over federal programs.
  • Do not oversell science-specific support when the public record is thin; keep the ask anchored in local value and broad NASA space science.

Expect this reaction: The Senator’s staff will likely be receptive but pragmatic, focusing heavily on how science funding creates 'efficiencies' or supports Utah's aerospace workforce. Expect a lukewarm reaction to requests for major spending increases unless they are tied to national security or local economic impacts. Expect a cautious, potentially non-committal response unless the ask is tied tightly to local priorities.

  • Committee Position: Leverage the Senator's seat on the Commerce, Science, and Transportation Committee, where he recently voted to advance the nomination of the new NASA Administrator.9

About Senator John R. Curtis (R-UT)

  • Holds a degree in business management from Brigham Young University and previously served as COO of the Provo-based company Action Target.
  • Served as the 44th mayor of Provo from 2010 to 2017, where he managed the city's fiber internet network acquisition by Google Fiber and launched clean air initiatives.
  • Founded and chairs the Conservative Climate Caucus to educate Republican members on climate solutions and has sponsored legislation related to energy development permitting and forest management.
  • Authored the Fairness for High-Skilled Immigrants Act to eliminate per-country caps on employment-based visas, citing the needs of high-tech businesses in his district.
  • Served a two-year mission in Taiwan, is fluent in Mandarin Chinese, and has focused on international trade and diplomacy through roles in the House and Senate.
