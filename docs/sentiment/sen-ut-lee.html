Meeting Strategy

Lead with: Lead with Utah’s Role in the 'Renaissance in Space'

Frame NASA science as the engine behind Utah’s high-tech 'BioHive' and aerospace workforce rather than a federal spending program. You should lean into the Senator’s own call for a 'renaissance in space' by showing how Utah-led missions like the Atmospheric Waves Experiment (AWE) at Utah State University are already making that vision a reality. Given the thin direct SMD record, treat this as the best available opening rather than evidence of likely support.

Bridge issues:

  • Technology and Innovation: "NASA science investments in Utah, such as the $51.5M Atmospheric Waves Experiment at Utah State, are direct catalysts for our state's innovation hubs and high-paying STEM jobs."
  • National Leadership: "Supporting a robust Science Mission Directorate ensures America maintains its 'rightful place in the stars' and stays ahead of global competitors through superior technology."

Watch out for:

  • Avoid assuming his 2018 praise for a 'renaissance in space' translates to automatic support for current budget increases; his record on 'limited government' suggests he will be highly critical of any request for more federal spending.
  • Do not oversell science-specific support when the public record is thin; keep the ask anchored in local value and broad NASA space science.

Expect this reaction: The office will likely be proud of Utah’s specific technical contributions but lukewarm or skeptical regarding any requests for funding increases without clear evidence of fiscal efficiency or private-sector parity. Expect a cautious response centered on budget constraints. Expect a cautious, potentially non-committal response unless the ask is tied tightly to local priorities.

About Senator Mike Lee (R-UT)

  • Served as a law clerk for the U.S. District Court for the District of Utah and twice for Supreme Court Justice Samuel Alito.
  • Held legal roles as an assistant U.S. attorney for the District of Utah and general counsel to Utah Governor Jon Huntsman Jr.
  • Represented a low-level radioactive waste facility provider in legal disputes regarding the storage of foreign waste.
  • Defended the nomination of Jim Bridenstine to lead NASA and falsely claimed the agency disputed the scientific consensus on climate change.
  • Advocated for U.S. withdrawal from the United Nations and the NATO alliance, and opposed military support for the Saudi-led campaign in Yemen.
