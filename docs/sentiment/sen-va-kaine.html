About Senator Tim Kaine (D-VA)

  • Earned a Juris Doctor from Harvard Law School and practiced law for 17 years, specializing in fair housing and representing clients discriminated against on the basis of race or disability.
  • Served as an adjunct professor at the University of Richmond School of Law for six years and later taught at the Jepson School of Leadership Studies.
  • Spent nine months in Honduras helping Jesuit missionaries run a vocational center where he taught carpentry and welding.
  • Held multiple executive leadership roles in Virginia, including serving as the 70th Governor, 38th Lieutenant Governor, and Mayor of Richmond.
  • Parent of a U.S. Marine and recipient of the Shipbuilders Council of America Maritime Leadership Award in 2024.
