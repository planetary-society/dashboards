Meeting Strategy

Lead with: The Appropriator’s Shield

Lead by thanking the Senator for her powerful defense of NASA’s science budget on the Senate floor, particularly her recent rejection of drastic cuts. Emphasize that as the top Democrat on the Appropriations Committee, her leadership is the primary reason Washington's aerospace and research hubs remain at the forefront of global discovery.

Bridge issues:

  • Jobs & Economic Growth: "NASA science missions are local economic engines for Washington, like the ion propulsion technology built by Aerojet Rocketdyne in Kent that powers robotic missions to other planets."
  • Education and Workforce: "By supporting the NASA BioS-ENDURES Consortium and grants at the University of Washington, you are directly training the next generation of scientists and keeping our state’s workforce competitive."
  • Global Competitive Edge: "As you have noted in committee hearings, fully funding NASA science is a matter of national security and maintaining our leadership in a highly competitive international environment."

Watch out for:

  • Avoid assuming her support for the overall NASA budget translates into a blank check for science; she has notably not signed recent letters specifically requesting a $9 billion Science Mission Directorate budget.
  • Do not focus exclusively on the Artemis Moon landings; while she supports human exploration, ensure you pivot the conversation to the critical science missions, like the VIPER rover, that make those programs scientifically valuable.

Expect this reaction: The office is likely to be highly receptive but may focus on broad fiscal constraints given the Senator's role in managing the entire federal budget. Because she has not signed recent science-specific funding letters, expect a supportive but non-committal response regarding specific dollar increases for the Science Mission Directorate.

Key Opportunities

  • Committee Position: Leverage her leadership as Ranking Member of the Senate Appropriations Committee to influence the overall funding levels for NASA's science divisions.2
  • Direct NASA Record: Reference her recent floor remarks where she specifically championed the protection of NASA science from a proposed 50 percent budget cut.1
  • District Fiscal Impact: Highlight the $44.8M contract for ion propulsion technology in Kent, which is a critical component for robotic planetary science missions like Discovery and New Frontiers.4

About Senator Patty Murray (D-WA)

  • Holds a Bachelor of Arts degree in physical education from Washington State University.
  • Served as a preschool teacher and a parenting teacher at Shoreline Community College prior to her legislative career.
  • Began her public service as a citizen-lobbyist for educational and environmental issues and served on the Shoreline School District board of directors.
  • Daughter of a World War II veteran and Purple Heart recipient; her family's experience with her father's disability influenced her early advocacy.
  • Member of the Senate Aerospace Caucus and the Senate Oceans Caucus.
