Meeting Strategy

Lead with: Connect Local Science Leadership to National Committee Power

Your best opening is to emphasize Wisconsin's role as a powerhouse for NASA Earth science research, specifically the millions in grants flowing to the University of Wisconsin. Frame your ask around her unique ability as a member of the Appropriations Committee and the Science Subcommittee to protect these high-tech research jobs and the 'Made in Wisconsin' innovations they produce. Given the thin direct SMD record, treat this as the best available opening rather than evidence of likely support.

Bridge issues:

  • STEM Workforce and Career Technical Education (CTE): "NASA’s science missions do more than explore; they provide the real-world inspiration that drives students into the Wisconsin Space Grant Consortium and the technical careers you champion as co-chair of the CTE Caucus."
  • Manufacturing and Economic Security: "With over $50 million in NASA science funding recently coming to Wisconsin, supporting the Science Mission Directorate ensures our local manufacturers and researchers continue to lead in high-precision engineering and data processing."

Watch out for:

  • Avoid assuming she will automatically support the full Science Mission Directorate budget request; while she supports NASA broadly, she has not yet signed recent letters specifically calling for the $9 billion science topline.
  • Do not oversell science-specific support when the public record is thin; keep the ask anchored in local value and broad NASA space science.

Expect this reaction: The office will likely be highly receptive to discussions regarding local research grants and STEM education, but they may remain non-committal or cautious regarding specific funding increases for the Science Mission Directorate given the current political climate.

Key Opportunities

  • Committee Position: Leverage her influential role as Ranking Member of the Subcommittee on Science, Manufacturing, and Competitiveness to champion the economic benefits of NASA's science portfolio.10 4
  • Local Institution: Highlight the $51 million in NASA science funding flowing to Wisconsin, including major Earth science research grants for the PREFIRE and REFIRE missions at the University of Wisconsin.1 2
  • Direct NASA Record: Point to her past success in restoring funding for NASA's STEM outreach and her commitment to preventing arbitrary research grant terminations as a sign of her support for the scientific community.5 11

Key Challenges

  • Evidence Gap: The public record shows broad NASA relevance but little recent, specific evidence on Science Mission Directorate funding priorities.12 3
  • Political Constraint: Her recent vote against the NASA Administrator nominee in committee indicates a potentially skeptical or critical approach toward current agency leadership that could influence funding priorities.13
  • Competing Priority: Her intense focus on 'Buy America' policies and domestic manufacturing may prioritize hardware and infrastructure over pure scientific discovery if the two are not explicitly linked in advocacy.4

About Senator Tammy Baldwin (D-WI)

  • Holds a Juris Doctor from the University of Wisconsin Law School and previously worked as an attorney in private practice.
  • Maternal grandfather, David E. Green, and aunt, Rowena Green Matthews, were both biochemists.
  • Authored the Jason Simcakoski PROMISE Act to strengthen opioid prescribing practices and guidelines within the Department of Veterans Affairs.
  • Advocated for 'Buy America' rules in federal funding and has opposed trade agreements like the Trans-Pacific Partnership due to concerns over U.S. manufacturing jobs.
  • Introduced the Ending LGBT Health Disparities Act to promote research and cultural competency in healthcare.
