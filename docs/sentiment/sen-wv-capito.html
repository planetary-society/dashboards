Meeting Strategy

Lead with: Championing West Virginia's Scientific 'Seeds of Excellence'

Lead with Senator Capito's powerful role on the CJS Appropriations Subcommittee and her proven record of defending West Virginia's unique space science assets. You should frame Science Mission Directorate funding not as a distant federal cost, but as the essential fuel for local pillars like the Green Bank Telescope and the Katherine Johnson IV&V Facility.

Bridge issues:

  • Planetary Defense and Green Bank Telescope: "Senator, we deeply appreciate your support for the Green Bank Telescope's role in planetary defense; we need your continued leadership on the Appropriations Committee to ensure the Science Mission Directorate is funded to utilize West Virginia's world-class radar to protect our planet."
  • Rural STEM and Economic Growth: "By supporting the Science Mission Directorate, you are directly investing in the $104 million of NASA science activity in West Virginia that keeps high-tech jobs at the Katherine Johnson facility and fuels research at Fairmont State and WVU."

Watch out for:

  • Be cautious about assuming the Senator is an automatic 'yes' for the full $9 billion science topline; while she is a NASA champion, her record does not yet show her signing the specific Science Mission Directorate funding letters.
  • Avoid framing science funding as a competitor to Artemis; she sees local science assets as critical contributors to human exploration, so frame them as two sides of the same coin.

Expect this reaction: Expect a highly informed response focused on local economic impact; while she is likely supportive of science assets in West Virginia, she may be cautious about specific topline budget increases given her role as a senior appropriator.

Key Opportunities

  • Committee Position: Leverage her senior position on the CJS Appropriations Subcommittee, where she has direct influence over NASA's annual funding levels and has previously challenged science mission cancellations.1
  • Direct NASA Record: Discuss the value of the Green Bank Telescope for radio astronomy and planetary defense against Near Earth Objects, a topic she has explicitly highlighted in recent hearings.3
  • District Fiscal Impact: Highlight the $104.3 million in NASA science obligations in West Virginia since FY2023, which supports high-tech jobs and software assurance at the local IV&V facility.5
  • Adjacent Interest: Connect NASA's Science Mission Directorate programs to her goal of ensuring rural institutions in states like West Virginia can compete for federal research funding via programs like EPSCoR.3 4

Key Challenges

  • Evidence Gap: Senator Capito has supported broad NASA funding but has not yet signed the specific Science Mission Directorate letters calling for a $9 billion science budget.6
  • Evidence Gap: While she strongly supports planetary defense and lunar science, there is very limited public evidence regarding her positions on Earth Science or Heliophysics programs specifically.12
  • Competing Priority: Her intense focus on the Artemis program and human spaceflight may lead to prioritization of those accounts over purely scientific missions if overall budget caps are strictly enforced.1 2 9

Strategic Considerations

  • Consider exploring her leadership in the Women in STEM Caucus to discuss how NASA science grants are currently funding female researchers and students at Fairmont State and West Virginia University.
  • You might find it useful to mention the synergy between NASA heliophysics research and her focus on advanced nuclear energy, as both require a deep understanding of complex plasma environments.
  • Think about whether to highlight her 2018 legislation naming the IV&V facility after Katherine Johnson to underscore her long-term personal commitment to West Virginia's role in NASA history.

About Senator Shelley Moore Capito (R-WV)

  • Served in the U.S. House of Representatives from 2001 to 2015 before being elected to the U.S. Senate in 2014 as the first woman from West Virginia to hold the seat.
  • Holds a bachelor's degree in zoology from Duke University and a master's degree from the University of Virginia School of Education and Human Development.
  • Maintains a legislative focus on STEM and energy, including sponsoring the ADVANCE Act of 2023 to support nuclear development and generation IV reactor technology.
  • Advocates for embryonic stem-cell research, having voted multiple times to repeal funding restrictions and support research using donated embryos.
  • Professional background includes serving as a career counselor at West Virginia State University and as director of the educational information center for the West Virginia Board of Regents.
